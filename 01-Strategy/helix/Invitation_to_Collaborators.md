**Subject:** A draft on how to structure healthcare moonshots, and a request for your red pen

Hi [Name],

I have spent the last several months writing up what the first generation of mission-driven research organizations has actually taught us about structure, and where the gaps still are. The result is an open working paper, *The Helix Framework: Comparative Lessons from Mission-Driven Research Organizations, and Structural Desiderata for Healthcare Moonshots*, plus a shorter companion essay on the part I found hardest: the seam where a nonprofit meets a for-profit.

I am writing to you specifically because [Astera / Convergent Research / Speculative Technologies] is one of the models the paper analyzes, and your work shaped a good deal of the thinking. [Astera: your Open Science Policy is the clearest articulation of structural openness I have seen, and I lean on it. / Convergent: the FRO concept is the backbone of the comparative analysis, and the paper leans on it throughout. / Spec Tech: the Playbook for Research Leaders provides much of the comparative scaffolding, and I cite it accordingly.] I would rather you mark it up than read it politely.

The paper is deliberately a draft for community commentary, not a finished claim. The part I most want pressure-tested is the second half: how IP should be held between a 501(c)(3) and an affiliated PBC, how to keep the interactions genuinely arm's-length, and how to stay clean of the 501(c)(3) constraints (private inurement, excess-benefit, unrelated-business-income tax, fair-market valuation of pre-revenue IP). I lay out a design and then list the questions I cannot answer alone. Eight of them are in the paper; three are squarely legal and would benefit enormously from people who have already navigated this.

Two concrete asks, either of which would mean a lot:

1. Read it and send me your disagreements, in whatever form is easiest. Marginal notes are perfect.
2. If the framing resonates, I would love to explore turning this into a jointly signed commentary on how healthcare-focused research organizations should be structured in the age of AI. I think a piece co-signed across our organizations would carry far more weight than anything any one of us publishes alone.

Everything is open (CC BY 4.0), the source is on GitHub, and it will carry a Zenodo DOI before any citation. No obligation, and no rush, though I am actively making structural decisions for Cytognosis right now, so timely reactions are especially valuable.

Thank you for the work that made this possible, and for considering it.

Warmly,
