# The Helix Series: Structural Lessons for Healthcare Moonshots

A six-part essay series on how mission-driven research organizations are structured, what the first generation of metascience experiments has taught us, and what healthcare-specific structures still need to address.

These posts are the companion to our open commentary paper, *The Helix Framework*, which we are sharing with Astera Institute, Convergent Research, and Speculative Technologies for joint publication. Each post stands alone; the series reads best in order.

## The six posts

1. **[The Gap Between Where Healthcare Moonshots Start and Where They Need to End](01_The_Gap.md)** · Why healthcare requires a 15-year structural commitment and why no existing mechanism covers the full arc.
2. **[Pasteur's Quadrant and the Heilmeier Catechism](02_ARPA_Like_Models.md)** · What ARPA-like models got right, what ARPA-H, ARIA, and Wellcome Leap each tested, and where even the best ARPA structure runs out at Year 5.
3. **[Focused Research Organizations: What Convergent Research Got Right](03_Focused_Research_Organizations.md)** · The FRO concept from Marblestone et al., what makes an idea "FRO-shaped," and why FROs stop short of clinical deployment by design.
4. **[The Hybrid Experiment: Mozilla, OpenAI, Arc, and What Mission-Lock Actually Means](04_Hybrid_Organizations.md)** · Five hybrids, five different ways of pairing mission and commerce, and one consistent lesson about whose mission-lock is structural and whose is aspirational.
5. **[Openness as Operational Discipline: Astera, Arcadia, OpenBCI, and Episteme](05_Openness_As_Discipline.md)** · What happens when "we support open science" stops being a value statement and becomes a contract term.
6. **[What's Still Missing: Structural Desiderata for Healthcare Moonshots in the Age of AI](06_Desiderata_And_Helix.md)** · The twelve capabilities we believe any healthcare-moonshot organization should ship from day one, and a first sketch of the Helix Framework as one way to provide them.
