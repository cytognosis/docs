## First-draft Specific Aims (v0)

**Title:** *An adaptive, noninvasive optical sensor for synchronizing deep-tissue brain activity with behavior, anchored to a multiscale brain-state map.*

Quantifying behavior and synchronizing it with the underlying neural and physiological state — continuously, noninvasively, outside the lab — remains a core unmet need. Existing wearables capture coarse physiology; research-grade neural readout is bench-bound. We propose an **adaptive spectroscopy-based headset (fNIRS-class)** that reads brain activity at greater depth and resolution and, critically, **triangulates the wearer's state on a precomputed multiscale brain map (Cytoverse)** so that recordings are interpretable in a common coordinate system and synchronized with simultaneously captured behavior.

- **Aim 1 — Sensor.** Develop/validate the adaptive optical sensor for deep-tissue, region-targeted readout; benchmark depth, resolution, and signal types vs. standard fNIRS.
- **Aim 2 — Synchronization.** Build the pipeline that time-locks the optical readout with behavioral/physiological streams and aligns them to the Cytoverse map's coordinates (molecular→connectomic→behavioral).
- **Aim 3 — Validation.** In an initial human cohort (with a clinical partner), test whether the synchronized, map-anchored readout discriminates brain-behavior states better than behavior or physiology alone.

**Expected outcome:** an open, validated sensor + synchronization framework that turns noninvasive optical signals into interpretable, map-anchored brain-behavior coordinates — infrastructure for BBQS and for proactive brain health.
