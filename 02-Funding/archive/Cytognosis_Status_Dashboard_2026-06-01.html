How salary-splitting works (so the $255K is real)

  • Grant salary = your base × % effort; a grant buys a slice of your time, not a top-up.
  • Total effort across Purdue + all grants stays at or under 100%; the same hours cannot be billed to two funders, and effort must be certified (2 CFR 200.430).
  • The NIH/federal salary cap is $228,000/yr (FY2026) and caps only what federal awards pay, not total comp. Private funders (AWS, LTFF, Coefficient, CZI, Wellcome) do not impose it, so your near-term grants can budget toward ~$255K. The old $165K was a self-imposed board cap, raisable with a reasonable-comp basis.
  • Illustrative split: 30% Purdue + 25% AWS + 25% LTFF + 15% Coefficient + 5% unfunded = 100%, ~$190K, scaling toward $255K as lines fill.

A grants administrator + CPA must set the base-salary definition and the Purdue↔Cytognosis effort reconciliation. Not financial advice.

2 · ARPA-H: green light

Submitting IGoR does NOT block HSF. IGoR is a Proactive Health Office program (ARPA-H-SOL-26-155), not a Mission-Office ISO. The "one at a time" rule applies only to the same idea across the four Mission-Office ISOs. You can submit IGoR (Jun 25) and an HSF Mission-Office ISO in parallel, as long as the HSF concept is scoped distinctly from IGoR. Confirm the one edge case via the "Ask a Question" channel on solutions.arpa-h.gov before Jun 25. Do not submit the same idea to both HSF and PHO ISOs.
