## Second-Pass Calibration (2026-06-11): the asks were too low

**These revised dollar figures supersede any older figure elsewhere in this document.** Source for all distribution numbers: the live `funds.effectivealtruism.org/grants` database (1,631 grants, rendered in-browser on 2026-06-11), plus BlueDot program pages.

### The real EA distribution

| Fund | Grants | Median | 90th pct | Max | Apply-page range |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LTFF** | 682 | $27,000 | $99,330 | $494,510 | $1K to $500K |
| **EAIF** | 539 | $16,385 | $105,318 | $593,334 | (same portal) |

Grants above $200K are uncommon (~3% of LTFF) but real and recurring. The point: **a structured, deliverable-bearing sprint is exactly the kind of grant that lands in the top decile**, and we were sizing in the bottom third.

### LTFF: revised ask

A realistic 9 to 12-month Cytonome sprint costs **PI (~70% of $165K loaded ≈ $115K) + one edge/ML engineer (~$130K) + validation hardware ($15K to $25K) + external security audit ($20K to $40K) ≈ $280K to $310K**. Direct precedents:

| Precedent | Amount | What it funded |
|---|---|---|
| Anonymous (LTFF, 2024) | $275,000 | 1-yr stipend + compute + contracts for an AI interpretability **platform** |
| Apart / Ashgro (LTFF, 2023) | $272,800 | 6-mo multi-person salaries + ops, safety talent incubation |
| BERI (LTFF, 2021) | $250,000 | 12-mo dev salary building an **open-source ML-safety library** |
| Jessica Rumbelow (LTFF, 2023) | $195,000 | Seed year for a new AI-safety research org |

**Recommended LTFF: mainline $250K, stretch $320K, floor $130K.** Floor $130K = PI only (already top ~15% of LTFF grants). Mainline $250K = PI + engineer + hardware (multiple exact precedents). Stretch $320K = add the audit and a 3-month buffer. Our prior $125K was the floor, mislabeled as mainline.

### EAIF: revised ask (if routed there)

EAIF funds digital infrastructure (Lightcone $250K) and career-training orgs (Training for Good $593K). If LTFF routes us there, ask **$200K to $250K**, framed as **safety-community epistemic infrastructure** (the open primitives as a shared public good).

### Career Transition: revised ask

CDTF's own named grants skew small ($25K to $52.5K) and short (3 to 6 months), but the program states **no maximum** and explicitly funds a **one-year ML-professor sabbatical**. The right anchor for a 12-month senior Bay-Area transition is the comparable **full-time programs**: Constellation Astra pays **$8,400/mo (~$100,800/yr)** and MATS **~$7,680/mo (~$92K/yr)**. LTFF has also funded explicit career-transition upskilling at **$110K**.

**Recommended Career Transition: ~$100K/12 mo** (lead), $120K defensible ceiling, $75K conservative floor. The bigger risk is not the number; it is the **sitting-CEO / full-time** issue (next).

### The sitting-CEO problem and how to route around it

BlueDot's career-transition grant carries a hard clause: **"This is not something to do alongside a full or part-time role."** Coefficient's own CDTF language is softer (it asks you to disclose other income and pro-rates, rather than disqualifying). Since Coefficient makes the final decision and is only **temporarily** co-evaluating with BlueDot (window opened May 2026), **apply directly to Coefficient CDTF**, and frame yourself as the individual in a defined transition (deepening technical AI-safety capability, community integration), with Cytognosis as the vehicle the new skills serve, and a brief note on stepping back from day-to-day operations. Do not lead with "CEO."

### Topic framing that actually gets funded

From funded-grant descriptions, the frames that win (in order): (1) technical AI-safety research; (2) **open-source safety tooling and digital infrastructure for the safety ecosystem** (BERI $250K, Lightcone $250K, Alignment Ecosystem Development $99K); (3) new-org seed ($195K to $494K); (4) senior independent researcher ($120K to $220K); (5) policy-plus-technical hybrid.

**Our strongest frame:** "open-source, privacy-preserving AI-safety infrastructure" with an alignment overlay (manipulation-defense and zero-custody as **deployment-time auditability and oversight**). There is **no prior LTFF or EAIF grant mentioning post-quantum crypto or W3C Solid**, so this is a clean novelty claim, not a crowded gap. **Avoid the "mental-health app / digital-therapeutic" frame**: LTFF has no mental-health grant above ~$30K, and EAIF's one recent mental-health grant ($80K, Overcome) was an RCT intervention. Mental health is our first deployment domain, not the thesis.

## Part 2. Coefficient Career Development and Transition Funding (dedicated)

### 2A. What it funds

Individual career-capital building: graduate study, internships, independent study, certifications, and **career transition and exploration periods**, for people aiming to reduce global catastrophic risks. Their own examples include a **senior ML engineer's six-month exploration** and a **professor's one-year sabbatical into technical AI safety**, your two closest templates. You are evaluated as an individual; no org financials requested.

### 2B. Amount (revised)

**Ask ~$100K for 12 months** (lead), $120K ceiling, $75K floor. Frame as a senior full-time transition, budgeted against living costs (Bay Area), with a small tools and travel line. Anchor to the comparable full-time programs (Astra ~$100,800/yr, MATS ~$92K/yr) and the no-cap, one-year-sabbatical precedent. Declare any Cytognosis compensation; Coefficient pro-rates rather than disqualifies.

### 2C. Eligibility, the full-time issue, and routing

A sitting Founder/CEO is a gray area. BlueDot's parallel grant requires full-time commitment ("not alongside a full or part-time role"); Coefficient's own language is softer. Because Coefficient makes the final decision and is only temporarily co-evaluating with BlueDot, **apply directly to Coefficient CDTF**. Write as the individual in a defined transition and upskilling period, note stepping back from day-to-day operations, and avoid "CEO seeking runway." Avoid the documented failure pattern: a mid-career researcher leaning on institutional-leverage arguments with an unclear direct AI-safety contribution.

### 2D. How to write it

They weigh **career capital toward GCR**, a **detailed theory of change for the career**, and **counterfactual necessity**. Materials: 250-word proposal, 250-word personal statement, half-page budget, 2-page CV. Keep the TBX1 lived-experience statement; tie it to the direct safety contribution. Decision ~6 weeks; expedite on request.

## Part 3. EA Funds: LTFF, EAIF, GHDF

### 3A. Long-Term Future Fund (LTFF): primary

**Open, rolling with quarterly batches.** Funds individuals and orgs including **salaries** at ~70% of industry rate, with a +20% to +40% self-employment gross-up. Decisions ~3 to 6 weeks; fast-track available. Distribution: 682 grants, median $27K, 90th percentile $99K, max $494,510.

**Revised ask: mainline $250K, stretch $320K, floor $130K** (see Second-Pass Calibration for precedents). Frame as open-source privacy-preserving AI-safety infrastructure; the senior-neuroscientist-into-AI-safety transition is a strength. The PI line at ~70% of $165K (~$115K) is well within norms.

### 3B. EA Infrastructure Fund (EAIF): secondary (free transfer)

**Open, rolling.** Pivoting to principles-first EA (epistemic infrastructure, field-building). Max $593K; 90th percentile ~$105K. Check the EAIF transfer box on the LTFF form. EAIF angle: **cognitive liberty as epistemic security**, an open tool that protects independent human reasoning from AI-mediated manipulation. If routed here, ask $200K to $250K.

### 3C. Global Health and Development Fund (GHDF): drop it

**Closed to applications.** The apply page says verbatim: "This Fund currently isn't accepting applications. Consider applying to GiveWell." Even if open, it uses GiveWell cost-per-DALY bars a privacy-preserving edge tool for high-income early adopters would not clear. The global-disability-burden scale argument still works at LTFF as the tractability case for the first deployment, and at dedicated mental-health funders later (Wellcome, Baszucki, atai).

### 3D. Routing

**Primary LTFF, secondary EAIF (transfer box). Do not apply to GHDF.**

## Part 4. The translation: writing our work in AI-safety and cognitive-liberty language

> **Framing refinement (2026-06-11; see companion memo `Framing_Verifiability_vs_CognitiveLiberty_2026-06-11.md`):** lead the **technical** claim with **verifiability and deployment control** (CAP/Cytoplex is the crown-jewel, already-built, fundable artifact), and use **cognitive-liberty as the values motivation**. Do not label Cytoverse "interpretability" (reviewers mean mechanistic interpretability of frontier LLMs). New federal matches added to the pipeline: ARPA-H PRECISE-AI (strongest CAP match), NSF SLES, NSF ER2.

### 4A. Method (from the Foresight resubmission)

Lead with the funder's concern (centralized-AI capture and manipulation of immutable cognitive data), translate the work into their vocabulary without changing a deliverable, anchor in already-shipped proof, and list concurrent asks with non-overlap notes (within the scrub rules in Part 5).

### 4B. Yar's essential early components, mapped to funder primitives

The minimum viable cognitive-liberty stack **already exists**; the two primitives that do not exist yet are exactly what these grants pay to add.

| Funder-named primitive | Yar early-version component | Built today? | How to say it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cytoplex safety-boundary** | CAP / CAP-Lite: hard execution boundary (blocks diagnosis and treatment, no external write without consent, raw data local, blocks engagement-maximizing or manipulative optimization, forces uncertainty language) | **Yes.** 93 passing safety tests of 243 total | The on-device control layer that makes an AI companion structurally unable to manipulate, diagnose, or exfiltrate. "Suggest, not decide." **Lead with this.** |
| **Zero-custody / Solid storage** | Local-first default: raw captures stay on device (SQLite + Anytype, end-to-end encrypted, user-owned) | **Partial.** Local-first shipped; the published Solid pod schema is the deliverable | Zero-custody architecture: the org is architecturally incapable of pooling, selling, or being compelled to surrender data it never holds. |
| **Post-quantum crypto** | PQC encapsulation (NIST ML-KEM, ML-DSA) of any sync and federated-gradient traffic | **Not yet.** Open reference implementation is the deliverable | Built in before networked sync ships, so immutable cognitive data is "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" resilient from day one. |
| On-device inference (supporting) | Gemma 4 via flutter_gemma and LiteRT; working mobile voice loop | **Yes.** Milestone 1 end-to-end | No cloud egress of raw cognitive signals; edge-native. |
| CSP sensor protocol (later) | "MCP for sensors": per-sensor consent, on-device-by-default | Partial (P1) | Open, consent-governed standard; Apache 2.0 public good. |
| P2P federated learning (later) | PQC-wrapped gradients, epsilon budgets | Roadmap | Collective improvement with no bulk-aggregator honeypot. |

**Essential early trio (all built): on-device inference + CAP/Cytoplex + local-first storage.** PQC and the Solid schema are what the grants fund next. Lead the safety asks on the already-built proof; do not over-promise CSP or federated learning.
