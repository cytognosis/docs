This is the **separate companion list** you asked for: organizations for building the **Patient Advocacy Council (PAC)** — the binding governance body in the v2.0 master plan with decision rights over study design, release pace, and the open/proprietary boundary — plus the patient-recruitment and research-partnership network around our mental-health pilot. It is deliberately kept apart from the funding master so "money" and "voice/partnership" don't blur. **OneMind is cross-listed** here and in the funding master because it is uniquely funder + accelerator + advocacy-council all at once.

> Why this matters now: the PAC is a first-class governance structure in the plan, and several funders (OneMind's Rising Star Award, Wellcome's Mental Health prize, PCORI) explicitly reward lived-experience integration. Standing up the PAC early is both a values commitment *and* a measurable strengthener of the Wave-2 mental-health applications.

---

## How to read this

Each organization is tagged by the **primary role** it can play for Cytognosis:

- **PAC seat candidate** — a source of lived-experience members for the council itself.
- **Model-to-emulate** — its governance structure is a template for our PAC charter.
- **Advocacy / policy partner** — alignment, co-signing, legitimacy, reach.
- **Recruitment channel** — access to participants for studies / the Yar beta.
- **Research / data partner** — academic centers (no PAC seat, but data, pilots, co-PIs).
- **Dual/tri-use** — also a funder/accelerator (see funding master).

PAC-fit scores (0–10) and full contact paths are in the workbook. SF Bay Area orgs are flagged — they're priority for a foundership-local council.

## Tier 1 — build the PAC around these

### OneMind — tri-use (funder + accelerator + PAC model) · PAC fit 9 · `P01`
The single most strategically valuable organization across the whole effort. Its **Lived Experience Council (OMLEC)** is the most operationally mature PWLE governance body in mental-health tech — it *co-reviews* research applications and advises startups on product/research design — and its **Community Advisory Network (OMCAN)** extends that to the community. OMLEC is precisely the architecture our PAC charter should adapt. Menlo Park / Napa base makes it Bay-adjacent.
- **Do:** open a relationship with CSO **Tahilia Rebello** and engage OMLEC *before* the 2027 Rising Star cycle; ask to study the OMLEC charter as a template; explore an OMLEC member for a Cytognosis PAC seat.
- https://onemind.org · (funding angles: master `F019` grant, `F051` accelerator)

### DBSA — Depression & Bipolar Support Alliance · PAC fit 8 · `P02`
Mood disorders = our core pilot population, and DBSA's chapters are **peer-run by people with lived experience** — the closest analog to a binding, member-led council, with precedent including a peer-led Congressional briefing.
- **Do:** invite a DBSA peer leader to a PAC seat; pilot the Yar app with DBSA members (recruitment + design feedback). Bay-area chapters exist.
- https://www.dbsalliance.org

### ASAN — Autistic Self Advocacy Network · PAC fit 8 · `P03`
The "**Nothing About Us, Without Us**" standard-bearer; entirely autistic-run, with deep community-based participatory research (CBPR) expertise via AASPIRE. Directly relevant to the **Yar neurodivergent-led design** principle and to governing the open/proprietary boundary with community control.
- **Do:** a CBPR consultation for Yar's ND design; invite a board member to a neurodivergent PAC seat.
- https://autisticadvocacy.org

### NAMI San Francisco · PAC fit 7 · SF Bay anchor · `P04`
The local anchor: a **Youth Council** of people with lived experience (bi-weekly) and a Diversity Advisory Committee, with deep SF Bay community ties for both a PAC seat and community recruitment.
- **Do:** contact the Youth Council (aisha@namisf.org) and Diversity lead (anne@namisf.org); co-sponsor a community info session.
- https://www.namisf.org
