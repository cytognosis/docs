## Flagship plan: RFA-DA-27-004 (TMM R01)

**Why it fits:** the RFA funds mechanistic, predictive, multiscale computational models grounded in empirical data, plus novel ML methods that yield mechanistic insight, with mandatory public tool release. That is exactly the Cytoverse 22q11DS multiscale causal-modeling thesis (and reuses the IGoR TA1 work).

**Recommended structure (decision flagged):** submit **multi-PI with Grama as contact PI and Purdue as the lead applicant; Cytognosis as subaward.**

- **Why:** it nearly eliminates the small-nonprofit risk signal (Purdue HPC, F&A environment, track record), and it dovetails with the **Purdue Research Professor appointment** we already planned for you. You keep full scientific co-PI credit.
- **Trade-off:** Purdue becomes the administrative home and takes the higher F&A; you run your scope via subaward. For a first NIH R01 this is a feature, not a cost.
- **Alternative if you want Cytognosis as prime:** doable (you are eligible), but expect tougher review on infrastructure; mitigate with cloud-compute documentation and a Purdue HPC letter.

**Timeline:** Oct 6, 2026 submit → study section ~spring 2027 → Council ~mid-2027 → earliest funding **late 2027**. This does not touch the Oct 1, 2026 runway cliff.

**Budget:** modest by design (~$150-350K/yr direct). A complement to the diversified runway, not a survival lever.
