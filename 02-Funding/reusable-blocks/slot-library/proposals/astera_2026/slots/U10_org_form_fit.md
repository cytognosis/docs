# U10 — Why-not-academia / why-Cytognosis (Astera 2026 overlay)

## U10.1 — Why not a standard academic lab

The work spans three full PhD's worth of expertise (foundation-model engineering at sequence scale; single-cell genomics + ontology engineering; cross-disorder phenotypic neuropsychiatry) and demands sustained shipping cadence rather than publication cadence. A traditional PI lab optimizes for the next paper; the asset Cytognosis is building is the **shipped artifacts** — open models, open atlases, open evaluation benchmarks — that are downstream of any one paper. Academic lab structure also struggles with the engineering rigor required: continuous-integration training pipelines, model registry hygiene, FAIR-compliant data infrastructure, public model cards. These are operations functions that academic labs systematically under-resource.

## U10.2 — Why not a VC-backed startup

The outputs we are committed to releasing — open foundation models trained on consortium-restricted data, open evaluation benchmarks, public model cards documenting failure modes — are inherently misaligned with venture economics. A startup raising on Cytoverse would be pressured toward proprietary release, controlled-access models, and IP capture of consortium-derived assets. Astera's open-science requirement matches what we want to build; it would actively contradict what a VC-backed structure would demand.

## U10.3 — Why this form (Astera Residency)

Astera Residency is the only program structurally aligned with shipping open infrastructure as the deliverable rather than as a side-effect. The 12-month period matches the natural rhythm of three sequential foundation-modeling milestones. The Emeryville on-site requirement creates the cross-pollination between residents that an early-stage research nonprofit benefits from disproportionately. Astera's published Open Science Policy is the operational scaffold we already conform to (Apache 2.0 / CC BY 4.0; FAIR data; public model cards; bias audit reporting). We are submitting because Astera is, structurally, what we already are.

## U10.4 — What Cytognosis can do that alternatives can't

Cytognosis (a) ships **open** at the foundational layer (models, atlases, benchmarks) where everyone else releases proprietary or paywalled artifacts, (b) maintains **operations rigor** (release pipeline, model cards, evaluation harness) that traditional academic labs do not staff for, and (c) holds the cross-scale architecture (micro-genomics + meso-connectomics + macro-behavior) as a unified design rather than a federated patchwork. Astera-as-residency is the form that makes those three commitments enforceable.
