# ARIA (UK) — Full Programmes

> **Status**: Active
> **Date**: 2026-07-10
> **Author**: @shahin
> **Audience**: leadership, grant team
> **Tags**: `funding`, `opportunity-profile`

## Eligibility
Solicited applications welcomed from across R&D ecosystem: individuals, universities, research institutions, SMEs and large companies, charities, public sector research organisations. Primary focus on UK-based applicants. Requires majority of project work conducted in the UK (>50% of project costs and personnel time). Non-UK orgs can participate as part of a UK-led consortium. No restriction on prior funding history.

## Funding range
Programme awards typically multi-million GBP per project, with full programmes spanning multi-year multi-project portfolios coordinated by a Programme Director. Total programme budgets often £50M+. Period of performance variable per programme. Indirect-cost rules: ARIA uses cost-based contracts, not standard UKRI fEC. Cost share not required.

## Deadline pattern
Per-call deadlines published on aria.org.uk/funding-opportunities/. Each opportunity space has its own coordinated programme call schedule with concept paper deadlines and full proposal deadlines. Multiple distinct calls open at any time. Effectively rolling at the agency level but per-call discrete dates.

## Submission shape
Two-stage for most programme calls:
1. Concept Paper (short, technical sketch + budget envelope)
2. Full Proposal (invited from competitive concepts) — project & technical info, team, administrative info (budget, IP, conflicts of interest)

Some opportunity seed calls skip the concept paper. Submission via aria.grantplatform.com.

## Required artifacts
- Concept paper (page limit varies per call; typically 5-10 pages)
- Full proposal (technical narrative, team composition, budget)
- Statements of work
- Letters of support / collaboration commitments
- Budget justification
- IP & conflict-of-interest declaration
- Risk register

## Required topics / questions
Each call's solicitation specifies. Across calls, recurring topics:
- Opportunity-space framing (consequential · under-explored · ripe-for-now triple)
- Technical approach with falsifiable hypothesis
- Why-now / enabling conditions
- Quantitative milestones and go/no-go gates
- Team (PI + collaborators)
- Comparative advantage (why this team / consortium)
- Budget by phase, by partner
- Risk register with mitigations
- IP plan (ARIA permits flexible IP arrangements; default open-by-design with carve-outs)
- Conflict-of-interest declaration

## Notes
- ARIA Programme Directors set the agenda; opportunity spaces are PD-curated rather than open RFP topic.
- 10 live opportunity spaces as of late 2024. Not all align with Cytognosis focus; Programmable Plants, Scalable Neural Interfaces, Mathematics for Safe AI, Forecasting AI, Safeguarded AI most relevant.
- Award notification: contracting expected within 6 weeks.
- ARIA does not mandate open-access publication but encourages public-good outputs.
- Selection criteria detailed per solicitation.
