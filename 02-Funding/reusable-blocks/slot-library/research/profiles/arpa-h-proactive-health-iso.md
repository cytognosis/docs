# ARPA-H Proactive Health Office (PHO) ISO

## Eligibility
Academic institutions, non-profits, for-profit companies, hospitals, community health centers, non-federal research orgs. **Restricted**: FFRDCs and government entities prohibited from direct participation except under exceptional justification. International participation allowed via teaming with US-led primes.

## Funding range
Other Transaction (OT) agreements. No fixed dollar ceilings or floors stated; multi-million per project typical for ARPA-H Mission Office work. Cost share not required.

## Deadline pattern
Rolling through 2029-03-05. Same as HSF ISO timing.

## Submission shape
Identical to ARPA-H HSF ISO:
1. Solution Summary (Appendix A) — required, must receive ARPA-H written feedback
2. Full Proposal (T&M Volume + Cost Volume + TDD) — only after feedback

## Required artifacts
Same as HSF ISO (see arpa-h-health-science-futures-iso.md).

## Required topics / questions
PHO-specific scope priorities:
- **Prevention & early detection**: scalable sensing, low-cost diagnostics
- **Behavioral & population-level interventions**: wellness adoption, mental resilience, independent aging
- **System innovations** for proactive care delivery: new valuation models for prevention, predictive epidemiological modeling
- Quantitative milestones (Heilmeier-style)
- Population-level mechanism of action
- Equity / access (proactive health must reach beyond highest-resource populations)
- Implementation pathway (who delivers, how paid)
- Behavioral-design ethics if relevant
- Data privacy for sensing / digital health

## Schema gaps observed
- **Population-level intervention design** — partly U17 (adoption), but population-scale public-health ROI is distinctive. Could be U17 sub-slot or new U18 sub-slot ("population-scale value capture").
- **Behavioral / adoption science** — covered by U17 broadly; specific behavioral-design sub-slot might help.
- **New valuation models for prevention** — financing innovation distinct from typical TEA. Could fit U18.5 (TEA / LCA analog).
- **Continuous sensing / wearable data ethics** — U14.6 + U15.4 cover; PHO emphasizes long-horizon data custody.
- Otherwise same gaps as HSF ISO.
