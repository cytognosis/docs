# Coefficient Giving (formerly Open Philanthropy) — Science and Global Health R&D

## Eligibility
Open globally to research orgs, nonprofits, academic institutions, for-profit orgs (with public-good outputs). No geographic restriction. Grant historically went to ~1-2% of unsolicited proposals. Mostly proactive grant-making (Coefficient reaches out to shape proposals); minority of funded grants from cold inbound.

## Funding range
No published range. Historically Open Philanthropy made grants from ~$100k to $50M+. Typical R&D grant $500k-$5M over 1-3 years. Indirect cost flexibility (negotiated). No cost share.

## Deadline pattern
Rolling. Inbound by email to science@coefficientgiving.org. No formal cycles.

## Submission shape
Email submission:
- Existing proposal submitted to another funder (PDF), OR
- New 1-2 page proposal with rough budget outline and timeline

Coefficient may follow up with structured questions or invite a longer proposal.

## Required artifacts
Initial:
- Email with 1-2 page proposal OR existing proposal from another funder
- Rough budget
- Timeline

If invited to develop:
- Detailed scientific proposal
- Detailed budget
- Team / org info
- Open-output / data-sharing plan
- Counterfactual / cost-effectiveness analysis (Coefficient is rigorous on counterfactual)

## Required topics / questions
- Problem and significance (global health priority)
- Approach and feasibility
- Counterfactual: what would happen without this grant
- Cost-effectiveness (DALYs averted per $, or analogous)
- Team and capacity
- Open-output / public-good plan

## Schema gaps observed
- **Counterfactual analysis** — distinct from existing slots. EA / OpenPhil-style "would this happen without you" framing. Could be U10 sub-slot ("counterfactual narrative") — extends "why-not-academia / why-not-VC" with "why-not-without-funder."
- **Cost-effectiveness / DALY framing** — partly U04.3 (magnitude/reach) and U18.5 (TEA/LCA analog). Could be U18.5 sub-slot for "cost-effectiveness model (DALY/QALY/$)."
- **Email-based / unstructured submission** — no fixed template. Render-time flexibility, no new slot.
- **Proactive vs. reactive grant-making** — funder metadata.
