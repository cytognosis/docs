# Convergent Research — Focused Research Organization (FRO)

> **Status**: Active
> **Date**: 2026-07-10
> **Author**: @shahin
> **Audience**: leadership, grant team
> **Tags**: `funding`, `opportunity-profile`
> **Variants**: Technical (this doc) - Readable (Obsidian twin optional, same filename) - Agent (n/a)

## Eligibility
Open globally to founding teams pursuing scientific ideas that fit the FRO model: well-defined milestones, 5–7 year timeframe, $20–80M scale, requiring engineering-heavy team, with public-good outputs unlikely to be funded by academia or VC. No formal entity required to start; team / individual proposers welcome.

## Funding range
Convergent does NOT fund directly. They incubate FROs and match them with their network of philanthropic funders (Schmidt Sciences, Astera Institute, Open Philanthropy, individual donors). FRO budgets typically $20–80M total over 3–7 years; staffing 20–60 FTE.

## Deadline pattern
Rolling. No deadlines — exploratory inbound is encouraged at any time.

## Submission shape
Multi-stage pre-launch process:
1. **Initial idea sketch** (FAQ-checked self-fit) submitted via the get-involved page
2. **Exploratory conversations** with Convergent team
3. **Programmatic incubation**: scoping, technical roadmapping, operational planning, budget building, fundraising support
4. **Extensive technical proposal** developed jointly with Convergent and external expert reviewers
5. **Funder match-up** and proposal finalization
6. **FRO launch** with Convergent-supported infrastructure

## Required artifacts
Idea-stage:
- Idea description with founding-team narrative
- Why-FRO-not-academia narrative
- Initial milestone sketch

Proposal-stage (developed during incubation):
- Extensive technical proposal with milestones
- Technology transfer / open-output plan
- Budget by year/phase, organizational design
- Team plan (which roles, hiring plan)
- Funder pitch deck
- Risk register
- Confidentiality: do NOT disclose unfiled IP without an agreement; Convergent will not share without permission

## Required topics / questions
Self-fit checklist (FRO model criteria):
- Is the project scientifically high-impact?
- Is there a 5–7 year goal with falsifiable milestones?
- Is the project too applied for academia / too pre-commercial for VC?
- Does it require engineering talent at scale?
- Are outputs public goods (data, software, hardware platforms)?
- Is it the kind of work where a tight team executing intensely can outperform a distributed academic effort?

Topics covered during proposal development:
- Plain-language objective
- State of the art and limits
- Novelty / approach / why-now
- Beneficiaries (research community, downstream applications)
- Quantitative milestones
- Org form (FRO-specific) and team
- Open science / IP plan
- Sustainability / transition (FRO sunset or graduation)
- Risk
