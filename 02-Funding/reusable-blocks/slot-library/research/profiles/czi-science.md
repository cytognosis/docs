# Chan Zuckerberg Initiative — Science Grants

## Eligibility
Domestic (US) and foreign nonprofit organizations, public and private institutions (universities, hospitals, laboratories), state/local government, eligible federal agencies. **For-profit organizations not eligible** to receive funding directly but may collaborate. Grants awarded to institutions, not individuals. Each application designates a Coordinating Principal Investigator. Foreign collaborators welcome.

## Funding range
RFA-specific. CZI Science RFAs typically range from $50k pilot grants (Open Science) to $1-3M Imaging / Computational Biology / Cell Science programs. Multi-year (1-5 years). Indirect cost capped at 15% MTDC for most CZI grants (lower than NIH). No cost share required.

## Deadline pattern
Per-RFA. Most RFAs run annual or biennial cycles with discrete deadlines. CZI's grants management portal lists all open RFAs. Inventory deadline 2026-06-05 likely tied to a specific open RFA.

## Submission shape
Online application via apply.chanzuckerberg.com. Per-RFA structure:
- Standard required fields (PI, institution, key personnel, % effort)
- Project narrative (length varies by RFA, often 5-10 pages)
- Budget + justification
- Bibliography
- CVs / biosketches
- Letters (where applicable)

## Required artifacts
- Online application form
- Project narrative (RFA-specific length)
- Budget + justification (CZI templates)
- Key personnel listing with % effort (mandatory)
- Biosketches / CVs
- Bibliography
- DEI / Open Science statement (for many programs)
- Letters of support / collaboration (where applicable)

## Required topics / questions
Across CZI Science RFAs:
- Scientific question and CZI grand-challenge alignment (cell biology, infectious disease, single-cell, imaging, AI in biology)
- Methodology and feasibility
- Open science commitments (CZI is open-by-default — preprints, data, code, protocols)
- Open Science Plan: data, software, hardware, models
- Diversity, Equity & Inclusion plan (workforce + research)
- Ethical considerations (where applicable)
- Risk and mitigation
- Budget justification with effort allocations
- Timeline / milestones
- Career stage / institutional environment

## Notes
- CZI also runs Education, Community, Special Projects, Open Science streams.
- Co-managed with the CZ Biohubs (Network: SF, Chicago, NY, Imaging Institute).
- Grant funds cannot be transferred into mainland China for some programs.
- Aggressive open-by-default ethos — preprint mandates, code release, data deposition.
- Multi-year strategic moves (2024-2025): increased focus on AI in science (with Schmidt Sciences partnership).
