# EA Funds — Long-Term Future Fund (LTFF)

> **Status**: Active
> **Date**: 2026-07-10
> **Author**: @shahin
> **Audience**: leadership, grant team
> **Tags**: `funding`, `opportunity-profile`
> **Variants**: Technical (this doc) - Readable (Obsidian twin optional, same filename) - Agent (n/a)

## Eligibility
Individuals, non-profit organizations, academic institutions, other entities. Global eligibility (no US/UK requirement). Particular interest in small teams / individuals trying to get projects off the ground or needing less than typical institutional grant size.

## Funding range
$1,000 – $500,000+ (no fixed cap). Common buckets:
- Independent research projects (3-12 months): $20,000-$80,000
- Conference travel / upskilling: $1,000-$10,000
- Organizational seed funding: $50,000-$200,000
- Inventory typical $100,000

No indirect-cost rules; treated as direct. No cost share.

## Deadline pattern
Rolling applications. Application reviews approximately every 4 months (October, February, June). Decisions usually within 2-8 weeks.

## Submission shape
Single online form via funds.effectivealtruism.org/apply-for-funding:
- Short Description (~few sentences)
- Summary (most-reviewed section)
- Project narrative (recommended 2,000-5,000 chars; max 10,000 chars total)
- Budget
- Team / individual background

## Required artifacts
- Online application form
- (Optional) supporting docs / CV / prior work
- (Optional) references / advisors

## Required topics / questions
- Brief project description
- Expected impact on existential / global catastrophic risk
- Counterfactual value (would this happen without LTFF?)
- Theory of impact
- Applicant ability to execute
- Cost-effectiveness of budget
- Honest weaknesses / risks of project
- Timeline and key milestones

## Schema gaps observed
- **Counterfactual narrative** — same gap as Coefficient Giving; suggests U10 sub-slot ("why-not-without-this-funder").
- **X-risk / GCR alignment statement** — existential-risk framing distinct from typical health-impact framing. Could be a sub-slot under U04 (stakes) for "GCR / x-risk theory of change."
- **"Honest weaknesses" prompt** — explicit funder ask. Already mostly U05.4 (failure-mode narrative); may be the same content, but the explicit funder framing matters.
- **Character-count budgets (10,000 char hard cap)** — render-time concern.
- **Individual-as-grantee** — A03 sub-slot (eligibility for individuals).
