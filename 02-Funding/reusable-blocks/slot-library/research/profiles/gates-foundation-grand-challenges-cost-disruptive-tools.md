# Gates Foundation Grand Challenges — Cost-Disruptive Tools for Diagnosis and Screening

## Eligibility
Open globally. Eligible: research institutes, nonprofit orgs, for-profit companies, international organizations, government agencies, academic institutions. Multi-stakeholder partnerships encouraged. LMIC-relevance is mandatory.

## Funding range
Up to 5 awards of approximately $300,000 each. Grant term up to 24 months. Designed for early feasibility / prototyping, including technically risky / out-of-the-box concepts. Indirect cost: Gates uses 10% indirect cost cap typically. No cost share required.

## Deadline pattern
Single deadline: 2026-04-28 at 11:30 AM PDT. Webinar: 2026-03-30 8:30-9:30 AM PT.

## Submission shape
Single-stage online application via submit.gatesfoundation.org. Standard Grand Challenges proposal format.

## Required artifacts
- Online application form
- Project proposal narrative (typically 5-page / structured sections)
- Investigator info / CVs
- Budget + justification
- Letters of support (optional / situational)
- Open-output / data-sharing commitment

## Required topics / questions
- **In-scope challenge area**: TB, HIV, malaria, STIs, maternal & newborn health, anemia/women's health, etc
- **Cost-disruption mechanism**: how does this device/test materially reset the cost curve?
- **Capital amortization to near-zero incremental cost** OR **$1-class consumable test**
- LMIC deployment constraints (real-world fieldability, supply chain, climate, training)
- Novel sensing modality OR software-defined diagnostic OR AI-enabled / software-only approach
- Performance characteristics (sensitivity, specificity, throughput)
- Feasibility of 24-month proof-of-concept
- Team capability
- Open-output / Gates Foundation Open Access policy compliance
- Risks and contingencies
- Path from PoC to scale

## Schema gaps observed
- **Cost-disruption framing** — distinctive Gates language; covered by U18.5 (TEA/LCA) and U17.4 (switching cost).
- **LMIC deployment constraints** — partly U13.2 (affordability/access plan). Gates wants explicit LMIC deployability evidence; could be U13 sub-slot.
- **Gates Foundation Open Access policy** — strict requirements; partly U12. Funder-specific OA policy reference (CC-BY 4.0 mandate, immediate open access). Could be U12.2 funder-hook.
- **Global Access commitment** — Gates-specific term-sheet item. Could be U18 / U19 sub-slot.
