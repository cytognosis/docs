## Eligibility
**Consortium required** for most calls: at least 3 independent legal entities, each established in a different country, of which at least 1 in an EU Member State and ≥2 others in Member States or Horizon Europe Associated Countries. Eligible legal entities: universities, research institutions, SMEs, large companies, NGOs, public bodies, hospitals, individual researchers (rarely as standalone). UK is currently associated. US entities can participate but typically not funded — contribute their own resources or via subcontract.

## Funding range
Indicative EU contribution €1.5M – €10M per project for most Cluster 1 topics; up to 100% costs covered for RIA/CSA, up to 70% for IA (industrial). 25% flat-rate indirect cost. Typical 3-5 year duration. Cost share required for IA actions but not RIA.

## Deadline pattern
Most 2026 topics: single-stage submission with 16 April 2026 deadline. Some topics two-stage (Stage 1 short proposal → Stage 2 full proposal). Annual Work Programme cycles; 2026-2027 Work Programme released December 2025.

## Submission shape
Submitted via EU Funding & Tenders Portal. Two action types most common:
- **RIA (Research and Innovation Action)**: full proposal, ~70 page Part B narrative
- **IA (Innovation Action)**: full proposal with stronger commercialization
- **CSA (Coordination & Support Action)**: 30-50 page narrative
- **PPI (Public Procurement of Innovative Solutions)**: distinct format

## Required artifacts
**Part A** (administrative, in portal forms):
- Consortium identification, all beneficiaries with PIC numbers
- Budget by partner, by work package
- Ethics issues table

**Part B** (PDF upload, structured template):
- Section 1: Excellence (objectives, ambition, methodology, GoLs/PoCs, gender dimension, Open Science practices)
- Section 2: Impact (expected outcomes, measures to maximize impact, IPR strategy, communication & dissemination & exploitation [CDE] plan)
- Section 3: Implementation (work plan, work packages, deliverables, milestones, risks, consortium description, resources)
- Annex: Letters of support, ethics self-assessment, declarations
- Optional: Security Aspects Letter (SAL) for sensitive topics

## Required topics / questions
- **Excellence (40%)**: scientific objectives, novelty, methodology, interdisciplinary approach, gender dimension in research content, Open Science practices, FAIR data
- **Impact (40%)**: contribution to expected destinations/KIPs (Key Impact Pathways), magnitude/significance, measures to maximize impact, exploitation/CDE plan, IPR
- **Implementation (20%)**: work plan, work-package structure, deliverables, milestones, risks, consortium roles, resource allocation
- **Ethics self-assessment**: human/animal subjects, data protection, dual use, third country engagement, EU values
- **Gender Equality Plan**: required of beneficiary organizations
- **Open Science**: open access publications, FAIR data, RDM plan, Open Science other practices

## Schema gaps observed
- **Three-pillar evaluation** (Excellence / Impact / Implementation) — distinct from US grant structure. Render-time concern; canonical slots cover the content.
- **Key Impact Pathways (KIPs) / Destinations** — Horizon Europe's outcome framework. Could be U04 sub-slot for "destination/KIP alignment narrative."
- **Gender dimension in research content** — distinct from U13 (broader equity); EU-specific. Could be U13 sub-slot "gender-in-research-content."
- **Gender Equality Plan** — institutional artifact; A01 sub-slot.
- **CDE Plan (Communication, Dissemination, Exploitation)** — partly U12, U17, U18 but EU-structured. Could need U12 / U17 sub-slot for "CDE Plan template."
- **Ethics Self-Assessment** — covered by U16, but EU template is structured; could be A03 sub-field with EU-specific 13-issue checklist.
- **Consortium structure** — work-packages with partners-by-WP. Covered by U20.2 but Horizon-Europe-specific work-package format is detailed. Could be U07 / U20 sub-slot.
- **PIC / EU portal IDs** — A01 administrative ID extension.
- **Security Aspects Letter** — for dual-use topics; A03 sub-slot.

## Notes
- Cluster 1 Health is one of 6 Clusters in Pillar 2 (Global Challenges).
- 2026 Work Programme: 6 Destinations including "Staying healthy in a rapidly changing society," "Living and working in a health-promoting environment," "Tackling diseases and reducing disease burden," "Ensuring access to innovative, sustainable and high-quality health care," "Unlocking the full potential of new tools, technologies and digital solutions for a healthy society," "Maintaining an innovative, sustainable and globally competitive health industry."
- Info Day held 2026-02-10.
- Heavy on consortium-management overhead — typically 18+ months from submission to start.
