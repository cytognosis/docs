# NIH Bridge2AI Initiative (Stage II)

> **Status**: Active
> **Date**: 2026-07-10
> **Author**: @shahin
> **Audience**: leadership, grant team
> **Tags**: `funding`, `opportunity-profile`

## Eligibility
US institutions of higher education, research institutes, eligible nonprofits. Researchers with expertise in both AI/ML methods and specific disease areas. Bridge2AI explicitly values interdisciplinary teams (AI + clinical + ethics + standards). Foreign collaboration permitted as sub-recipients.

## Funding range
$130M total over 4 years for Stage 2 (pending appropriations). Per-award amount not yet set; Stage 1 awards (data generation projects) ranged $20–35M over 4 years. Stage 2 Innovation Funnels likely lower per-award (~$5–15M over 4y) but multi-award. NIH standard F&A indirect rates apply (typically 60-70% MTDC).

## Deadline pattern
Stage 2 RFAs not yet posted as of January 2026; expected mid-2026. Inventory deadline 2026-07-01 reflects expected RFA window. Bridge2AI uses RFA (Request for Applications) mechanism with specific receipt date(s).

## Submission shape
Standard NIH RFA full proposal:
- Letter of Intent (often 30 days before due date, advisory)
- Full application via Grants.gov / ASSIST / eRA Commons
- SF-424 (R&R) form set
- PHS 398 Research Plan (12 pages typical, may be longer for U01/U54 mechanisms)

## Required artifacts
- SF-424 (R&R) cover, project/performance sites, key personnel
- Project Summary/Abstract (30 lines)
- Project Narrative (3 sentences public health relevance)
- Specific Aims (1 page)
- Research Strategy (Significance, Innovation, Approach — 12 pages)
- Bibliography
- Facilities & Other Resources
- Equipment
- Biosketches (NIH 5-page format) for all key personnel
- Budget (R&R modular or detailed) + Justification
- Subaward budgets (if applicable)
- Letters of Support
- Resource Sharing Plan / Data Management & Sharing Plan (NIH DMS Policy 2023+)
- Authentication of Key Biological & Chemical Resources
- Vertebrate Animals (if applicable)
- Human Subjects Section (if applicable; PHS Human Subjects & Clinical Trials Information form)
- Inclusion of Women, Minorities, Children plan
- Multiple PI Leadership Plan (if applicable)
- Consortium / Contractual Arrangements
- AI / ML methodology disclosure (Bridge2AI specific)

## Required topics / questions
- Significance of biomedical / health problem
- Innovation: what AI-method + dataset + clinical advance is novel
- Approach: study design, methods, analyses, timeline
- Investigators / Environment
- Data ethics, FAIR principles, ELSI (Ethical, Legal, Social Implications) — Bridge2AI emphasizes
- Standards & metadata (Bridge2AI's flagship is interoperable AI-ready datasets)
- Workforce diversity / training plan (Bridge2AI specific)
- AI safety / trustworthy-AI commitments — Stage 2's "Network for AI Health Science" includes safety framework
- Data sharing through NIH-designated repository
- Health equity and inclusion plans

## Schema gaps observed
- **NIH Data Management & Sharing Plan (2023+ policy)** — covered by U12.5 (FAIR/reproducibility) and U15.3 (data governance), but DMS Plan has specific elements (data types, standards, repositories, timelines) — could be U12 sub-slot.
- **AI-readiness / FAIR-by-design** — Bridge2AI demands datasets be AI-ready before funding generation. U12 doesn't explicitly capture AI-readiness; consider U12.6 or U14.7.
- **ELSI framework** — Ethical/Legal/Social Implications block explicit in NIH AI; partly in U14, U16, U13. Could add U14.7 ELSI plan.
- **Inclusion across the Lifespan** — U13 covers but NIH-specific subform required.
- **Multiple PI leadership plan** — A02 (POC) doesn't model multi-PI governance; could need sub-slot.
- **Network/Consortium roles within Bridge2AI** — covered by U20.
- **Clinical-trial / IRB-stage detail** — U16.2 covers, may need more granular IRB-stage tracking.

## Notes
- Stage 1 funded: a) Data Generation Projects across 5 disease areas, b) BRIDGE Center (integration/dissemination/evaluation), c) Skills & Workforce Center.
- Stage 2 Innovation Funnels = AI tools/devices using Stage 1 datasets. Network for AI Health Science = safety/ethics framework.
- Approved by NIH Council of Councils 2026-01-29.
- Likely U54 / U01 / U24 cooperative agreement mechanism.
- Bridge2AI sets a high bar for cross-disciplinary teams — single-PI applications unlikely competitive.
