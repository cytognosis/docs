# NIH PRIMED-AI (Precision Medicine with AI: Integrating Imaging with Multimodal Data)

## Eligibility
US institutions of higher education, research institutes, eligible nonprofits, hospitals, and (for Academic-Industrial Partnerships initiative) industrial partners. Several initiatives have specific eligibility — Translating Models to Clinic, Multi-use Framework Playbooks, Academic-Industrial Partnerships. Multidisciplinary teams (AI/ML, clinical imaging, EHR, biostatistics, ethics) expected.

## Funding range
$25M typical per inventory; UG3/UH3 cooperative agreement biphasic mechanism. UG3 (R&D phase) typical $1-2M/yr; UH3 (translation/clinical phase) typical $2-5M/yr; combined 3-5 years. Standard NIH F&A indirect rates apply.

## Deadline pattern
First wave RFAs announced early 2026; inventory deadline 2026-02-11. Multiple sub-RFAs (Translating Models to Clinic UG3/UH3; Multi-use Framework Playbooks UG3; Academic-Industrial Partnerships UG3/UH3) with separate due dates.

## Submission shape
Standard NIH RFA full proposal; UG3/UH3 biphasic cooperative agreement.

## Required artifacts
Standard NIH (see Bridge2AI profile) plus PRIMED-AI specific:
- Multimodal data integration plan (imaging + clinical + lab + omics + EHR)
- AI-readiness statement for datasets
- Standardized imaging protocol description
- Clinical decision support (CDS) tool development plan
- EHR vendor partnership letters (Academic-Industrial initiative)
- Federated-learning / privacy-preserving design plan (where applicable)
- Bias / equity assessment of AI models
- Validation cohort design (retrospective + prospective)
- IRB / human subjects assurance
- DMS Plan
- Phase transition criteria UG3 → UH3 (NIH-specific go/no-go)

## Required topics / questions
- **Standardization & Data Integration**: standardized imaging, metadata retention, ontologies
- **AI-Based CDS Tools**: harmonized ML platform, bias mitigation, privacy
- **Demonstrating Clinical Utility**: diverse datasets, EHR vendor partnerships
- **Innovative Methods**: federated learning, multimodal fusion, foundation models
- AI ethics / responsible AI use
- Validation methodology (technical performance + clinical impact)
- Health-equity / bias-audit plan
- Open-source / sharing of models, code, datasets (where IRB permits)
- UG3-to-UH3 transition criteria

## Schema gaps observed
- **Multimodal data integration** — distinct concept; partly U03.2 (technical approach), U15.3 (data governance). May need U03 sub-slot for "data modality inventory."
- **Federated learning / privacy-preserving compute architecture** — covered by U14.6 (governance) and U15.4 (privacy architecture). Adequate.
- **CDS tool development pathway** — partly U17 (adoption) + U16.1 (FDA SaMD/IVD). Adequate.
- **UG3-to-UH3 phase transition** — biphasic NIH mechanism. U07.2 / U08.2 cover phasing but NIH-specific UG3/UH3 has structured go/no-go template; could be a sub-slot under U08.
- **EHR vendor partnership** — covered by U20.1.
