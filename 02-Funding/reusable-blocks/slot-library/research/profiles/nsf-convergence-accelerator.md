# NSF Convergence Accelerator

> **Status**: Active
> **Date**: 2026-07-10
> **Author**: @shahin
> **Audience**: leadership, grant team
> **Tags**: `funding`, `opportunity-profile`
> **Variants**: Technical (this doc) - Readable (Obsidian twin optional, same filename) - Agent (n/a)

## Eligibility
US institutions of higher education + multi-sector partners (must include partners from multiple sectors: colleges/universities, industry, non-profits, community orgs, government). Letter of Intent step required identifying multidisciplinary team. Foreign partners allowed but not as lead.

## Funding range
- **Phase 1**: up to $750,000 for 12 months (9 months of intensive curriculum + 3 months pitch prep)
- **Phase 2**: up to $5,000,000 for 24 months (proof-of-concept to product/deliverable)
Total ≤ ~$5.75M over <3 years. Standard NSF F&A indirect rates apply.

## Deadline pattern
Track-specific cohort cycles. Tracks announced annually with phase-1 LOI/proposal deadlines. Currently in 2025 cohort cycle (per NSF 23-590); 2026 cohort tracks TBD. Inventory deadline blank — depends on cohort.

## Submission shape
- **LOI** (track-specific deadline, ~1-2 months before full proposal)
- **Phase 1 full proposal** (selected cohort)
- **Phase 2 proposal** by invitation (only Phase 1 winners) with formal pitch presentation

## Required artifacts
- LOI: team identification, deliverable summary, track alignment
- Phase 1 Project Description (~15 pages): deliverable, research plan, team formation process, proof-of-concept scope, partnerships
- Standard NSF artifacts: biosketches, budget, current & pending, COA, facilities
- Letters of Collaboration from partners
- Statement of intent for participation in 9-month NSF curriculum (innovation curriculum, human-centered design, customer discovery, pitch coaching)
- Pitch deck (Phase 2)

## Required topics / questions
- Track relevance: how the project fits track topic & subtopic
- Track-success contribution: how proposed work advances the overall Track goals
- One or more concrete deliverables with societal impact
- Team formation process and partner engagement plan
- Path to transition into practice (key deliverable for the program)
- Phase 1 milestones (proof-of-concept by month 9)
- Phase 2 transition vision (product, policy, deployment)
- Customer discovery / human-centered design plan
- Sustainability after grant period

## Notes
- Track examples (historical): AI-driven food security, climate resilience, mental health, etc.
- Program model emphasizes use-inspired research with deliverables (not basic science).
