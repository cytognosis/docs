# NSF-NIH Smart Health Program (NSF 25-542)

## Eligibility
US institutions of higher education, non-profit research orgs, certain other entities (per NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide PAPPG). Multidisciplinary teams required: must include at least two of {fundamental science/engineering, biomedical/health/clinical research, technology vendors, client/end-user groups}. Foreign collaboration permitted.

## Funding range
Up to $1,200,000 per project ($300,000/year × 4 years max). Annual program budget $15-20M; estimated 10-16 awards per year. NSF standard cost principles. Indirect costs at institutional negotiated F&A rate.

## Deadline pattern
Annual full-proposal window (typically September/October per NSF cycle). Inventory date 2026-09-10. No LOI required per current solicitation.

## Submission shape
Single-stage full proposal via Research.gov / Grants.gov. PAPPG format.

## Required artifacts
- Project Summary (1 page) with Overview, Intellectual Merit, Broader Impacts
- Project Description (15 pages max) — required to address SCH-specific elements
- References Cited
- Biographical Sketches (NSF format, 3 pages)
- Budget + Budget Justification
- Current & Pending Support
- Facilities, Equipment & Other Resources
- Data Management & Sharing Plan (2 pages)
- Postdoctoral Mentoring Plan (if postdocs included, 1 page)
- Collaborators & Other Affiliations (COA)
- Letters of Collaboration (NSF-specific format, no opinion-letters)
- Project Personnel and Partner Org list
- Optional: Single-Copy Documents (List of Suggested Reviewers, etc.)

## Required topics / questions
Project Description must cover:
- Health problem and clinical impact
- Multi-disciplinary collaboration plan (active participation across sectors required)
- Computing/engineering/data-science contribution + biomedical contribution
- AI/ML methodology where relevant; data science approach
- Risks and contingencies
- Evaluation methodology (validation in real-world or representative setting)
- Broader impacts (NSF-specific): workforce, education, society, equity
- Fundamental contributions to two or more disciplines (mandatory)
- Data sharing, software/code release plan
- Collaboration with technology vendors / client groups (optional but encouraged)

## Schema gaps observed
- **NSF Broader Impacts** — distinct review criterion separate from intellectual merit. U13 partially covers; could need explicit U13 sub-slot for "NSF Broader Impacts narrative" with 5-criterion structure (workforce, STEM education, public engagement, infrastructure, society).
- **Collaboration plan as standalone artifact** — Smart Health requires explicit "Collaboration Plan" describing roles. U20 covers partnerships; may need a Collaboration Plan sub-slot under U20.
- **Multi-PI roles** — A02 sub-slot needed for multi-PI/co-PI structures.
- **Program-attribution language** — funders increasingly require specific publication acknowledgment phrasing; could be A04 sub-slot for "publication acknowledgment template".
- **Data Management & Sharing Plan** — partly U12, U15.3; NSF DMSP is structured, may need U12.5 sub-slots aligned with NSF DMSP elements.

## Notes
- Joint NSF (CISE, ENG, EHR) + NIH (NIBIB, NCI, NHLBI, NIDA, NIA, NLM etc) program.
- Up to 4-year duration. 25-542 is current solicitation; 23-614 was prior.
- Required publication attribution phrase: "as part of the NSF/NIH Smart Health and Biomedical Research in the Era of Artificial Intelligence and Advanced Data Science Program."
