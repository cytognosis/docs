# NSF Tech Labs

## Eligibility
Independent research teams (FROs, nonprofit research orgs, novel institutional forms). RFI welcomed responses from across R&D ecosystem; full solicitation expected spring 2026 with team selections in first half of 2026. US-based. Researchers must be full-time after the 9-month Phase 0; can be achieved by reimbursing the researcher's employer for their salary and benefits (i.e., secondment-style allowed).

## Funding range
$10M to $50M per year for at least 4 years. Structured as Other Transaction (OT) contracts, not traditional NSF grants. Reduced administrative burden, milestone-based payments. Phased: Phase 0 (9 months scoping) → Phase 1 (24 months) → Phase 2 (24+ months). No fixed cost-share requirement. Indirect cost rates negotiated under OT framework, not standard NSF F&A.

## Deadline pattern
RFI responses due 2026-01-20. Full solicitation expected spring 2026. Selection in first half of 2026. After full solicitation issued, expected single-cycle then potentially multi-cohort.

## Submission shape
RFI stage (now) requires written response to ~7 questions. Full solicitation stage (TBD) expected to be: Phase 0 application (white paper / feasibility study scoping) → Phase 1 proposal (full project plan) → Phase 2 continuation (milestone-gated).

## Required artifacts
RFI:
- ~7 narrative responses
- Organizational background

Full solicitation (anticipated, based on RFI structure and ARPA-E precedent):

- Q2: Comparative advantage of independent research orgs vs. status-quo
- Q3a: Phase 0/1/2 plan: i. objective; ii. PI team; iii. governance; iv. consortium / partners; v. eligibility design

- Q4: Eligibility criteria, phase structure, IP defaults, governance, funding levels — feedback prompts
