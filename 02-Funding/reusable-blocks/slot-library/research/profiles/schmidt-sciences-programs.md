## Eligibility
Schmidt Sciences runs many distinct programs each with own eligibility. Major buckets:
- **AI in Science Postdocs Program** — postdoc fellowships at partner universities (UCSD, Michigan, Chicago, Toronto, Oxford)
- **AI Safety Science** — independent researcher / org grants
- **Humanities and AI Virtual Institute** — humanities scholars
- **Polymath / Convergence Labs** — early-career investigators
- **Ocean / Astronomy / Energy** — domain-specific calls

Most programs require a faculty / institutional mentor or sponsor. Some standalone org-level grants exist (FRO funding via Convergent Research network).

## Funding range
Typical inventory $1M. Postdoc programs ~$200-300k/yr stipend. Org-level grants $1-10M over 2-5 years. AI Safety grants typically smaller (~$100k–$1M). Indirect cost rules program-specific; postdoc fellowships typically include modest indirect.

## Deadline pattern
Per-program annual cycles. Inventory deadline 2026-05-17 likely tied to a specific call. Most postdoc programs have September-October opening with October-November close for the following year's cohort.

## Submission shape
Highly variable per program. Typical pattern:
- Online application form on schmidtsciences.smapply.io
- Research statement (often 2-5 pages)
- Mentor / nomination letter
- CV / biosketch
- For postdoc programs: 2 letters of recommendation, IDP (Individualized Development Plan)

## Required artifacts
- Application form (program-specific)
- Research statement / proposal (2-5 pages typical)
- CV / biosketch
- Letters of recommendation (2-3)
- Mentor or institutional sponsor letter (postdoc programs)
- Budget (org-level grants)
- Open-output / data-sharing plan (where applicable)

## Required topics / questions
- Research vision / agenda
- AI-in-science methodological grounding (apply AI to improve science)
- Mentor / institutional fit narrative
- Career stage / development trajectory
- Demonstrated research excellence
- For org grants: open-science commitments, milestones, sustainability
