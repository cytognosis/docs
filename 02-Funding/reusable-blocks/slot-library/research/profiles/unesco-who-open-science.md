# UNESCO/WHO Open Science Projects

## Eligibility
UNESCO Participation Grant Programme: applications submitted by UNESCO Member States and NGOs in official partnership with UNESCO. Routing via National Commission for UNESCO required (national-government channel). Open-science-aligned projects across education, science, culture, communication priorities.

WHO open-science / health-research grants are separate; not the same scheme.

## Funding range
UNESCO Participation Grant: up to $26,000 USD per project (well below inventory's $250,000 estimate — inventory may reflect a different/larger UNESCO/WHO open-science instrument).

## Deadline pattern
2026-2027 UNESCO Participation Grant Programme cycle: deadline 2026-02-27. Inventory date 2026-04-19 may reflect a downstream national-commission deadline.

## Submission shape
Two-tier:
1. National Commission for UNESCO (or designated government channel) prepares submission
2. UNESCO HQ reviews / awards

## Required artifacts
- Project proposal aligned with UNESCO thematic priorities (education, science, culture, communication)
- Detailed budget
- Realistic timeline
- Sustainability plan
- Community impact narrative
- Government endorsement (via National Commission)

## Required topics / questions
- Alignment with UNESCO Recommendation on Open Science (2021)
- Open access, FAIR data, equitable participation
- Local development contribution
- Long-term sustainability
- Community impact
