## Eligibility
**Lead applicant**: Established researcher with permanent / open-ended / long-term rolling contract, or guarantee of one (not conditional on this award). Must contribute ≥20% of research time to the project. If <3 years contract remaining, must have secured next position with letter of support.

**Host organisation**: Eligible nonprofit organisation in UK, Republic of Ireland, or low/middle-income country (except mainland China and India).

**Coapplicants**: Any career stage, anywhere in the world (except mainland China). Must contribute significantly and essentially.

**Disciplinary scope**: Any discipline (STEM, experimental medicine, humanities, social sciences, clinical/allied health, public health) provided potential to improve human life, health, wellbeing, aligned with Wellcome's funding remit (Discovery / Health / Climate & Health / Mental Health / Infectious Disease).

**Restrictions**: Cannot transfer funds into mainland China; cannot apply if waiting on 2 other Discovery Award decisions as lead; cannot hold parallel Career Development Award.

## Funding range
Typical award £1-3M over 5-8 years (based on typical Discovery Award model). 100% of allowable costs covered (full economic costs in UK system / direct + appropriate overheads internationally). No cost share. No fixed cap; ambition expected.

## Deadline pattern
Multiple cycles per year. Inventory deadline 2026-03-31. Application windows announced on Wellcome website with specific deadlines for preliminary application and full application stages.

## Submission shape
Two-stage:
1. **Preliminary application** (online form, ~5 pages narrative): research vision, novelty, team, host organisation
2. **Full application** (invited): detailed research plan (~10-15 pages), budget, full team, ethics, data, dissemination

## Required artifacts
Preliminary:
- Project summary (lay + scientific)
- Research vision and key questions
- Track record & why-this-team
- Host organisation environment statement
- Letter from host organisation

Full:
- Detailed research plan
- Project budget (UK fEC or international full direct + overheads)
- Team members / coapplicants with roles + contributions
- Ethics self-assessment
- Data Management & Sharing Plan
- Open access publication plan
- Equality, Diversity, Inclusion (EDI) statement (Wellcome-specific)
- Letters of support
- Career history / CVs

## Required topics / questions
- Research vision (bold, ambitious, transformative)
- Novelty / state of the art
- Methodology and feasibility
- Team & environment (host org commitment, coapplicants)
- Equality, Diversity, Inclusion plan
- Open Research practices (preprints, data, code, sharing)
- Ethics / regulatory considerations
- Risk management
- Budget justification (UK fEC or international fEC equivalent)
- Long-term sustainability

## Notes
- Wellcome Trust UK-based but globally open in spirit (LMIC eligibility).
- Successor scheme to Wellcome Investigator Awards (different: Investigator was career-track; Discovery is research-project-focused).
- Aligned with Wellcome's strategic priorities: Discovery Research, Mental Health, Infectious Disease, Climate & Health.
- Awards are large enough to fund a research programme not just a single project.
- Different from Wellcome Leap (separate org, separate funding model).
