## Aggregate schema gaps observed

(Deduplicated across all 71 profiles, grouped by what they imply for the canonical schema.)

### Submission-shape / staging gaps
1. **Two-stage submissions** (LOI → Full Proposal; Concept Paper → Full; Solution Summary → Full; Abstract → Full; Stage 1 → Stage 2) — used by ARIA, ARPA-H (HSF + PHO), Wellcome Trust, Wellcome Leap, Horizon Europe, RWJF, NSF Convergence Accelerator, AWS Imagine Grant, AstraZeneca CHANGE. Currently not modeled in canonical funder schema; need a `submission_stages: [...]` funder-metadata field.
2. **Mandatory written-feedback gate** between stages (ARPA-H ISOs require ARPA-H feedback before any full proposal). Funder-metadata flag.
3. **Cohort vs. rolling** — Astera Residency (cohort), Fast Forward (cohort), 5050 (cohort), most VCs (rolling), most accelerators (cohort/batch). F-family field.
4. **Multi-program funder** (Schmidt Sciences, RWJF, Wellcome Leap have many sub-programs). Funder file should support program disambiguation.

### Eligibility / compliance gaps
5. **Entity-type eligibility** discriminators beyond simple 501(c)(3): individuals (Emergent Ventures, EA Funds), pre-company (Lux, 5050), no-revenue startups (NVIDIA Inception eligible), revenue caps (AstraZeneca CHANGE ≤$12M), age caps (10-year company age), state-level eligibility (CHANGE: CA/DE/MD/MA/TX), 509(a)(3) exclusion (CHANGE), LMIC-only host (Wellcome Trust LMIC list), university-affiliation (Berkeley SkyDeck), in-residency-during-program (IndieBio CA/NY, Astera Emeryville, Foresight Nodes SF/Berlin).
6. **PI minimum effort threshold** (Wellcome Discovery Awards 20%).
7. **Permanent-contract gating** (Wellcome Discovery Awards).
8. **Min applicant age** (Emergent Ventures 13+, Anthropic 18+).
9. **Country-fund-flow restrictions** (Wellcome cannot fund mainland China; CZI similar).
10. **Activate/program-provider gating** (AWS Activate Portfolio tier requires Activate Provider org-id).
11. **Limited-submission per institution** (DOE Genesis: 1 lead per institution).
12. **Research-security disclosures** (NSPM-33, Section 223) for federal funders — beyond what U16 currently models.

### Cost / instrument gaps
13. **Investment instrument fork** — grant vs. equity vs. SAFE vs. credits vs. discount vs. in-kind vs. fellowship vs. hybrid. Currently absent. Strong candidate for a top-level F-family field.
14. **Non-cash compensation** — salary as part of award (Astera), API credits (Anthropic AI for Science), cloud credits (AWS, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA), software licenses (MATLAB, Adobe), hardware discounts (Dell, HP, Lenovo), free SaaS (Canva, Notion, Github).
15. **Joint multi-funder programs** (Microsoft + NVIDIA Healthcare, NSF + NIH Smart Health). F-family field "joint_program / partner_funders."
16. **Indirect-cost cap** funder-specific (CZI 15%, Gates 10%, NIH 60-70%, ARIA 100%-direct, RWJF ~25%, OT-flexible). A03 sub-slot for "indirect cost cap" or funder metadata.
17. **fEC vs. F&A vs. flat-rate vs. OT-negotiated** costing-model variants. A03 sub-slot.
18. **Full-economic-cost (UK)** — Wellcome / ARIA UK — distinct from US F&A.
19. **Currency / VAT** — UK ARIA includes VAT in £500k cap. Funder metadata.
20. **Phase-budget envelopes** (NSF Tech Labs: $10-50M/yr × ≥4y; ARPA-H base + options; DOE Phase I/II; NSF Convergence Phase 1/2). U06.5 covers tiered budgets but per-phase envelope is more structured.

## Recommendations for canonical schema bump (1.0 → 1.1)

1. **Promote F-family to first-class manifest section** — currently inventory has F01-F11; expand to F30 (above) and document in `manifest.yaml` as sibling to `slots:` and `funders:`. Each funder file gets these metadata keys at the top.

2. **Add 2 new slots (U21 Counterfactual, U22 Theory of change)** rather than burying them as sub-slots; they're load-bearing across philanthropy and EA funders.

3. **Expand U13 (equity/access)** with sub-slots U13.6 (gender-in-research-content), U13.7 (community co-design). Horizon Europe and RWJF make this load-bearing.

4. **Expand U14 (responsible AI)** with U14.7 ELSI plan — explicitly required by NIH Bridge2AI and Smart Health.

5. **Expand U16 (regulatory/biosecurity)** with U16.7 research-security/NSPM-33 — this is a federal grant must-have post-2024.

6. **Expand U17 (adoption)** with U17.7 customer-discovery / HCD — NSF Convergence asks specifically.

7. **Expand U18 (Translation-to-Mission)** with U18.6 cost-effectiveness model and U18.7 Global Access — both used by global health funders.

8. **Expand A03 (eligibility/compliance)** with sub-slots A03.6-A03.13 covering: costing model, mentor letter, residency, contract threshold, region list, revenue cap, age cap, limited-submission flag.

9. **Expand A04 (artifacts)** with A04.6-A04.11 covering: demo video gate, pitch deck, 501c3 letter, confidentiality, wet-lab needs spec, publication acknowledgment template.

10. **Document new funder kinds** in `manifest.yaml`:
    - `kind: federal_grant` (existing)
    - `kind: private_grant` (existing)
    - `kind: private_fellowship` (existing)
    - `kind: accelerator` (existing)
    - `kind: federal_OT` (NEW — distinguishes ARPA-H ISO, NSF Tech Labs, Genesis from FAR-grant)
    - `kind: corporate_credit` (NEW — covers AWS, Google Cloud, NVIDIA Inception, etc.)

    - "Two-stage" rendering: pre-stage (LOI/concept/summary) is its own sub-template; full proposal is another.

13. **Update master mapping table** in `universal_template.md` to include rows for: ARIA, Wellcome Leap, Wellcome Discovery, CZI, NIH Bridge2AI, NIH PRIMED-AI, NSF-NIH SCH, NSF Convergence, Horizon Europe, ARPA-H PHO. (Currently only has heatmap entries for the 10 canonical funders.)

14. **Maintain inventory's F01-F11 column IDs** as backwards-compatible alias for new F-family (the Monday-board column IDs in `funding_opps_inventory.md` should continue to work).

15. **Capture funder-process distinction** (proactive vs. reactive) — Coefficient Giving primarily proactively reaches out; many philanthropies do too. Affects how applications should be styled.

## Cross-cutting observations

- **The 10 existing canonical funders (heilmeier, nih_r01, nsf_tech_labs, arpah_mission_office, doe_genesis, astera_residency, brains_accelerator, foresight_nodes, google_impact_challenge, yc_nonprofit) cover only 10/71 opportunities directly.** The remaining 61 need either:
  - **New canonical funder files** for the heavy ones (Wellcome Leap, Wellcome Trust Discovery, NIH Bridge2AI, NSF-NIH SCH, NSF Convergence, Horizon Europe Cluster 1, ARPA-H PHO, Convergent Research FRO, CZI, DRK, EA Funds, Coefficient Giving, Gates Grand Challenges, AWS Imagine, RWJF) — about 15 new funder files.
  - **Lightweight `kind: corporate_credit` and `kind: nonprofit_discount` templates** for the rest — ~30+ entries that all share a 5-field minimal schema.
  - **`kind: vc_investment` template** for VCs (7 entries: YC, IndieBio, BEV, Lux, DSV, TNS, Foresite Labs, 5050, Anthology).

- **Common omission across funders today**: the canonical schema does not yet model the **funder process** (proactive intermediated vs. reactive open-call) which materially changes how Cytognosis should engage. This is captured in F21 above.

- **Heaviest schema-stretch** comes from federal OT contracts (ARPA-H, NSF Tech Labs, DOE Genesis): research security, OT IP terms, phase-envelope budgets, mandatory feedback gates. These are likely to grow as the OT instrument expands.

- **Lightest schema-stretch** comes from corporate discount programs which need a 5-field minimal record. Building a separate `kind: nonprofit_discount` template avoids forcing them through the U/A scaffolding.

- **Gap most cited but absent**: a canonical "two-stage submission" model. Currently funder files implicitly assume a single full-proposal submission shape. Adding `submission_stages: [...]` as F13 and a render-time "stage-1 sub-template" pattern is the minimum viable extension.

- **Gap second-most cited**: **counterfactual / theory-of-change** narrative. Coefficient Giving, EA Funds, DRK, AstraZeneca CHANGE, RWJF all ask in different forms; promoting U21 (counterfactual) and U22 (theory of change) cleans up authoring across these funders.
