### 3.2 The Helix model in one picture (your canon, confirmed)

Two strands, one backbone. **Foundation = mission strand** (mission-locked charter, owns the platform IP, funded by grants/philanthropy/royalties, holds a governance majority over the PBC). **PBC = capital strand** (raises dilutive but non-controlling VC, manufactures/scales regulated products, receives a non-exclusive royalty-bearing license). Control comes from **charter + IP, not the cap table**. Mission-lock is triple-redundant (bylaws/IP terms + binding Patient Advocacy Council + independent annual review; any two can block).

The five arm's-length mechanisms your canon already specifies (this is your contract checklist for when the PBC forms):

1. Independent majority of Foundation directors with no PBC stake; anyone on both sides recuses from the vote and the negotiation.
2. Fair market value on everything crossing the interface (IP license, services), with independent valuation, documented before value moves.
3. Royalty structured as passive IP-license income (UBIT-excluded), not the Foundation running the business.
4. Reasonable compensation, benchmarked and approved by independent directors (this is exactly your $165K capped-PI logic).
5. Written instruments (master IP license, services agreements, PBC charter with mission-lock + governance majority) executed and counsel-reviewed before the first dollar or line of code crosses.

## 8. Glossary

- **Bifurcation:** the dated Board act (~month 36) after which continuous individual tracking data becomes proprietary (PBC), while everything before stays open (Foundation) forever.
- **PBC:** statutory Public Benefit Corporation (the for-profit strand). Not a "B-Corp" (that is a private certification; different thing).
- **Arm's-length:** structuring Foundation-PBC dealings so no impermissible benefit flows to insiders (avoids private inurement, excess-benefit, UBIT, loss of exemption).
- **Capped-PI pool:** total PI compensation <=$165K/yr across all funded sources, so concurrent grants do not double-pay.
- **$800K threshold:** the cumulative external funding at which your CEO/ED contract starts paying cash (the amendment in 3.6 #2 unlocks designated PI grant funds before that).
- **OpenBCI model:** open core + revenue from hardware sales + subscriptions + paid R&D partnerships. The canon's revenue thesis for Yar/sensors. Not ads.
- **CSP / USAP:** the open universal sensor-adapter protocol (two names, same thing; canonical name still to be decided).
