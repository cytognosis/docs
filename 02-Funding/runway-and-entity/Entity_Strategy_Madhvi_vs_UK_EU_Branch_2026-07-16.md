## 3. Recommendation and rationale

**Do not form a UK or EU legal entity at this time.** Rationale:

1. **Scope is narrow.** Of the 71 tracked opportunities plus the EU/UK peer set, only **Horizon Europe** structurally benefits from a Cytognosis-controlled EU entity — and only if the goal is to receive *direct EU funding* rather than self-fund participation. Wellcome Trust is fully solved by routing through Madhvi's Manchester appointment.
2. **Madhvi's existing affiliation already unlocks the two funders that need an EU/UK legal entity in the current pipeline** (Wellcome Trust via Manchester-as-administering-org; Horizon Europe partner-count via Manchester-as-one-of-3). Entity formation would duplicate what her university appointment already provides, at real cost (formation, registered agent, UK/EU tax filings, possible transfer-pricing and permanent-establishment exposure).
3. **Runway math doesn't support it right now.** Per the Oct 1 cliff and Career Transition runway framing, spending founder time and cash on UK/EU entity formation competes directly with higher-probability, faster-cash tracks (Coefficient, EA Funds, Biswas Fast Grants, ARPA-H).
4. **No Cytognosis-controlled UK entity is needed to keep the "UK office" narrative alive** — Madhvi as a geographic anchor and future Immunoverse v1 collaborator already carries that story for grant narratives; a legal entity is a 2027-scale decision, not a 2026 one.

**Trigger to revisit:** if a specific Horizon Europe call is identified where (a) mission fit is strong, (b) Manchester is unwilling or unable to be the EU-side administering beneficiary, and (c) the award size justifies entity overhead — re-open this memo and route to Duane + a CPA before proceeding.

## 4. Entity-formation mechanics and tax/legal specifics — FLAG FOR COUNSEL, NOT LEGAL ADVICE

The items below are **structural considerations to raise with Duane Valz (counsel) and a CPA**, not conclusions. Do not act on any of these without their sign-off:

- **Choice of UK vehicle** if ever pursued: options seen in comparable US-nonprofit-in-UK situations include (a) a UK-registered charity or CIO as a genuinely separate entity, (b) a UK branch/establishment of the US 501(c)(3), or (c) a "friends of" structure where a UK charity re-grants to the US parent. Each has different governance, reporting (Charity Commission), and funder-eligibility consequences — **counsel and CPA must assess which (if any) satisfies a specific funder's "UK legal entity" test.**
- **Tax exposure**: permanent-establishment risk in the UK if Cytognosis staff/activity there rises above a coordination threshold; UK corporation tax and Gift Aid mechanics differ from US 501(c)(3) treatment; cross-border grant flow-through (US 501(c)(3) receiving EU/UK funds, or vice versa) has withholding and equivalency-determination implications — **CPA input required.**
- **Madhvi's own status**: if her role expands beyond research collaborator (e.g., toward an employment, officer, or director role at a future UK entity), that changes her Cytognosis relationship from "collaborator" to something requiring its own contract, IP assignment, and conflict-of-interest review (parallel to how Duane structured Brad Ruzicka's McLean subaward and Jose Davila-Velderrain's Human Technopole subaward) — **flag for Duane before any formalization.**
- **IP and data**: any UK-administered award (e.g., a Wellcome Trust grant routed through Manchester) will have Manchester's IP policy govern outputs generated under that grant, not Cytognosis's — **have Duane review Manchester's standard PI/IP terms before committing Cytognosis co-investigator time or data to a Manchester-administered award.**
