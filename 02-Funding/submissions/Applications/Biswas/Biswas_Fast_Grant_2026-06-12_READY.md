## About Your Project

**Project Title**
> An open foundation model for dimensional psychiatric biotyping: a case study on schizophrenia and bipolar disorder

**One-Sentence Summary** (≤200 characters; publishable)
> We build an open foundation model that replaces categorical psychiatric labels with continuous biological axes, so care can target each person's biology, not a broad diagnosis.

**Question or Idea** (3–5 bullets, ~275 characters)
- Categorical psychiatric labels blur distinct biology, so people wait years for fitting care.
- Can one foundation model recover continuous Mood and Thought axes across diagnoses?
- Do those axes predict response to rapid-acting treatments (e.g., neuroplastogens)?

**What You'll Do** (3–5 bullets, ~275 characters)
- Harmonize multimodal data across both psychiatric indications (genomic + single-cell).
- Fine-tune AlphaGenome using pseudobulked single-cell data.
- Assess reproducibility of biotypes and treatment response predictions across cohorts (discovery: McLean, validation: MtSinai).

**Why Now / Why This Team** (2–3 bullets, ~200 characters)
- Personal genome sequencing < $1k.

- The first single-cell + WGS atlases of schizophrenia and bipolar (generated by our team).
