## About Your Project

**Project Title**
> Cytoverse-Neuro: an open foundation model for dimensional biotyping of bipolar disorder

**One-Sentence Summary** (≤200 characters; publishable)
> We train an open AI model on multimodal psychiatric data to recover continuous biological axes of bipolar disorder, so care can target biology instead of broad diagnostic labels.

**Question or Idea** (3–5 bullets, ~275 characters)
- Categorical psychiatric labels blur distinct biology, so people wait years for fitting care.
- Can an open foundation model recover continuous Mood, Thought, and Cognitive axes from multimodal data?
- Do those axes track BDNF/TrkB signaling and predict illness trajectory?

**What You'll Do** (3–5 bullets, ~275 characters)
- Harmonize multimodal bipolar data: the published McLean cohort, Mount Sinai validation, and public datasets.
- Fine-tune the open Cytoverse model to learn the continuous axes.
- Validate against held-out cohorts and BDNF/TrkB markers, then release the model and a benchmark.

**Why Now / Why This Team** (2–3 bullets, ~200 characters)
- 20 years of computational biology and single-cell AI; published bipolar biotyping.
- Methods and data are ready; compute is the missing piece.
- The founder lived the diagnostic delay this work targets.
