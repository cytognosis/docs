  

# Coefficient Giving – Application form for Capacity Building RFPs

  

Use this form to apply for funding for:

-   Work that builds capacity to address risks from transformative AI (see this program [page](https://www.openphilanthropy.org/funding-for-work-that-builds-capacity-to-address-risks-from-transformative-ai/) for more information).
    
-   Programs and events on global catastrophic risk, effective altruism, and other topics (see this program [page](https://www.openphilanthropy.org/funding-for-programs-and-events-on-global-catastrophic-risk-effective-altruism-and-other-topics/) for more information).
    

  

Applications are open until further notice and will be assessed on a rolling basis.

  

If we think your application is a better fit for open programs hosted by our technical AI or AI governance and policy [teams](https://www.openphilanthropy.org/focus/potential-risks-advanced-ai/), we may redirect your application to them. We’ll let you know if we do this.

  

If you have any questions about this application, you can email us at [cb-funding@coefficientgiving.org](mailto:cb-funding@coefficientgiving.org)

  

How to fill out this form

You can find a Google document version of the questions on this form [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1vqzq4H6AajPDjztujmird4d2EfLYz9qISdBeRNt_P6M/edit#heading=h.8hqpr9yx8mxw), in case you would prefer to draft your answers separately before copying them over. If you already have a funding request prepared in the form of a longer document, feel free to link to relevant sections of that document when filling this out, instead of typing in long-form responses.

  

The form should auto-save any information you enter; if you aren't able to complete it in one session, you should be able to fill in parts of it and return to it later, as long as you’re using the same device. You will receive a copy of your responses via email.

  

While we don't have hard word limits given the diversity of application sizes we receive, we generally encourage applicants to err on the side of being concise and preserving their time. If you're applying as an individual, we encourage you to aim to spend no more than two hours on this form.

  

Confidentiality

By default, we will share your application with staff inside Coefficient Giving (previously Open Philanthropy) as well as a select number of external advisors. If there's any information that you'd like to keep confidential to Coefficient Giving staff, please share it in the relevant form field below. You may also use this form to permit us to share your materials with other funders who we think may be interested in your project.

  

Timing

We expect to make most funding decisions in 3 months or less (assuming prompt responses to any follow-up questions we may have), and we may or may not be able to accommodate requests for greater time-sensitivity. Applicants asking for over $500K should expect a decision to take the full 3 months (or more, in particularly complex cases), and apply in advance accordingly. We’ll let you know as soon as we can if we anticipate a longer than 3-month decision timeline.

  

If we decide to offer funding, our grants team will follow-up to collect and confirm basic details to complete any required [due diligence](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1BunV9IXaJUy3NGql3JhNTpH91_Ghvryk/view). Grants are generally paid within one month of receiving the required information, though grants to certain types of entities (including international organizations, universities, and for-profit organizations) require additional due diligence and generally take longer. Our grants team is able to make payments urgently in some cases, but is generally unable to pay grants in less than 5 business days, even in urgent cases.

  

Minimum standards

If awarded funding, we expect organizations to have organizational policies that meet the minimum standards described in [this document](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1dHfWgypFZpIkhY3BJtQ2g0ToTR7QTR6Jkmdp8RRrgR4/edit). Before receiving funding, we’ll require you to confirm your intent to comply with these standards as soon as is practicable, and no later than by the next time you’d like to be considered for funding (this includes potential grant renewals).

  

If you are a project at an organization or a sub-organization at an organization, we require only the parts of the organization relevant to the funded activities to comply; please see our [FAQ](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1dHfWgypFZpIkhY3BJtQ2g0ToTR7QTR6Jkmdp8RRrgR4/edit#heading=h.g9mg8ajnjzip) for details. If you are a fiscally sponsored project, we only require your project to comply, not the entire organization that sponsors you.

  

Please note that we do not require the above if your organization or project is formally part of a university.

When sharing Google Docs or Sheets, please grant comment access to cb-funding@openphilanthropy.org to facilitate efficient follow-up communication.
