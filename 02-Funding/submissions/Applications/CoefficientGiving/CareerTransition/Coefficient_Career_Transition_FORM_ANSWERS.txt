PROPOSAL (250 words max)

I am a senior computational neuroscientist (20 years across MIT, the Broad Institute, insitro, and GenBio AI) transitioning full-time into technical AI safety. This six-month grant funds that transition: a period to build core safety skills (control evaluations, activation monitoring, and calibrated-uncertainty methods) and to ship a working open-source artifact, using my nonprofit, Cytognosis Foundation, as the vehicle.

The artifact has two pillars. First, verifiability: interpretable-by-design representations (Cytoverse) that make a model's outputs falsifiable, so a claim can be checked against transparent biological axes instead of opaque embeddings. Second, deployment control: Cytoplex, an on-device layer that blocks unverified clinical claims, enforces calibrated uncertainty, refuses out-of-distribution inputs, and resists manipulative reward optimization. It ships with a control-evaluation suite (currently 93 passing safety tests).

I am entering through mental health because it is the highest-stakes near-term deployment: millions now use cloud LLMs as ad-hoc therapists, with no verifiability and no deployment control, and the cognitive data they generate is immutable. The same primitives generalize to any high-stakes personal-data AI.

Why me, why now: I bring rare signal from inside this deployment domain, a published single-cell record in psychiatry, and lived experience of the harm; clinical co-lead Brad Ruzicka, MD/PhD (McLean), anchors validation. The methods and data are ready; the missing input is six months of focus. By month six I will have shipped the open-source verifiability-and-control layer, completed the skill transition, and established a permanent technical AI-safety track I will continue regardless of where I sit.


PERSONAL STATEMENT (250 words max)

My commitment to technical AI safety is the convergence of a 20-year scientific arc with a lifelong personal one, and that is why I can do this work well.

I was born with a TBX1-associated genetic variant, an ultra-rare condition carrying one of the strongest known risk factors for the neuropsychiatric disorders I would spend my career mapping. I went undiagnosed for 37 years, through three degrees and a research career across MIT, the Broad Institute, insitro, and GenBio AI, because variable presentation and categorical psychiatric thinking made the biology invisible. I resolved my own case through computational genomics, the same methods I had spent two decades building to map others' brains. That gives me a dual vantage: a researcher's grasp of what these systems must do, and a patient's grasp of what their failure costs.

I co-led, as co-first author, the first multi-cohort single-cell atlas of schizophrenia (Science, 2024), and contributed to the first single-cell study of Alzheimer's disease (Nature, 2019). I built ACTIONet, an open-source framework used across the psychiatric-genomics consortia (PsychENCODE, PsychAD, ROSMAP). I have developed this thesis since 2019; it is now the verifiability-and-control work above.

I am making this pivot because the highest-stakes deployment of AI is arriving in exactly the domain where I bring rare signal, and the defensive tools, verifiability and deployment control, do not yet exist. Six months of focus converts twenty years of neuroscience and machine learning into a permanent technical AI-safety track.

OTHER COMMENTS

The choice to build Cytognosis as a 501(c)(3) rather than a venture-backed for-profit is load-bearing. A for-profit framework would have made capitalization easier, but the 23andMe asset-liquidation pattern shows that for-profit custody of immutable biological data fails the moment fiduciary pressure overrides consent, and brain connectomes and behavioral exposomes are even less recoverable than genomes. Operating impact-first is the institutional analogue of a zero-custody data architecture: structural integrity, not stated good intent. This transition grant funds the personal runway that makes that choice operationally possible during the bootstrap phase.

This is the first funding application in which I have included my TBX1 background. I am sharing it because I believe Coefficient Giving and BlueDot will evaluate the case better with it than without it, and because it is why, when this transition concludes, the work will not stop.
