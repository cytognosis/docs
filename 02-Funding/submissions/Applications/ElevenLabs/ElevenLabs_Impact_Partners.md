**Organization:** Cytognosis Foundation — 501(c)(3) nonprofit (Delaware; EIN 39-4383634; Daly City, CA), building open-source tools for precision mental health.
**Category:** Nonprofit (healthcare) — access to ElevenLabs technology for an internal project (Yar).

**Mission.** Our founder spent 37 years misdiagnosed across ten specialties, finding answers only through self-directed genomic analysis. That drives our mission: no one should wait decades for answers — and the tools that make mental health legible should be open and private by default.

**What we're building — Yar.** Yar is a private, **local-first** AI companion for neurodivergent adults (ADHD, autism, mood and anxiety). Everything sensitive stays on the user's device: their raw microphone audio and the **vocal-biomarker emotion sensing** (HuBERT + openSMILE — pitch variability, speech rate, jitter, shimmer, tracking cognitive load and emotional state over time) run **entirely on-device** and are never transmitted. This is enforced by our **Cytoplex**, a safety-and-authority layer that gates every model call and external write. On-device Gemma 4 handles conversation; a working mobile voice loop is already shipped. The payoff for users is longitudinal insight into their own patterns — including objective before/after measures when they try a new medication, therapy, or routine.

Yar is the first deployment of **Cytonome**, the privacy-first "navigator" of our open cellular-intelligence platform (a *GPS for Health*), built on a **universal sensor protocol (the Cytonome Sensor Protocol — an open "MCP for sensors")** where each modality — voice, wearables (Oura, smartwatches), text, and our future brain sensors — is a pluggable sensor the user can connect or disconnect at will. **Voice is our first sensor — and the voice Yar speaks back with is where ElevenLabs comes in.** Yar's companion is also an **adaptive persona** (coach, buddy, guardian, parent, partner, …) that auto-tunes to each user rather than forcing an overwhelmed brain to configure it — and each persona carries its own voice. For a neurodivergent person in a vulnerable moment, the warmth and naturalness of the companion's voice directly determine whether they can build trust with it.

**Why ElevenLabs, specifically.** Yar's hard constraint is that nothing sensitive leaves the device. Until recently, a high-quality companion voice meant a cloud round-trip — incompatible with our architecture. **ElevenLabs' On-Device TTS changes that:** it is the first best-in-class voice that can run fully offline, so even the companion's *spoken output* stays on-device, completing a 100% local loop (Gemma understands → HuBERT senses → ElevenLabs speaks), all under Cytoplex. Yar is a natural flagship for what on-device voice AI is *for*: privacy-first mental-health support for people who most need to trust that they aren't being surveilled.

**How we'd like to partner:**

1. **On-Device TTS (early access) — the core ask.** Include Cytognosis in the On-Device TTS early-access program (at nonprofit terms). Its offline operation, **70+ languages**, and edge optimization (CPU/NPU/ARM) are exactly what Yar requires — a natural, trustworthy companion voice in users' native languages, with no audio ever leaving the device. Many of the people we serve are in communities with few mental-health professionals and languages underserved by existing tools; on-device multilingual voice is how Yar reaches them.

2. **Voice Design for Yar's companion personas.** Yar supports multiple **personas** (coach, buddy, guardian, parent, partner, …) that the app adapts to each user automatically. We'd use ElevenLabs Voice Design — without recording a real person — to give each persona its own warm, calm, **non-clinical** voice (a trusted companion, not a customer-service bot or a medical authority), designed once in the cloud and then **deployed on-device** via the On-Device TTS model, so the voice matches the relationship the user needs in the moment.

3. **A HIPAA-compliant pathway for clinical validation (Phase 3+).** As we move toward IRB-approved studies validating Yar's longitudinal biomarker tracking against gold-standard neuropsychological assessments, we'll need HIPAA-compliant voice processing. ElevenLabs' **BAA + VPC / on-premise** options (with Zero-Retention Mode) are the most mature in voice AI; in that consented, compliant setting, ElevenLabs' **Audio Isolation** could also clean study recordings to improve analysis accuracy (this is the only context where we'd process user audio off-device, under explicit consent and a BAA).

**Why this fits Impact Partners.** Cytognosis is a healthcare nonprofit; all of our software is open-source (Apache 2.0), and our sensor architecture lets *any* organization build a plugin for Cytonome. A partnership would put ElevenLabs' on-device voice technology directly in the hands of underserved neurodivergent communities worldwide — in their own languages, on their own devices, under their own control. We believe voice AI should be accessible to everyone, and Yar is a chance to prove it where privacy matters most.
