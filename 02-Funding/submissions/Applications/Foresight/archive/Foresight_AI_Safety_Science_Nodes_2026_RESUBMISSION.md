### What are you trying to do? (max 350 characters)

We are building a zero-trust, edge-native framework that maps human cognitive and neural states across multi-modal streams (conversation, wearables, EEG/fNIRS) and bridges them to single-cell molecular connectomics. The result is a decentralized, post-quantum, open-source substrate for tracking biological intelligence and securing safe BCIs.

### How is today's approach limited? What is new in your approach, and what does success look like?

#### Today's Limits — A Centralization Crisis, Not a Clinical One

The neuro-AI landscape is racing toward a future where corporate and state actors centrally own the highest-fidelity map of human cognition ever assembled — and we have no defensive primitives in place.

Three structural failures define today's landscape:

1. **The capability/defense gap has inverted.** Recent clinical validation (Nan et al., May 2026) shows N-of-1 ML models can decode individual psychological states with up to 95% accuracy (Cohen's *d* = −0.89 for depression alleviation under AI-guided lifestyle optimization). Population-scale genomic modeling now resolves psychiatric variance into a five-factor structure across 14 disorders, explaining approximately 66% of disorder co-occurrence variance (Grotzinger et al., *Nature*, 2025), and our own *Science* 2024 schizophrenia atlas resolves continuous disease axes within a single DSM label. The capability to map, predict, and behaviorally steer a human being is here. The defensive open-source infrastructure to prevent its capture is not.

2. **Centralized custody is the wrong primitive.** The May 2025 23andMe bankruptcy and consent crisis turned millions of users' immutable genomic profiles into liquidatable assets the moment shareholder duties shifted. Standard federated learning does not solve this — the bulk aggregator remains a single point of failure vulnerable to gradient inversion, institutional coercion, and re-consent under restructuring. Cognitive connectomes are even less recoverable than genomes: you can change a password, you cannot change your brain.

3. **Quantum + behavioral timelines are a "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" target.** State and non-state actors are actively capturing encrypted high-fidelity health time-series today, intending to decrypt them with cryptanalytically relevant quantum computers within the decade. Multi-modal neural and behavioral data from individuals' most psychologically vulnerable moments is prime HNDL material.

4. **The Macro-to-Micro mapping does not exist as a public good.** No open-source system connects real-time macro-behavior (wearables, conversation) to meso-scale connectomics (EEG/fNIRS) to micro-scale single-cell molecular dimensions. Without this Rosetta Stone, safe BCI alignment and whole-brain emulation primitives are being designed in the dark — and whoever builds the closed version first owns the substrate.

#### What's New

Cytognosis is building a "GPS for Health" platform with three named layers. The **Cytoverse** (the Map) anchors the platform in validated molecular coordinates derived from our published single-cell atlases. The **Cytoscope** (the Sensor) handles longitudinal clinical measurement. The **Cytonome** (the Navigator) is the privacy-first, edge-AI layer that translates continuous multi-modal streams into actionable health coordinates on the individual's own device. This proposal funds the Cytonome safety substrate, which maps brain variation across three complementary scales:

- **Micro** — genotype and single-cell transcriptomics (PsychENCODE 388 paired WGS + scRNA, ROSMAP, NeuroBioBank 10K+ genomes) — the molecular-neuroscience track record described above
- **Meso** — brain connectomics via multi-resolution graph neural networks over spatio-temporal fMRI/EEG/fNIRS at 1M-node, whole-brain scale
- **Macro** — behavioral and wearable physiology (HRV, sleep architecture, prefrontal hemodynamics, conversational state) — the edge-AI Navigator layer Foresight funds

Our scientific credibility at the Micro and Meso scales is established by the publication record above. The Foresight project funds the missing piece: the **decentralized, edge-native Macro layer of Cytonome** that connects this stack to individual people under a regime of total data sovereignty. Foresight's interest in safe BCI and whole-brain emulation (WBE) maps directly onto Cytonome: it is the privacy-first substrate any safe BCI or WBE architecture will need to interface with at the behavioral edge.

Three architectural innovations differentiate us:

1. **Edge-native perception layer.** Purpose-built compact language models (Gemma 4 family, ≤3B parameters) fine-tuned with LoRA + DPO, distilled and quantized for on-device inference via LiteRT and Flutter. Conversational state quantification, wearable signal fusion, and personalized baseline learning happen entirely on the user's device. No cloud egress of raw signals — ever.

2. **Sovereign storage primitive (Solid pods).** All raw multi-modal streams are written into the user's cryptographically owned [Solid](https://solidproject.org/TR) personal data pod. The Cytognosis Foundation operates as a **zero-custody entity**: we are architecturally incapable of selling, pooling, or being legally compelled to surrender data we never possess. This is the structural answer to 23andMe.

3. **Post-Quantum Cryptographic (PQC) edge protocol.** All model-derived embeddings, federated gradients, and inter-pod traffic are encapsulated with NIST-approved PQC primitives (ML-KEM, ML-DSA). Multi-modal behavioral and connectomic time-series remain resilient against future quantum cryptanalysis from day one.

```
 [ wearables: Oura · Muse S Athena EEG/fNIRS · Emotiv ]
                          │
                          ▼
       [ on-device perception: Gemma 4 via LiteRT ]
                          │
                          ▼
  [ sovereign storage: user-owned Solid personal data pod ]
                          │
                          ▼
[ peer-to-peer federated learning · PQC-encapsulated gradients ]
```

Every component — model weights, training code, federated learning protocol, PQC integration, Solid pod schemas, evaluation benchmarks — is released as a public good under Apache 2.0 (code/models) and CC BY 4.0 (documentation/non-personal data). Not "open weights." **Truly open**: full transparency into data, code, weights, and evaluation.

### If there are risks, is it possible to differentially advance safety-enhancing aspects of the technology first?

Yes, and we have organized the entire 18 months around exactly this principle. We treat Cytonome as a **defensive-first technology stack**, with all safety-enhancing primitives built, audited, and open-sourced before any high-fidelity neural sensor stream is ingested or any external participant is enrolled.

We address risk across four nested layers, in this order:

#### 1. Cognitive Liberty & Defensive Asymmetry (Months 1–6)
The dominant long-term risk is asymmetric: centralized actors (corporate, state, or both) deploy invasive neuro-profiling capabilities at scale before the open-source community has a verifiable defensive equivalent. We differentially advance the defensive infrastructure first by open-sourcing the layered privacy protocol, zero-custody data specification, PQC edge module, and local verification toolkits **in Months 1–6, before any sensor or participant data touches the system**. The result: any researcher or organization can audit every privacy claim and adopt the same architecture into their own work.

#### 2. Sovereign Ingestion Safety (Architectural, Months 1–18)
Solid pod integration means Cytognosis Foundation holds zero raw data, ever. We eliminate the 23andMe-class risk where institutional restructuring rewrites user consent or liquidates immutable biological profiles. The foundation is structurally incapable of becoming the honeypot.

#### 3. Post-Quantum Resilience (Months 1–6, ahead of all data work)
Because brain connectomes and behavioral exposomes are immutable, they are uniquely high-value HNDL targets. We integrate NIST-standardized PQC (ML-KEM, ML-DSA) into the edge communication and federated learning protocols from day one, not as a v2 retrofit. The PQC reference implementation ships open-source under Apache 2.0 and is reusable by any Foresight Node working with sensitive personal time-series.

#### 4. Edge-Native Clinical "Duty-to-Warn" Override (Months 1–6, hardcoded before deployment)
General-purpose centralized LLMs are being adopted by millions as ad-hoc therapists. Documented cases show these models reinforce harmful feedback loops and have been linked to amplifying suicidal ideation, while their architectures cannot safely execute a clinical "duty to warn" without creating a dystopian centralized surveillance vector. We resolve this paradox with a cryptographic, **on-device** override module: when locally computed crisis markers cross a threshold, the stack bypasses the conversational agent and surfaces localized emergency resources (988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, Crisis Text Line) and/or alerts a pre-designated human proxy via cryptographic envelope — **without ever exporting raw psychological or neural signals**.

#### Safety-First Development Order

- **Months 1–6:** Layered privacy protocol, PQC primitives, Solid pod schemas, edge-native duty-to-warn module, bias audit framework, open protocol specification, IRB submission. **All safety systems deployed, externally audited, and open-sourced before any participant-facing system exists.**
- **Months 7–12:** Clinical validation against gold-standard assessments under the already-audited safety stack.
- **Months 13–18:** Pilot deployment only after all Month-6 and Month-12 safety gates pass.

This resubmission reflects four substantive changes: (1) we have reframed Neuroverse from a clinical/digital-health framing to its true identity — a decentralized, post-quantum substrate for cognitive sovereignty and safe BCI/WBE alignment, which is what we were always building but did not surface clearly; (2) we have integrated Solid personal data pods as a zero-custody storage primitive (architectural answer to the 23andMe consent crisis); (3) we have integrated NIST-standardized post-quantum cryptography from day one rather than as a v2 retrofit, in response to "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" risk against immutable biological data; (4) we have made the inter-Node integration commitment concrete — we intend to actively seek pairing with projects on private or decentralized AI infrastructure and on BCI hardware decoding, where the Cytonome edge-behavioral layer can serve as a shared privacy substrate, rather than operating as a standalone team.
