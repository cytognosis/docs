**One-line:** A GPS for brain health — a noninvasive sensor + multiscale map that detects the drift toward cognitive and sensory decline years before symptoms, and guides personalized intervention.

### The problem (cognitive & sensory aging)
Age-related cognitive and sensory decline is detected far too late — typically once function is already lost and pathology is largely irreversible. Alzheimer's pathology, for example, begins decades before memory symptoms. The field lacks a **continuous, individual-level readout** of brain state that works outside the clinic and spans the molecular roots to the behavioral surface, so we cannot see decline beginning, stratify who is on which trajectory, or intervene while change is still reversible. Population averages fail because two people with identical clinical scores can be on completely different biological paths.

### The solution / technology
Cytognosis is building a three-layer platform:
- **Cytoverse** — an open, multimodal, multiscale *map* of brain/biological state (micro: molecular/cellular/genomic; meso: connectomic/EEG/fMRI; macro: behavior/physiology), built with foundation models, that replaces categorical labels with continuous, trackable coordinates.
- **Cytoscope** — a noninvasive, adaptive **fNIRS-like spectroscopy headset** (developed with a collaborator) that reads brain activity at fine resolution and depth, triangulating an individual's position on the map in real time — usable longitudinally, outside the clinic.
- **Cytonome** — a privacy-first, on-device AI navigator that turns those coordinates into mechanistic, personalized guidance (what to avoid, how to reverse, how to cope).

For this track, the platform enables **early detection and interception of cognitive and sensory decline**: catching the trajectory toward MCI/dementia and sensory aging years early, stratifying by mechanism, and personalizing intervention — i.e., extending cognitive **healthspan**, not just diagnosing disease.

### Why it fits "Aging Reimagined" + the Cognitive & Sensory track
Functional resilience in aging depends on catching decline before it becomes loss. Our platform is purpose-built for **continuous, individual-level, multiscale monitoring** — the missing measurement layer for proactive cognitive and sensory health. It is mechanism-grounded (so guidance is causal, not correlational) and designed for real-world, longitudinal use.
