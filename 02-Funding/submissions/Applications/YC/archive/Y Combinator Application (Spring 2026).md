The health intelligence market has four categories, each proving demand while exposing critical gaps:

**CGMs (Dexcom $4.66B revenue, Abbott Libre $6B+, ZOE $100-200M):** Proved consumers want continuous data. But these players provide strictly univariate data, with one molecule (glucose).

**Blood panels (Function Health $2.5B valuation, 200K subscribers; Molecular You $5M Series A):** Proved consumers want multivariate biomarker data. But episodic, quarterly snapshots of rapidly changing systems. Immune responses fluctuate in minutes and hours, not months.

**Liquid biopsy (Natera $1.5B revenue, Exact Sciences $2.76B, Guardant $980M):** These players provide a gold standard for depth of disease insight. But their solutions are reactive and diagnostic, testing for the presence of disease. Cytonome will instead recommend personalized health interventions based on individual health trajectories.

**What we understand that they don't:** The market is fragmented along a 2x2 matrix of temporal resolution (continuous vs. episodic) and marker depth (univariate vs. multivariate). Every major player occupies one quadrant. The continuous AND multivariate quadrant is empty. That is where Cytognosis sits.

Disease is not a static presence of a biomarker in the blood. It is a dynamic cellular conversation that changes in minutes. We continuously monitor cellular states using programmable biosensors, tracking how each individual's unique neural and immune networks shift in real time. Instead of "inflammation markers elevated," we identify which cellular circuits are dysregulated, why, and what to do about it. The distinction is real-time molecular navigation vs. periodic satellite photos.

### Please describe the scientific basis for your product. How does it work?

The scientific basis rests on three converging advances:

**1\. Disease axes are real and discoverable.** Our Science 2024 publication identified distinct continuous "disease axes" in schizophrenia affecting specific neuronal subtypes, proving that one DSM diagnosis encompasses multiple biological diseases. This builds on the latest GWAS, which identified shared genetic signatures across 14 psychiatric disorders (Grotzinger et al., Science, 2025), confirming that categorical diagnoses mask biological heterogeneity. We are not proposing theoretical frameworks; we have molecular maps.

**2\. Cross-modal imputation works.** Recent studies demonstrate that clinically relevant but hard-to-obtain readouts (e.g., tau-PET in Alzheimer's disease) can be imputed from simpler, noninvasive measurements when paired training data are available (Nature Communications, 2025). Our multimodal foundation models extend this principle: training on paired datasets in which multiple modalities are measured on the same patients, and then predicting missing modalities from available ones. This bridges the gap between what our sensors can measure and what clinicians need to see.

**3\. Continuous molecular sensing is technically feasible.** Affinity-based continuous monitoring technologies have demonstrated real-time tracking beyond glucose, with prototypes rapidly expanding the set of continuously measurable biomarkers (Nature Communications, 2024). The Caltech/FAS molecular monitoring FRO proposal (now funded by Convergent Research) validates the same technical approach we plan for Cytoscope: CMOS-integrated biosensors with programmable detection across 100+ analytes.

**How it works together:** Cytoverse uses extensive molecular data to establish continuous reference health coordinates, replacing categorical disease labels with precise biological positions. Cytoscope continuously measures molecular signals that triangulate an individual's position within this coordinate system. Cytonome's interface converts deviations in these coordinates into personalized preventive interventions to get back on the health track. The same architecture that lets Google Maps route you through traffic lets Cytognosis route you away from disease.
