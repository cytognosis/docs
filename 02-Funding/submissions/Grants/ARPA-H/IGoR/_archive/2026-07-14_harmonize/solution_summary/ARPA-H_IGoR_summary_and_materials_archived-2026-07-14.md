## First-draft Solution Summary (v0 — Heilmeier-structured)

**Title:** *Cytoverse-IGoR: An AI engine for mechanistic, multi-scale, continuously-validated disease models — starting with the brain.*

**1. What are you trying to do?** Build an AI system that generates, tests, and continuously revises **mechanistic, multi-scale models of human disease** — linking molecular/cellular, connectomic, and behavioral scales into causal models whose predictions are automatically checked against new data, replacing static, irreproducible, single-scale studies.

**2. How is it done today, and what are the limits?** Disease models today are siloed by scale and discipline, published in non-reproducible pieces, and rarely mechanistic or predictive at the individual level. Reproducibility is poor; integration across scales is manual and slow.

**3. What's new in our approach?** A foundation-model architecture (Cytoverse) trained end-to-end across scales with cross-resolution attention, modeling disease as movement in a residual ("delta-from-healthy") space, and a closed validation loop that treats every new dataset as an automatic test of the model's mechanistic predictions. Built open and versioned for reproducibility by construction.

**4. Who cares? If you succeed, what difference does it make?** A reproducible, mechanistic, multi-scale disease model — first for neuropsychiatric/neurodegenerative disease — lets researchers and clinicians predict trajectories, stratify mechanism, and intervene proactively; it is reusable public infrastructure for all of biomedical research.
