## Section 1: Level of Effort and Labor

### Overview

PsychIGoR fields approximately 23 funded full-time equivalents (FTEs) per year across eight performing organizations. Total direct labor over 60 months is approximately $18.8M, representing 43.8% of the ARPA-H request, with approximately 130,000 funded labor hours at a blended fully-loaded rate of approximately $145/hour.

**Answering ARPA-H's stated concern about junior staffing:** Appendix C.3 of the solicitation states that proposers are discouraged from staffing efforts with junior personnel to appear more cost-effective. PsychIGoR answers this directly. Every performing organization contributes substantive senior expertise: four PhD faculty (Grama, Carpenter, Tegtmeyer, Ruzicka) each contribute 2–3 person-months per year for scientific strategy, laboratory oversight, and translational framing. The computational and engineering execution is carried by senior postdoctoral researchers, research scientists, and software engineers (all at 1.0 FTE) individually named where confirmed and drawn from the top of the labor-market range for computational biology and ML engineering. SIFT contributes 24,204 labor hours of senior standards-engineering depth (70% Bryce / 30% Goldman and engineers). Cytognosis contributes approximately 5 ML/comp-bio FTE plus PM and Architect, including a Software and Systems Architect (a planned Cytognosis hire; Elham Jebalbarezi Sarbijan (IPAI) interim) at 1.0 FTE and a named PM (Patricia Purcell) at approximately 400 hours per year. PhD students at Purdue (two funded at 1.0 FTE each) are appropriate for their role: executing bench protocols under faculty supervision, not substituting for senior scientific decision-making.
