# Appendix B: Solution Summary Format and Instructions (Amendment 01)

> [!NOTE]

> **Governs:** The cover template, page limits, formatting, and six required sections of the Solution Summary (the non-gated first-round submission).

## At a glance (BLUF)

Appendix B is the fill-in template and rulebook for the **Solution Summary**, the short first-round document due **06/25/2026, 12:00 PM ET**. **Sections 1 through 4 are capped at 5 pages total**; the cover/profile tables, the Basis of Estimate, and References Cited do not count. This is the **Amendment 01** version and is markedly more concise than the original, but the substance (concept, innovation, work, team, BOE, references) is unchanged.

## Key facts

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Submission** | Solution Summary (first round, **non-gated**: ARPA-H returns "encouraged" or "discouraged" feedback only) |
| **Due** | `06/25/2026, 12:00 PM ET` (per the ISO Summary Information section) |
| **Page limit** | **5 pages total for Sections 1 to 4** |

| **Guidance text** | Blue-font content recommendations **must be deleted before submitting** |

## Structure / contents

- **Cover / profile tables** (not counted)
  - Opening title, solution summary title, prime org name and type (For-Profit / Non-profit / Academia)
  - SAM.gov UEI (not required at this stage if unregistered), contact state, key personnel, technical and administrative POCs
  - Estimated project duration, animal/human subjects flags, **total funds requested (all phases)**, resource sharing, place(s) of performance
  - Sub-awardee and team-member table (organization name, technical POC, org type; add rows as needed)
- **Section 1, Concept Summary:** the concept with minimal jargon, mapped to the ISO interest areas
- **Section 2, Innovation and Impact:** outcomes/problems; disruptiveness versus state of the art; a **quantitative metrics comparison table** (challenge progression, SOTA baseline, aggressive per-year metrics, summary outcomes)
- **Section 3, Proposed Work:** deliverables, interim milestones, technical approach, alternatives considered, supporting data/citations, adoption challenges, and the **technical risk versus transformative impact** balance
- **Section 4, Team Organization and Capabilities:** org roles and responsibilities; each key member's name, position, institution, and a 1 to 2 sentence skills statement
- **Section 5, Basis of Estimate (no page limit):** all costs for the whole team via the cost-category table (direct labor, labor hours, subcontracts/consultants, materials, equipment, travel, other direct, indirect, profit, total all phases, resource sharing) plus taxpayer value proposition
- **Section 6, References Cited (no page limit):** first author, title, source, year, volume/pages, and a persistent identifier (DOI or PubMed ID)

## What it requires of a proposer

- **Address all ISO interest areas** in the concept, written for a non-specialist reader.
- **Provide a quantitative comparison table** in Section 2; reviewers expect numbers, not adjectives.
- **Balance technical risk against transformative impact** explicitly in Section 3; ARPA-H expects substantial risk.
- **Name every key team member** with institution and a one to two sentence capability statement.
- **Supply a full-team Basis of Estimate** covering all phases, even though it is outside the page limit.
- **Strip all blue guidance text** and confirm formatting before export.

## Watch-outs

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The **5-page limit applies only to Sections 1 to 4**. Putting substantive technical argument into Section 5 or 6 to dodge the limit is a misuse reviewers will notice; keep cost and references in their own sections.

> [!WARNING]
> Leaving any **blue-font recommendation text** in the submitted file is an avoidable error. Delete it all before generating the PDF.
