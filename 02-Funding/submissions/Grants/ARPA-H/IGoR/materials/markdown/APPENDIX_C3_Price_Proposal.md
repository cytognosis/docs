# Appendix C.3: Price Proposal

> [!NOTE]
> **Type:** DOCX · **Source file:** `materials/APPENDIX+C.3_Price+Proposal_IGoR.docx` · **Extracted text:** `materials/extracted-text/APPENDIX_C.3_Price_Proposal_IGoR.txt`
> **Governs:** The cost volume of the proposal; must cover all team members and cross-walk to TDD tasks and milestones.

## At a glance (BLUF)

Appendix C.3 is the cost narrative template that accompanies the separate Price Proposal Workbook (C.4). It has no page limit and does not count against the 40-page cap. Every line item must be supported by method documentation, and all team members (prime, sub, consultant) must be included. Equipment items over $10,000 require backup documentation.

## Key facts

| Item | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Page limit** | None; does not count against the 40-page cap |
| **Phase breakdown** | Prices required separately for Phase I, Phase II, and Phase III |
| **Total price** | Inclusive of all phases and any resource sharing |
| **Equipment backup threshold** | Items exceeding $10,000 total require backup documentation (quotes, past POs, invoices) |
| **Coverage** | Must include prime, sub-awardees, and consultants |
| **Resource sharing** | Government and performer percentages must sum to total price |

## Structure / contents

Cover page table:
- Proposal title
- Prime organization name
- SAM.gov UEI number
- Proposer's internal reference number (if any)
- Total price (all phases and resource sharing)
  - Phase I subtotal
  - Phase II subtotal
  - Phase III subtotal
- Resource sharing split: Government percentage/amount and Performer percentage/amount

Table of contents:
1. Level of Effort and Labor
2. Equipment Purchases
3. Travel
4. Other Direct Costs (ODCs)
5. Resource Sharing

Narrative sections:

- **Level of Effort and Labor**, labor categories, fully burdened rates, estimated hours per task
- **Equipment Purchases**, itemized list with quantities, unit prices, proposed vendors, and basis of estimate; backup documentation required for items over $10,000
- **Travel**, per-trip justification explaining necessity and linkage to specific tasks or milestones
- **Other Direct Costs (ODCs)**, itemized list (e.g., graduate student tuition, computer maintenance, user fees) with explanations of contribution to the project
- **Resource Sharing**, source, nature, and amounts; percentages and nature of contribution (cash or in-kind)

## What it requires of a proposer

- **Break out total price by Phase** (Phase I, II, III) on the cover table.
- **Include all team members:** prime, sub-awardees, and consultants in every cost category.
- **Document estimation methods** for every line item; include supporting documentation as necessary.
- **Itemize labor** by category with fully burdened rates and estimated hours per task.
- **Itemize equipment** with vendor information, unit price, quantities, and basis of estimate. Attach backup documentation (quotes, past purchase orders, invoices) for any item exceeding $10,000 total.
- **Justify each trip** by linking it to specific tasks or milestones from the TDD.
- **List all ODCs** individually with an explanation of how each contributes to the project.
- **Detail resource sharing** if proposed: include percentages, nature (cash vs. in-kind), and amounts for both government and performer. Government and performer shares must sum to the total price.
- **Cross-walk cost details to TDD tasks and milestones.** The type and number of labor hours, materials, and other costs should be traceable to specific TDD tasks.

## Watch-outs

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Any equipment item exceeding $10,000 total must include backup documentation. Missing documentation can delay negotiations.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Government and performer resource-sharing amounts must sum to the total proposed price. Any arithmetic inconsistency is a conforming-submission risk.

> [!WARNING]
> All team members (prime, sub, consultant) must have costs detailed in the proposal. Costs for any team member not substantiated in C.3 will be questioned during negotiation.

> [!WARNING]
> ARPA-H discourages low-risk staffing solely to reduce budget. "Proposers are discouraged from proposing low-risk ideas with minimum uncertainty or to staff the proposed effort with junior personnel to be more appealing from a budget perspective."

> [!CAUTION]
> Travel costs are scrutinized individually. Each trip must be justified against a specific task or milestone from the TDD, not stated in aggregate.

> [!CAUTION]
> The Price Proposal Workbook (Appendix C.4, an .xlsx file) is a separate deliverable from this narrative. Both must be submitted; C.3 provides the written narrative and C.4 the structured spreadsheet.
