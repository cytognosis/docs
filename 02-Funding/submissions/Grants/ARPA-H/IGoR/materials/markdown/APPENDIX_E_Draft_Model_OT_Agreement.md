# Appendix E: Draft Model Other Transaction (OT) Agreement (Amendment 01)

> [!NOTE]
> **Type:** DOCX · **Source file:** `materials/APPENDIX+E_Draft+Model+OT+Agreement_IGoR_Amendment+01.docx` · **Extracted text:** `materials/extracted-text/APPENDIX_E_Draft_Model_OT_Agreement_IGoR_Amendment_01.txt`
> **Governs:** The terms and conditions of the award instrument itself: scope, term, payment, intellectual property, security, and special terms. Proposers submit this with redlines as part of the full proposal.

## At a glance (BLUF)

Appendix E is the **model contract** ARPA-H will execute with each funded team, written as a federal **Other Transaction** under **42 U.S.C. 290c(g)(1)(D)**. It runs **22 Articles plus 4 Attachments** and is **fixed-price by milestone**, with the Performer generally **retaining title to inventions** while the Government keeps a **Government Purpose license**. The full proposal must include this document **with the proposer's redlines**; silence is treated as acceptance of the terms.

| **Term** | **60 months** from the Effective Date (date of last signature) |

## Structure / contents

- **Cover page:** agreement number `AY2AX000XXX`, authority, total and per-phase amounts (placeholders), signature blocks
- **Table of Contents**
- **Article I, Scope:** background, perceived-benefit narrative prompts, **Definitions**, scope of project
- **Article II, Term:** 60 months; termination by either party; extension by mutual agreement
- **Article III, Management:** Performer runs the project; modifications via the AO; **3-year records retention**
- **Article IV, Points of Contact:** Government (AO, PM) and Performer POC blocks
- **Article V, Obligation and Payment:** SAM and PMS registration; milestone reports; 14-day acceptance/rejection; payment justification (<= 1,000 characters, no PII)
- **Article VI, Disputes:** good-faith resolution; escalation to HCA then Deputy Director; **damages limited to obligated funds**, no consequential damages
- **Article VII, Intellectual Property Rights:** patent rights (Performer retains title), invention disclosure (4 months), **trade-secret protection option** (notify AO within 8 months), iEdison reporting, data rights (Government Purpose Rights on reports), data marking legends
- **Article VIII, Foreign Access to IP:** controls on transferring program IP to foreign entities; 45-day prior notice; substantial U.S. manufacture preference
- **Article IX, Tangible Property:** title and disposition rules; **$10,000** threshold
- **Article X, Public Release:** **10-day prior notice** to COMPASS (media@arpa-h.gov) and the PM before any release or publication; required funding-acknowledgment statement
- **Articles XI to XVI:** Civil Rights (HHS Form 690); Security (**NIST SP 800-171** for CUI); Applicable Law (U.S. federal); Order of Precedence; Execution; Survival
- **Article XVII, Telecom prohibition:** Section 889 ban on covered Chinese telecom/surveillance equipment
- **Article XVIII, Research Security:** disclosure of foreign affiliations; **annual MFTRP certification** by covered individuals (within 30 days of each performance year)
- **Article XIX, Regulatory Correspondence:** furnish FDA/EMA correspondence; invite ARPA-H to substantive meetings
- **Articles XX to XXI:** synthetic nucleic-acid sourcing (OSTP framework); **prohibition on dangerous gain-of-function research** (E.O. 14292)
- **Article XXII, Special Terms and Conditions:** (A) salary-rate limitation, (B) **human subjects research** (45 CFR 46, FWA, IRB, restriction table), (C) animal subjects, plus fraud reporting, anti-terrorism, research misconduct, and the **Associate Performer Agreement** (cross-team data sharing)
- **Attachment 1, Task Description Document (TDD)** placeholder
- **Attachment 2, Report Requirements:** technical status (every 90 days), program plan, milestone, and final reports (within 60 days of completion)
- **Attachment 3, Milestone Payment Schedule** table (milestone, task, due month, description/exit criteria, payment, amount funded)
- **Attachment 4, Background Intellectual Property Assertions** table

## What it requires of a proposer

- **Submit this OT with redlines** in the full proposal; proposed deviations belong in the redline, not a separate letter.
- **Maintain active SAM registration** and register for **PMS within 10 days** of award to enable payment.
- **Structure work as fixed-price milestones** with clear exit criteria and deliverables (Attachment 3 feeds the price proposal).
- **List all Background IP** in Attachment 4 to preserve pre-existing rights.
- **Disclose foreign affiliations** and certify annually that covered individuals are not in a malign foreign talent recruitment program.
- **Give 10 days notice** before any publication or public release tied to the work.

## Watch-outs

> [!CAUTION]
> The cover-page **phase months (24 / 18 / 12)** are unfilled model-template placeholders and sum to 54, not the 60-month term. The **binding phase structure is Appendix A: Phase I 18mo, Phase II 18mo, Phase III 24mo**. Do not plan against the OT cover-page numbers.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Article VII protects a trade-secret path.** A Performer may elect to protect a Subject Invention as a trade secret by notifying the AO in writing within 8 months of disclosure. This is the contractual basis for keeping proprietary methods off public deliverables while still meeting reporting duties.

> [!WARNING]
> **Article XXII.B restricts human-subjects spending.** Funds may not be spent on human subjects research until the restriction in the HSR table is lifted, which requires an OLAW/OHRP-recognized **FWA** and **IRB** approval on file. Confirm the organization's FWA and IRB-of-record before budgeting any HSR task.

> [!WARNING]
> **Article XXII.A caps direct salary** paid from program funds at Federal Executive Schedule Level II. It limits the federally funded portion of salary, not total compensation, and it also applies to subawards.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Article X requires 10-day prior notice** to the PM and COMPASS before publications, presentations, or open-source releases. Build this lead time into any open-science release calendar.
