# Partner outreach strategy (internal)

**Created:** 2026-06-14. **Internal only; do not share with candidates.** This governs how we approach TA3 and TA4 candidates in parallel without overstating commitments or signaling an empty roster.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The honest anchor.** Two things are true and committed: **IPAI/Purdue (Ananth Grama, PI) is the prime, and Cytognosis and IPAI lead TA1 and TA2.** Lead with that real momentum. TA3 and TA4 are **roles we are assembling**, and we are **in active discussion with several leading groups**. Both statements are true and can be said to anyone.

## The rule of three statements

Use only statements that stay true regardless of who joins:

1. "IPAI/Purdue and Cytognosis anchor the AI and disease-model core (TA1, TA2)."
2. "We are assembling the experimental side (TA3, TA4) and are in active discussion with several leading groups."
3. "We see a specific role for you in [their capability]."

Never say a named candidate "has joined" or "is committed" until a teaming agreement or NDA-backed commitment exists. Never say "we do not have any partners yet." The anchor team plus active discussions is the accurate, momentum-positive framing.

## Per-candidate framing (parallel outreach)

- **Tailor the role, not the roster.** Tell each candidate the specific TA3 or TA4 capability we want from them. Do **not** name other candidates or their status. This avoids both overclaiming and disclosing competitors.
- **TA4 has room for several.** A team needs **at least two TA4 labs**, and Phase II to III adds more, so multiple TA4 partners can co-exist. Courting several TA4 labs in parallel is honest and expected.
- **TA3 lead is a single seat.** If we are talking to more than one TA3-lead candidate, frame it as "the team is forming" and **do not promise the TA3-lead role to two parties.** Decide internally before any commitment hardens; keep the runner-up as a TA4 or contributor option where it fits.
- **Sequence by fit and readiness.** Prioritize the highest-fit, highest-readiness candidate per slot; keep a named backup. Track every candidate in `IGoR_Candidate_Slate_Tier1-3_2026-06-03.md` with stage, exclusivity risk, and next step.

## What to share, and when

| Share now (one-pager, non-confidential) | Share only after NDA |
|---|---|
| The program, the closed-loop vision, the anchor team | The proprietary factorization method |
| The disease focus and the Phase I phenotypic-triage screen | The perturbation model under review |
| The role and a rough budget envelope, the timeline | Any personal-genomic detail |
| Open-standards and reproducibility commitment | Internal candidate slate and status |

The partner one-pager (`partner_onepager/IGoR_OnePager.pdf`) is the approved, non-confidential leave-behind. It contains no proprietary, personal, or competitor information, so it is safe to send to any candidate in parallel.

## Do say / do not say

| Do say | Do not say |
|---|---|
| "We are assembling TA3 and TA4 and in active discussion with several groups." | "We have no partners yet." |
| "We see you in the TA4 imaging-plus-transcriptomics role." | "Lab X has already joined as our imaging partner." |
| "The team is forming; we would value you as the TA3 lead." | "You are our only TA3 candidate" or "you are one of three TA3 candidates." |
| "Budget for this role is in the low single-digit millions over five years." | A specific dollar figure before internal cost sign-off. |

## Sequence

1. Get Ananth's approval on the one-pager (today/tomorrow).
2. Send the one-pager with a short, role-tailored cover note per candidate (template below).
3. Log responses in the candidate slate; hold internal go/no-go on the TA3-lead seat before committing.
4. Convert the strongest fits to NDA, then teaming discussion, ahead of the June 25 Solution Summary.

**Cover-note skeleton (per candidate):** one line on the program and the anchor team; one line on the specific role we see for them; one line inviting a short call this week; the one-pager attached.
