# IGoR teaming: Phylo (Biomni) and Cytognosis/IPAI, TA2 agentic add-on

**Team PsychIGoR. IPAI (Purdue) prime, with Ananth Grama as PI; Cytognosis and IPAI lead the disease-model and AI core (TA1 and TA2). We lead the New Science Engine, and would welcome Biomni as an optional agentic collaborator.** A short companion sheet explains the IGoR program itself.

## What we bring, and have already built

- **A causal, self-updating disease model (TA1)** for psychiatric disease, grounded in penetrant genetic forms (such as 22q11.2 deletion) modeled in iPSC cells.
- **A New Science Engine (TA2)** that interrogates the model itself, not just the literature, to choose the highest-value next experiment through a hypothesis tournament and model-grounded planning.
- **A Phase I experiment ready to run** that gives any agentic layer a concrete, high-signal task to operate on.
- A committed prime (IPAI/Purdue) and a single-cell and psychiatric-genetics core.

## Where Biomni fits

- **Optional agentic and tool-execution layer under our TA2 lead.** Biomni's breadth of biomedical tools and plan-execute-collaborate workflow can extend our engine's reach into literature synthesis, data retrieval, and routine analysis around the model-grounded core that stays ours.
- **Open-source integration.** Biomni's wide adoption lowers integration friction across an academically anchored consortium.

## Why this is a strong match

- Complementary by design: **our engine supplies the model-grounded, value-of-information decision; Biomni supplies broad tool execution**, so the roles do not overlap.
- Shared open-science posture fits the ARPA-H mandate and our team's openness commitments.
