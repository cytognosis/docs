# IGoR teaming: Medra and Cytognosis/IPAI, TA3/TA4 autonomous execution

**Team PsychIGoR. IPAI (Purdue) prime, with Ananth Grama as PI; Cytognosis and IPAI lead the disease-model and AI core (TA1 and TA2). We are assembling the experimental side of the team, and Medra's autonomous wet lab is a compelling execution layer for the closed loop.**

## What we bring, and have already built

- **A causal, self-updating disease model (TA1)** for psychiatric disease, grounded in penetrant genetic forms (such as 22q11.2 deletion) modeled in iPSC cells.
- **A New Science Engine (TA2)** that designs the highest-value next experiment by interrogating the model, the hypothesis side that your robots can execute.
- **A Phase I experiment ready to run:** a phenotypic-triage screen on isogenic iPSC pairs across transcriptomic, morphological, and functional calcium-imaging readouts.
- A committed prime (IPAI/Purdue), a single-cell and psychiatric-genetics core, two TA4 experimental arms (Matt Tegtmeyer's lab at Purdue and Cellanome), and computational morphology models (Anne Carpenter) on the team.

## Where Medra fits

- **TA4 autonomous execution at scale.** Medra Lab 001 and your Physical AI Scientist platform can industrialize the perturb-measure cycles our TA2 engine generates, turning a research loop into a high-throughput one.
- **TA3 protocol automation.** Natural-language-instructable execution across standard instruments helps close the protocol-to-hardware gap that interoperable IGoR experiments require.

## Why this is a strong match

- Your throughput is the kind of **execution scale** a compounding closed loop needs; our model and engine supply the high-value experiment stream to keep that capacity busy with the right work.
- Your Genentech collaboration demonstrates you can meet rigorous, partner-grade data standards, which the IGoR marketplace (TA4) demands.
- Clear division of labor: we own TA1 and TA2 modeling; you anchor autonomous TA4 execution. A first joint task is adapting your platform to **iPSC-neuron assays** (imaging, scRNA-seq, calcium imaging), which our team can specify.
