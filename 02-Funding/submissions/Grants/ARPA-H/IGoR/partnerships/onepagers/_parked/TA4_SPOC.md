**Team PsychIGoR. IPAI (Purdue) prime, with Ananth Grama as PI; Cytognosis and IPAI lead the disease-model and AI core (TA1 and TA2). Thank you for reaching out. Your cell-free protein platform is a strong orthogonal addition to our validated-laboratory side (TA4).**

## What we bring, and have already built

- **A causal, self-updating disease model (TA1)** for psychiatric disease, grounded in penetrant genetic forms (such as 22q11.2 deletion and high-odds-ratio SCHEMA genes) modeled in iPSC cells.
- **A New Science Engine (TA2)** that designs the highest-value next experiment by interrogating the model; it can define the exact protein or antibody list for your platform to test.
- **A Phase I experiment ready to run** across cellular readouts, where protein-level validation adds an independent, orthogonal axis of confirmation.
- A committed prime (IPAI/Purdue) and a single-cell and psychiatric-genetics core, with two TA4 experimental arms (Matt Tegtmeyer's lab at Purdue and Cellanome) and computational morphology models (Anne Carpenter) on the team.

## Where SPOC fits

- **Orthogonal protein-level validation (TA4).** Cell-free expression of up to 1,152 proteins or antibodies, with SPR kinetics, Cryo-EM, and MALDI, to **confirm TA1-predicted protein-protein interactions** and resolve mechanism where cellular assays cannot.
- **Variant effect at amino-acid resolution.** Deep mutational scanning of our penetrant-variant panel to quantify binding and stability effects, feeding the causal model directly.
- **Biomarker readouts.** Plasma and CSF proteomics (autoantibodies, PTM- and isoform-specific markers) for translational endpoints.

## Why this is a strong match

- Your platform is **orthogonal to our cellular core** (imaging, scRNA-seq, calcium imaging), so it adds an independent validation axis rather than duplicating capability.
- A bounded, well-defined protein-validation task fits cleanly as a TA4 add-on, with the experiment list set by our TA2 engine.
