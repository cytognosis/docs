**Subject:** ARPA-H IGoR teaming: LabOP for TA3, and your value-of-information planning for TA2

Hi Dan and Robert,

Your SIFT lightning talk at the IGoR Proposers' Day was a highlight for us. LabOP is exactly the open, interoperable protocol layer our proposal needs for TA3, and the Bayesian value-of-information planning you presented maps closely onto how we are building TA2.

A bit of context. Our team, **PsychIGoR**, is anchored by Purdue's Institute for Physical AI (IPAI) as prime, with its director, Ananth Grama, as PI. Cytognosis and IPAI jointly lead the disease-model and AI-engine core (TA1 and TA2), and McLean Hospital, a Harvard Medical School affiliate, anchors the clinical and translational genomics. Over the past decade, our team has led and contributed to the international consortia that built foundational single-cell molecular atlases of neuropsychiatric and neurodegenerative disease, including the first multi-cohort single-cell atlas of schizophrenia (Ruzicka, Mohammadi et al., Science 2024).

Our science is a causal, self-updating model of psychiatric disease (schizophrenia as the primary focus, extending to bipolar disorder in Phase III) that jointly integrates human genetics and isogenic cellular models, designs its own next perturbation experiments, and validates them in a phenotypic screen across penetrant genetic forms in iPSC-derived neurons, using imaging and single-cell readouts. We are now assembling the protocol and validated-laboratory partners, which is exactly where you come in: that loop only closes if an experiment can move between laboratories as easily as a data file, which is the problem LabOP solves.

Two things we would like to explore with you:

1. **TA3, the interoperable protocol stack, with SIFT leading.** LabOP's layered representation, its primitives, its execution records with provenance, and its specialization to OpenTrons, SiLA, and human-readable formats are the strongest open foundation we have seen for carrying our assays across validated laboratories.
2. **A possible TA2 planning and scheduling contribution.** The core of our TA2 is not another literature-reading co-scientist; it is an engine grounded in the mechanistic, causal TA1 model itself, interrogating that model's own uncertain parameters and conflicting edges to choose the highest-value experiment. Your Bayesian experiment choice and Round Trip planning are a natural complement for turning that choice into a scheduled, executable plan that hands off cleanly to LabOP. Cytognosis and IPAI anchor TA2, and we would welcome a scoped role for SIFT if it fits.

The one thing we would want to think through together is how to bridge your synthetic-biology automation to mammalian iPSC work, imaging, or single-cell readouts.

Could we find 30 minutes this week? I have attached a short overview, and I would value your perspective either way, given the June 25 Solution Summary deadline.
