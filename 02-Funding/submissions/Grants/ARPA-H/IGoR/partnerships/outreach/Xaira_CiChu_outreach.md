Hi Ci,

Good to see you at the Xaira gathering at NeurIPS, and good to finally say a quick hello to Marc. What X-Atlas and X-Cell are doing at that scale is genuinely impressive.

I wanted to float something that you and I kept coming back to at insitro and never got to build. Both the insitro and Xaira playbooks model one domain and validate in the other after the fact: learn from human genetics and confirm in cells, or learn from in-vitro perturbation and confirm in human genetics. That ordering leaks, because a lot of in-vitro axes are cellular artifacts (differentiation stage, batch, culture state) rather than disease biology, and it bites hardest exactly where a scalable cell-line platform has to reach a hard domain like CNS. The fix we always talked about is a joint factorization across the clinical and cellular domains at once, so the axes that matter are constrained by both.

I now have a funded vehicle to actually do it. I am leading the disease-model and AI core of an ARPA-H IGoR proposal (IPAI at Purdue is the prime, Ananth Grama is PI; our team is PsychIGoR), built on penetrant psychiatric genetics (22q11.2 and related) in iPSC-derived neurons, with paired clinical cohorts through McLean. That gives us a clean, causal testbed for the cell-to-clinic transfer that I think matters for where Xaira is heading on neuro.

This is not a pitch for you to be a subcontractor on a grant. It is a peer collaboration idea: your perturbation data and platform, our joint clinical-cellular factorization and the psychiatric disease side, inside a program designed to fund the closed-loop and cycle-time work that makes a perturbation platform compounding rather than one-shot. I have attached a short program overview and a one-pager on the concept.

Worth a call with you (and Bo if useful) in the next week or two? The Solution Summary is due June 25, so the timing is good to at least sketch whether there is something here.
