## TL;DR (read this first)

1. **IGoR is one integrated proposal that must address ALL FOUR TAs.** There is no per-TA bidding. "Proposals not addressing all four TAs will not be reviewed." One PI, one prime per proposal, ~3 teams funded. So the question "can someone be on the TA1 proposal and the TA2 proposal" does not apply: every team does TA1+TA2+TA3+TA4.

2. **Organizations may be sub-performers on multiple proposals** (explicit, Appendix A §5.1). **Individuals on multiple proposals is unaddressed** (allowed by silence). The only hard bite is **post-award**: if the same sub is on two selected teams doing overlapping same-TA work, ARPA-H may fund it once or make the sub pick one.

An org may be sub on multiple proposals (explicit, App A §5.1). Real limits are committed-effort ≤100%, OCI disclosure, reviewer optics (same PM Paul Sheehan reviews all ~3 awards), and a post-award sub de-duplication rule if the same Purdue bench does overlapping same-TA work on two selected teams. Elevated concern: both of our TA4 anchors (and our potential lead-PI candidate, Anne) are on a competing team. Resolve before Aug 6. Not a blocker for the Jun 25 SS.

**If you only read one thing:** The solicitation imposes *no* cap on how many IGoR proposals a person or an organization can join. Both Matt Tegtmeyer and Anne Carpenter are on an IU-prime IGoR team (~5 lead PIs; likely Purdue subaward); this is allowed. The constraints that actually matter are effort allocation (≤100%), OCI disclosure (at full-proposal stage), and a post-award sub de-duplication rule if the same bench does overlapping same-TA work on two selected teams. ## 1. The structural reframe

| Fact | Detail | Cite |
|------|--------|------|
| Must address all 4 TAs | "proposals must address all four TAs… Proposals not addressing all four TAs will not be reviewed." | App A §5.1 |
| One PI, one prime | "a single proposal led by one Principal Investigator under a single prime performer" | App A §5.1 |
| One person can cover multiple TAs | "A single team member may cover more than one TA." | App A §5.1 |
| Awards expected | ~3 multidisciplinary teams, 5-yr program (Phase I 18mo → II 18mo → III 24mo) | ISO §2 |
| Award instrument | Other Transaction (OT) Agreement | App E |

**Consequence:** There is no TA-specific eligibility lane. Every proposal is a full-stack team. The only TA-specific *teaming* rule is that **TA4 requires at least two laboratories per team**; TA1/TA2/TA3 have no stated minimum lab count.

## 2. Master rule: who can be on more than one proposal

| Participant / role | On multiple IGoR proposals? | What the solicitation says | Cite |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Organization as SUB-performer** | **Yes, explicitly allowed** | "An organization may serve as a sub-performer under multiple proposals." | App A §5.1 |
| **Individual (PI / co-I / Key Personnel)** | **Not addressed = not prohibited** | No person-level multi-proposal rule exists anywhere in ISO, App A, B, C, D | SILENT |
| **Organization as PRIME on 2+ proposals** | **Not addressed; structurally discouraged** | Structure is one-PI/one-prime *per proposal*; priming two competing teams is not banned in text but is unusual and optics-risky | SILENT / ambiguous |
| **Prime on one + sub on another** | **Sub side is fine; combination unaddressed** | Sub-on-multiple is explicit; the prime+sub combination is not discussed | partial |
| **Same concept already funded elsewhere** | **Barred at proposal stage** | Conformance: "concept has not received funding or been selected for award negotiations for another funding opportunity" | App C §1.1(a) |

**The one hard consequence (subs):** "If ARPA-H selects two or more proposals in which the same sub-performer is proposed to perform work under the **same Technical Area**… ARPA-H may, at its discretion, (i) fund the overlapping work only once, or (ii) require the sub-performer to elect the project in which it will participate." (App A §5.1). This triggers **only at/after selection**, not at proposal time.

**OCI is disclosure-based, not structural.** The prime and every named sub must disclose OCI and submit a mitigation plan at full-proposal stage (App C §3.5). There is **no rule** that a given role (e.g., TA3 architect) is barred from another team, and **no TA-to-TA separation rule**. The one categorical OCI bar: an organization providing ARPA-H advisory/support services (SETA/ISC/PIA-type) cannot also be an R&D performer, with a 1-year look-back (App C §3.6).

## 3. Your specific questions: Matt, Anne, Purdue, IPAI, and the IU-prime team

**Confirmed facts (2026-06-17):** The other IGoR team has **Indiana University as prime**, with **approximately 5 lead PIs**. Both **Matt Tegtmeyer** and **Anne Carpenter** participate, **likely via a Purdue subaward** (to be confirmed with Matt at the Jun 29 to Jul 3 Purdue visit).

### (a) Does Matt's participation in the IU-prime team restrict what he can do for/with us?
- **No prohibition.** IGoR is silent on a named scientist appearing on two proposals. Matt can be our Key Personnel (academic experimental arm lead) and also be on the IU-prime team.
- **Real constraints:**
  - **Committed effort ≤ 100%** across all projects. His LOE on our proposal must be real and disclosed in Current and Pending Support at full-proposal stage. Divided commitment can read poorly to reviewers.
  - **Concept non-duplication:** our prime's concept must not already be funded/selected elsewhere (App C §1.1a). Matt working a different concept elsewhere does not taint ours.
  - **If the IU-prime team also selects and both primes win same-TA4 work with Matt:** the sub de-duplication/election rule (§2) can bite at the org level if the same Purdue iPSC-neuron bench is double-committed to TA4 on two selected teams.

### (b) Does Anne's participation in the IU-prime team restrict what she can do for/with us?
- **Same rules as Matt.** Anne's computational role (no bench) is less likely to trigger the bench-level de-duplication concern, but if she is our lead PI and also on a competing team, reviewers will notice.
- **Elevated concern:** Anne is our potential lead-PI candidate. A lead PI on a competing team creates optics and post-award risk. Confirm at the Purdue visit that her IU-team role is minimal, defined, and disclosed; and that she is primary on PsychIGoR.

### (c) Does the IU-prime team stop Purdue from being prime on our proposal?
- **IGoR does not cap an organization at one proposal.** The IU-prime team uses Purdue as a sub (not as prime), so there is no prime-level collision. Our proposal has IPAI/Purdue as prime; the IU-prime team has IU as prime with Purdue as a sub. These are two different prime legal entities (IU UEI vs. Purdue UEI). The solicitation explicitly allows an org to be sub on multiple proposals (App A §5.1).
- **IPAI = Purdue legally** (same UEI). You cannot split them into two separate primes. But this is moot here because IU is prime on the other team, not Purdue.

### (d) What are the post-award de-duplication risks?
- If both PsychIGoR and the IU-prime team are selected, and Purdue is running the same TA4 iPSC-neuron bench for both, ARPA-H may (i) fund the overlapping TA4 bench work only once, or (ii) require Purdue to elect one project. The mitigation is: (1) confirm that Matt's experimental arm on our team uses non-overlapping committed hours from his IU-team role; (2) confirm that the iPSC-neuron bench and Element AVITI24 capacity is not double-booked; (3) define his role on the IU team as light and non-overlapping at the full-proposal stage.

### (e) What to confirm at the Jun 29 to Jul 3 Purdue visit?
1. IU is prime on the other team (not Purdue); Purdue's role there is as a sub.
2. Matt is primary on PsychIGoR with primary committed hours; his IU-team role is light and defined, with non-overlapping effort.
3. The Purdue subaward routing on the IU team and that the same iPSC-neuron bench and Element AVITI24 capacity is not double-committed.
4. Anne's role on the IU team is minimal and disclosed; if she is our lead PI, this must be explicitly reconciled.

### (f) Does it stop Purdue from being prime on only one proposal?
- **IGoR does not cap an organization at one proposal.** Org-as-sub-on-multiple is explicit; org-as-prime-on-multiple is unaddressed (not banned).
- Our scenario: Purdue is prime on PsychIGoR and a sub on the IU-prime team. This is fine per the rules. There is no Purdue-as-prime-on-two-IGoR-proposals issue here.
- **If Purdue were somehow prime on two IGoR proposals simultaneously:** not prohibited in text, but structurally odd and optics-risky. Not our situation.

### (c) Is it IPAI that is capped at one, while Purdue can do more?
- **Legally they are the same entity.** IPAI (Institute for Physical AI) is a **unit within Purdue**, not a separately incorporated organization. The SAM/UEI registration, OCI disclosures, and "one prime per proposal" all attach to **Purdue University (the Trustees of Purdue)**. IPAI is the administering/performing unit.
- **You cannot split "IPAI" and "Purdue" into two distinct primes** to get two seats. Two proposals "from IPAI" are two proposals from Purdue on the same UEI.
- **But this is mostly moot**, because IGoR does not cap an org at one proposal anyway. Same-entity status only matters for the post-award sub de-duplication rule and OCI optics.
- **Confirm with Purdue Sponsored Programs (OSP)** that IPAI has no separate registration (standard case: it does not).

## 4. How the rules change across the three stages

| Dimension | Solution Summary (due **2026-06-25**, 12:00 ET) | Full Proposal (invited, due **2026-08-06**, 12:00 ET) | Award / receipt of funds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Team binding | Non-binding listing; roster can change | Basis for negotiation; team locked | Contractual |
| PI named | Yes | Yes (+ biosketch) | Contractual |
| PM + Software Architect named | Not required | **Required** (architect distinct from PI and PM) | Contractual |
| Subs listed | Name + POC only | Full table: org type, roles, effort | Contractual |
| SAM/UEI | Optional ("if already registered") | **Mandatory for prime** | Required; maintained through expiration |
| OCI disclosure | Not required | **Required** (prime + all subs) + mitigation plan | Annual recertification |
| Current & Pending (effort) | Not required | **Required** for Key Personnel | Contractual effort limits |
| Multi-proposal enforcement | None | None explicit (disclosure only) | **Sub de-duplication/election can trigger here**; APAs govern cross-team sharing |
| Page limit | 5 pp (+ cover, BOE, refs) | 40 pp (+ TDD, price, biosketches, Gantt) | — |

**Plain reading of the stage trend:** restrictions are essentially **nil at Solution Summary**, **disclosure-based at Full Proposal**, and **binding + de-duplicating at Award**. So at the June 25 SS stage, Matt and Purdue can appear on whatever they appear on with no formal conflict; the squeeze (effort, OCI, overlap election) only arrives later.

## 5. TA-by-TA differences that matter

- **No per-TA eligibility difference.** All teams do all four TAs; there is no TA you "qualify" into separately.
- **TA4 is the only TA with a hard teaming floor:** ≥ 2 laboratories per team (App A §4.1.4).
- **No TA-level OCI separation.** Nothing stops the same org or person from working across TA1–TA4 within a team, or from being on another team. "A single team member may cover more than one TA" (App A §5.1).
- **Required named roles span all TAs:** one PI, one Project Manager, one Software Architect (architect must be distinct from PI and PM), plus named integration leads with dedicated hours (App A §5.5).
- **IV&V is external:** ARPA-H separately funds independent V&V orgs (TA1 models; TA3/TA4 infrastructure). Performers do not budget IV&V but must allocate collaboration effort.
