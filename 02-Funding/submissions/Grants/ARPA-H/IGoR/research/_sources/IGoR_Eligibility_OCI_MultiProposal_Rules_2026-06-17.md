## A. ELIGIBILITY — Who May Be Prime or Sub

### A.1 General Eligibility

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.1, p. 3**

> "Except as noted herein, all sources that can satisfy the government's needs and that are neither debarred nor suspended from doing business with the government may respond to this ISO. Universities, non-profit organizations, small businesses and other commercial entities are encouraged to respond. Any submission received will be fairly considered against the terms and conditions of the solicitation and may be selected for award."

**Interpretation:** For-profit companies, 501(c)(3) nonprofits, universities, and small businesses are all explicitly eligible. No US-ownership or domestic-incorporation requirement is stated for eligibility in general (but see foreign entity restrictions below).

---

### A.2 FFRDCs and Government Entities — General Prohibition

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.1.1(a), p. 3–4**

> "ARPA-H is primarily interested in responses to this solicitation from commercial entities, academia, and non-profit organizations, not from federal or state governments. As such, Federally Funded Research and Development Centers (FFRDCs) and government entities (which includes government employees) will not be considered as either the prime or a sub-performer."

**Exception (Section 3.1.1(b)):**

> "However, an FFRDC or government entity may have unique capabilities that are not otherwise available. Accordingly, when a potential prime performer believes an FFRDC has a unique capability without which its solution is unachievable, it may provide documentation demonstrating it has exhausted all other options as part of its submission(s). ARPA-H will determine whether inclusion of the FFRDC is necessary for the solution."

**Note:** National labs that are FFRDCs fall under this prohibition unless the waiver process is followed.

---

### A.3 ARPA-H Support Contractors — Categorical Ineligibility

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.1.2, p. 4**

> "Support services involve contracted labor providing technical, professional, and/or financial expertise, and/or administrative assistance. Individuals and entities providing support services to ARPA-H are ineligible for an award stemming from this ISO."

**Supplementary — Appendix C, Section 3.6, p. 8 (Organizational Agency Supplemental OCI Policy):**

> "ARPA-H restricts Performers from concurrently providing professional support services, including Advisory and Assistance Services or similar contracted support services, in addition to performing as an R&D technical Performer. Therefore, the proposer must affirm whether it or any proposed team member (proposed sub-awardee, etc.) is providing professional support services to any ARPA-H office(s) under: (1) a current award or subaward; or (2) a past award or subaward that ended within one calendar year prior to the proposal's submission date."

**Interpretation:** Both the organization AND individual persons providing ARPA-H support services are ineligible. The look-back period for past support is one calendar year prior to proposal submission date.

---

### A.4 Foreign Entities

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.1.3, p. 4**

> "While ARPA-H prioritizes awards to entities (organizations and/or individuals) that conduct funded work in the United States, foreign entities may participate to the extent that they comply with non-disclosure agreements, security regulations, export control laws, and other governing statutes applicable under the circumstances. In no case will award be made to:
> - An entity of concern meeting any of the criteria in Section 10638(3) of the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022;
> - An individual who is a party to a malign foreign talent recruitment program (MFTRP) as defined in Section 10638(4) of the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022;
> - Non-domestic entities organized under the laws of a 'covered' foreign country (as defined in 50 U.S.C. § 3059)."

**Interpretation:** Foreign entities may participate as sub-performers but not as primes if they fall under the prohibitions above. "Covered foreign countries" (50 U.S.C. § 3059) currently include China, Russia, Iran, North Korea, and Cuba. The solicitation does not explicitly say a foreign entity cannot be a prime, but the ARPA-H preference for US-performed work and the CHIPS Act bars effectively preclude most non-US primes.

---

### A.5 SAM/UEI Registration

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.2, p. 5**

> "At the time of proposal submission, the proposer must be registered in the System for Award Management (SAM.gov) and have a valid Unique Entity Identifier (UEI). Proposers must also have an active, accurate registration in SAM.gov at the time of award and through its expiration."

**Interpretation:**
- SAM/UEI registration is required **at proposal submission** (not just at award).
- Registration must remain active through award expiration.
- This requirement explicitly applies to the **proposer (prime)**; the solicitation does not state that sub-performers must independently register in SAM at proposal stage.
- At award stage (Appendix E, Article V), the Performer (prime) must "Maintain an active registration for 'All Awards' in SAM throughout the life of the award."

**Ambiguity:** The solicitation requires SAM registration of the "proposer" at proposal submission. It is silent on whether sub-awardees must also register in SAM at that stage (vs. at award).

## B. ORGANIZATIONAL CONFLICT OF INTEREST (OCI)

### B.1 General OCI Disclosure and Mitigation Requirement

**Source: Appendix C, Section 3.5, p. 8**

> "Through submission of a proposal, the proposer is required to identify and disclose all facts relevant to a potential OCI involving the proposer, its organization, and/or any proposed team member (proposed sub-awardee). Along with the OCI disclosure, the proposer must submit an OCI mitigation plan, which is a description of the action the proposer has taken to avoid, neutralize, or mitigate the stated OCI. The government may require the proposer to provide additional information to assist the government in evaluating the OCI mitigation plan. The government may reject the proposal and withdraw it from consideration for award if it determines the proposer failed to:
> - fully disclose an OCI; or
> - provide the affirmation of ARPA-H support; or
> - reasonably provide additional information requested by the government to assist in evaluating the proposer's OCI mitigation plan."

---

### B.2 ARPA-H Support Services OCI (Supplemental Agency Policy)

**Source: Appendix C, Section 3.6, p. 8**

> "ARPA-H restricts Performers from concurrently providing professional support services, including Advisory and Assistance Services or similar contracted support services, in addition to performing as an R&D technical Performer. Therefore, the proposer must affirm whether it or any proposed team member (proposed sub-awardee, etc.) is providing professional support services to any ARPA-H office(s) under: (1) a current award or subaward; or (2) a past award or subaward that ended within one calendar year prior to the proposal's submission date. If any professional support services are or were provided to any ARPA-H office(s), the proposal must include:
> - The name of the ARPA-H office receiving the support
> - The prime contract number and
> - Identification of proposed team member (including any proposed sub-awardee) providing the support."

---

### B.3 OCI Disclosure Form (Admin Document)

**Source: Appendix D (Admin National Policy Document), OCI Affirmations Section, p. 3**

The required OCI affirmation form asks three questions (paraphrased from the template):

(a) Are any proposed team members providing ISC/SETA/PIA or similar support to ARPA-H currently?
(b) Did any proposed team member provide such support within one calendar year of this proposal submission?
(c) Are there other potential OCIs involving any proposed team member?

If (a) or (b) is yes, the proposer must provide: ARPA-H office name, prime contract number, name and type of support, and an OCI mitigation plan.

---

### B.4 OCI — What the Solicitation Does NOT Say

**CRITICAL FINDING:** The solicitation contains **no OCI provision restricting one organization from being on multiple proposals**, no prohibition on an organization simultaneously appearing as prime on one proposal and sub on another, and no exclusion of any role (e.g., systems engineering, TA3 architecture, advisory) that would trigger automatic OCI exclusion from other roles.

The OCI rules are entirely disclosure-based and support-services-based. They do not create structural role-based prohibitions between competing IGoR teams.

## C. MULTIPLE-PROPOSAL PARTICIPATION

### C.1 The Core Rule — Organization as Sub-Performer on Multiple Proposals

**Source: Appendix A, Section 5.1, p. 4–5 (CRITICAL)**

> "To be responsive to this solicitation, proposals must address all four TAs (TA1, TA2, TA3, & TA4). Proposals not addressing all four TAs will not be reviewed. **An organization may serve as a sub-performer under multiple proposals.** If ARPA-H selects two or more proposals in which the same sub-performer is proposed to perform work under the same Technical Area, ARPA-H reserves the sole right to determine whether the proposed efforts are substantially distinct or whether a material overlap exists. In the event ARPA-H determines that such an overlap exists, ARPA-H may, at its discretion, (i) fund the overlapping work only once, or (ii) require the sub-performer to elect the project in which it will participate."

**Verbatim key sentence:** "An organization may serve as a sub-performer under multiple proposals."

**Interpretation of the overlap clause:** ARPA-H explicitly permits sub-performer participation in multiple proposals at the **proposal stage**. The election/funding-once consequence is triggered only **if** ARPA-H selects two or more proposals containing the same sub-performer in the same TA and determines there is material overlap. ARPA-H — not the proposers — makes this determination and chooses the remedy.

---

### C.2 Single Prime per Proposal — Required

**Source: Appendix A, Section 5.1, p. 5**

> "Proposers must submit a single proposal led by one Principal Investigator under a single prime performer that addresses program Phase I, Phase II, and Phase III."

---

### C.3 Whether an Organization May Submit as PRIME on More Than One Proposal

**EXPLICIT RULE: NONE FOUND.**

The solicitation is **silent** on whether the same organization may be prime on more than one proposal. Section 3.1 says "any submission received will be fairly considered"; Section 5.1 addresses sub-performer multi-participation explicitly but says nothing about prime-proposer multi-participation.

**What can be inferred:** The logic of Section 5.1 — which explicitly permits sub-performer multi-team participation while conditioning it on ARPA-H overlap determinations — implies that prime multi-proposal submission is not structurally prohibited either, but this is inference, not explicit text.

---

### C.4 Whether an INDIVIDUAL (PI / Key Personnel) May Appear on More Than One Proposal

**EXPLICIT RULE: NONE FOUND.**

The solicitation is **entirely silent** on whether a named scientist (PI, Co-PI, Key Personnel, Senior Personnel) may appear on more than one competing proposal. There is no "one PI per person" rule stated anywhere in the ISO body, Appendix A, Appendix C, Appendix D, or the draft OT Agreement.

**What to note:** Key Personnel must submit Current and Pending (Other) Support forms (Appendix D, p. 2), which would disclose simultaneous participation. However, disclosure is not a prohibition.

---

### C.5 Conforming Submission Disqualification — Concept Already Funded Elsewhere

**Source: Appendix C, Section 1.1(a), p. 1**

> "A submission may be deemed non-conforming if it does not meet the following solicitation requirements: [...] The proposer's concept has not received funding or been selected for award negotiations for another funding opportunity (whether from ARPA-H or another government agency)."

**Interpretation:** This is a concept-level rule, not a person-level or organization-level multi-proposal rule. If the same technical concept has already been funded or selected for award, submitting it again is non-conforming. This does not bar the same organization or PI from participating in a second, distinct proposal.

## D. PRIME VS. SUBAWARDEE STRUCTURE

### D.1 Single Prime Required

**Source: Appendix A, Section 5.1, p. 5**

> "Proposers must submit a single proposal led by one Principal Investigator under a single prime performer that addresses program Phase I, Phase II, and Phase III."

**One prime, one PI per proposal** is an explicit requirement.

---

### D.2 Team Composition and Consortium

**Source: Appendix A, Section 5.1, p. 4–5**

> "Each proposal team is expected to include performers with the expertise and capabilities to address all four TAs. This may involve a single organization with breadth across all areas or, more likely, a team of organizations, each contributing expertise in one or more TAs. A single team member may cover more than one TA."

**Interpretation:** Multi-organization teams (consortia) are explicitly expected and encouraged. The prime holds the award and flows funds to subs. The draft OT Agreement (Appendix E) calls non-prime participants "sub-performers" and defines them as "Any person or entity awarded by the Performer part of the work or services under this Agreement. In this Agreement, the term sub-performer includes sub-awardees, subcontractors, and other team members."

---

### D.3 Who Holds the Award

**Source: Appendix E (Draft OT Agreement), Article I (Definitions)**

> "Performer: COMPANY/ORGANIZATION/TEAM NAME"
> "Sub-performer: Any person or entity awarded by the Performer part of the work or services under this Agreement. In this Agreement, the term sub-performer includes sub-awardees, subcontractors, and other team members."

The Performer (prime) holds the agreement with ARPA-H and flows work to sub-performers.

---

### D.4 TA4 Minimum Lab Requirement

**Source: Appendix A, Section 4.1.4, p. 7**

> "Each team must include at least two TA4 laboratories."

This is a team composition requirement, not a structural constraint on the prime/sub model. TA4 laboratories may be the prime or any sub.

## E. STAGE DIFFERENCES

### E.1 Solution Summary Stage

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 4.1, p. 5**

> "Non-Gated Solution Summary processes involve:
> Step 1: Submit Solution Summary through the Solutions site.
> Step 2: Potential proposers are provided feedback encouraging or discouraging the submission of a proposal.
> Step 3: Submit Proposals (Potential proposers may submit proposals, regardless of whether encouraged in Step 2)."

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 4.2, p. 5**
> "Solution Summary submissions are required, and due by the date listed in the ISO Summary Information Section."

No eligibility certifications (SAM, OCI, etc.) are required at the Solution Summary stage. No SAM registration is required at this stage.

**Eligibility rules do NOT differ at Solution Summary vs. Full Proposal stage in terms of who may submit.** The same rules apply, but formal certifications (SAM, OCI affirmation, research security disclosures) are embedded in the full proposal documents (Appendix D) and not in the Solution Summary.

---

### E.2 Full Proposal Stage

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.2, p. 5**
> "At the time of proposal submission, the proposer must be registered in the System for Award Management (SAM.gov) and have a valid Unique Entity Identifier (UEI)."

SAM/UEI registration is required **at proposal submission** (i.e., full proposal stage, August 6, 2026 deadline). It is NOT required at Solution Summary stage (June 25, 2026 deadline).

At the full proposal stage, the prime submits:
- Appendix D with OCI affirmations
- Research security disclosures
- Current and Pending Support forms for all Senior/Key Personnel

---

### E.3 Award Stage

**Source: ISO_main.txt, Section 3.2, p. 5**
> "Proposers must also have an active, accurate registration in SAM.gov at the time of award and through its expiration."

**Source: Appendix E, Article V**
> "The Government shall make fixed payments to the Performer for the completion of milestones. To enable payment, the Performer shall: Maintain an active registration for 'All Awards' in System for Award Management (SAM) throughout the life of the award..."

The overlap/election clause for sub-performers on multiple proposals (Section C.1 above) is triggered only at award stage — ARPA-H acts on overlapping sub-performer participation only "if ARPA-H selects two or more proposals."

---

### E.4 No "One Award Per Entity" Rule

**EXPLICIT RULE: NONE FOUND.** The solicitation does not state that an organization or individual may only be selected for one award. The ISO Summary states "Anticipated Award(s): Multiple Other Transaction (OT) Agreements," confirming multiple awards are expected. The sub-performer overlap clause addresses the scenario where the same organization is on more than one selected proposal but does not prohibit it.
