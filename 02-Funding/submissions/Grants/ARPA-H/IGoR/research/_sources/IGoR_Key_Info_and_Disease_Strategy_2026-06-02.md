# ARPA-H IGoR — Key Info + Disease-Scope Strategy

**Date:** 2026-06-02 · **Source:** the SAM.gov solicitation files in `./materials/` (ISO ARPA-H-SOL-26-155 + Appendices A-E). **Solution Summary due Jun 25, 12pm ET; full proposal Aug 6.**

## Disease scope: area anchored by 22q11DS (the recommendation)

IGoR is **disease-agnostic**: proposers choose their own complex disease area, which must be **multifactorial, multi-scale, mechanistically tractable, and progressable in 5 years**, and it **explicitly excludes "well-bounded problems."** A **second, related disease area is required by Phase III** (generalization).

- **Do not propose a single disease** (22q11DS alone): too narrow, reads as the well-bounded problem they reject, fails the Phase III generalization requirement.
- **Do not propose a vague area** with no anchor: weak on tractability (a top criterion).
- **Recommended: a neuropsychiatric / transdiagnostic AREA, with 22q11DS as the Phase I/II flagship exemplar,** generalizing to idiopathic schizophrenia or broader transdiagnostic psychiatry in Phase III. 22q11DS gives a clean genotype → cell → circuit → phenotype causal chain (one locus, high penetrance, existing single-cell atlases), which is exactly what makes the TA1 mechanistic models tractable and verifiable, an advantage most neuropsychiatric proposals lack. Frame 22q11DS as scientific rationale; personal disclosure optional.

## What IGoR is

An AI-enabled, interoperable research ecosystem to accelerate complex-disease therapy development (target: validated knowledge ≥10x faster). Four Technical Areas, **all four must be addressed or the proposal is not reviewed**:

- **TA1 Comprehensive Disease Models:** modular, mechanistic, multiscale digital twins (causal, not correlational).
- **TA2 New Science Engine:** AI orchestration that finds gaps, generates hypotheses, designs experiments, and explains its reasoning. Not a frontier-LLM wrapper.
- **TA3 Interoperable Experimental Procedures:** layered open-standard protocol stack across ≥3 experimental modalities.
- **TA4 Experiment Marketplace:** a network of validated labs (cloud labs, CROs, core facilities) executing TA3 protocols and returning model-ready data.

## Team, budget, submission

- **Team:** ~3 awards. Single prime + one PI; a single org may cover all TAs but a multi-org team is "more likely." **At least two TA4 labs required.** Named **Project Manager** and **software architect** required (distinct from PI).
- **Budget:** **not stated in any file** (no ceiling/floor/total). Period of performance **5 years, 3 phases (18 + 18 + 24 months)**; continuation gated on milestones. **OT** award. Cost-share encouraged, not required. (Scope implies large multi-million-per-team awards; confirm at Proposers' Day / question channel.)
- **Submission:** Solution Summary 5 pages (Concept, Innovation/Impact, Proposed Work, Team, BOE, References), due **Jun 25 12pm ET**; **non-gated** (encouraged/discouraged feedback, weight it heavily); full Technical & Management Proposal 40 pages due **Aug 6**. Proposers' Day TBD.
- **Evaluation (in order):** (1) scientific/technical merit, (2) team capability/experience, (3) mission relevance + end-user orientation, (4) cost realism. Proposals judged on own merits, not ranked.
