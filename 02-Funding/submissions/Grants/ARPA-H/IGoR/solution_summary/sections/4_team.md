## 4. Team Organization and Capabilities

We propose as **Team PsychIGoR**. Confirmed members are listed without a status note; roles still being finalized are marked.

| Role | Member and capability |
|---|---|
| Prime and Principal Investigator | **IPAI, Purdue University; Ananth Grama** computational science at scale, systems integration, and program management |
| TA1 and TA2 co-leads | **Cytognosis Foundation (Shahin Mohammadi) and IPAI, Purdue** single-cell atlases, causal and perturbation modeling, psychiatric genetics, and computational science at scale |
| **Academic experimental arm (TA4)** | **Matthew Tegtmeyer, Purdue** iPSC-derived NGN2 neuron disease models, 22q11.2 phenomics and transcriptomics; fixed-cell high-plex readouts (RNA ~350-plex, protein ~50-plex, Cell-Painting-style morphology, in-situ CRISPR-guide sequencing); experimental bridge to TA1 |
| **Computational morphology and imaging models** | **Anne Carpenter, IPAI/Purdue** inventor of Cell Painting and CellProfiler; interpretable models for morphology and cellular-imaging data; common analysis layer consuming readouts from both TA4 arms (no wet bench) |
| Clinical and translational | **W. Brad Ruzicka, McLean Hospital** psychiatric single-cell genomics, cohort interpretation, and data governance |
| TA3 interoperable protocols | **SIFT; Daniel Bryce (lead), Robert Goldman (advisory)** LabOP co-authors; protocol semantics, automated planning, and standards delivery |
| Project Manager | **Patricia Purcell** multi-team program and delivery management |
| Software and Systems Architect | **Elham Jebalbarezi Sarbijan, IPAI/Purdue** (tentative) interoperability and open-source systems architecture |
| TA4 external laboratories | Cellanome (live-cell imaging, same-cell scRNA-seq; advancing), Illumina (sequencing and bioinformatics; proposed) |

Every confirmed member sits directly on the critical path: genome-scale single-cell and psychiatric-genetics modeling (Cytognosis and IPAI), computational morphology and imaging models (Carpenter, confirmed), iPSC-neuron disease-model execution and multi-modal readouts (Tegtmeyer), interoperable protocol design (SIFT, confirmed), and clinical and cohort expertise (McLean). The Project Manager (Purcell) and the Software and Systems Architect (Elham, tentative) are distinct roles from the PI, as the ISO requires, and TA1 is staffed entirely in-house to protect the mission-critical core.
