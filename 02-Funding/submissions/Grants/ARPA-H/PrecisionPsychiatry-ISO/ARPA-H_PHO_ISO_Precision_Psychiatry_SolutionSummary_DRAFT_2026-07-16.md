**If you only read one thing:** psychiatry still has no objective, causal diagnostic. We will build a universal, genomics-grounded dimensional map of serious mental illness and a causal digital psychiatric twin that predicts and adapts the right treatment for each person, measured by lives and life-years restored.

## 1. Concept and objective (U01)

Serious mental illness is diagnosed and treated without an objective test. We will build the first **causal coordinate system for the human psyche**, a measurable map on which any patient can be located, and a **decision engine** that predicts which treatment will work for that individual and adapts as they change. We anchor in schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and major depression, and design the system to generalize transdiagnostically. The result is objective measurement plus personalized, mechanism-grounded treatment selection, replacing symptom-checklist labels and trial-and-error prescribing.

## 2. State of the art and its limits (U02)

- **No objective diagnostics.** Diagnosis rests on subjective symptom checklists; the same patient receives different diagnoses across clinicians, the exact inconsistency ARPA-H EVIDENT was created to address.
- **Masked heterogeneity.** Patients sharing one label are biologically diverse, and the specific symptom set behind a diagnosis is often not recorded, so within-diagnosis structure is lost.
- **Dimensional and transdiagnostic reality is ignored.** Symptoms are quantitative and shared across disorders; genomic factor structure and comorbidity both show shared causal drivers, yet care forces discrete categories, creating artificial comorbidity and frequent "missing diagnoses."
- **Multiscale causation is not integrated.** Genetics, connectomics, and the exposome interact (twin discordance in schizophrenia, exposome studies, genetics used to disentangle connectomics), but no framework unifies these scales at the individual level.
- **Treatment failure.** Treating symptoms rather than causes, discovered by slow trial and error, produces side effects, relapse, and the label of treatment resistance. Care arrives late and rarely tracks the individual trajectory.

## 3. Innovation and approach (U03, U09)

**A universal dimensional map.** We place SMI on 3 to 5 leading genomic factors (a Grotzinger-type five-factor architecture across fourteen disorders), aligned to DSM cross-cutting symptoms, HiTOP, and RDoC through our harmonized clinical-instrument crosswalk (111 instruments, 2,399 items mapped to HPO, SNOMED, LOINC, RDoC, and HiTOP).

**Multiscale biotypes.** Each person is characterized at three scales: **micro** (molecular and cellular, including BDNF/TrkB neuroplasticity axes), **meso** (connectomic nodes, edges, and subgraphs), and **macro** (phenotypic and cross-trial). These combine into individual coordinates rather than a single categorical label.

**A causal digital psychiatric twin.** A dynamic AI world-model of a person's cognitive, emotional, and behavioral state that simulates the causal effect of candidate treatments in silico (causal reinforcement learning over future outcomes), ranks the best course for that individual, and adapts to their trajectory.

**Mechanism-grounded treatment mapping.** Treatments are mapped by effect across scales: micro (Neuroscience-based Nomenclature pharmacology, neuroplastogens, EVIDENT-accepted mechanisms), meso (for example dlPFC targeting, SAINT-style stimulation, default-mode modulation), and macro (cross-trial instrument outcomes), organized by therapeutic hypothesis.

**Platform and delivery.** The Cytognosis platform provides the multimodal, causal, knowledge-graph-grounded engine. **Yar** delivers on-device causal AI and a neurodivergent-friendly companion that captures real-world trajectory. **Neuroverse** is the neuro foundation-model layer. **Brainscope** (with Herve Marie-Nelly) adds spectroscopy-based objective sensing.

**Why us, why now.** A founder with lived experience and two decades of computational biology, a shift toward new approach methodologies, and a direct complement to EVIDENT make this the right team at the right time.

## 4. Impact and beneficiaries (U04, U18)

The impact is measured in **lives and life-years restored** and in more therapies that work the first time, sparing patients repeated rounds of failed treatment. Beneficiaries include people living with serious mental illness, the clinicians who treat them, and drug developers who gain biotype-stratified trials. For the mission, the deliverable is objective psychiatric measurement plus personalized intervention. Transition follows an open-core path: the dimensional map is released as a public scientific resource (an FRO-style asset, complementary to a Convergent Research track) while clinical decision support and continuous monitoring move toward deployment through a public-benefit pathway.

## 6. Risks and mitigations (U05)

- **Biotype validity and replication.** Multi-cohort validation (McLean published, plus Mount Sinai), with pre-registered held-out concordance thresholds.
- **Causal identifiability.** Triangulate genetics, cellular perturbation, and longitudinal data; use causal reinforcement learning with conservative off-policy evaluation.
- **Data access and human subjects.** North Star IRB reliance in place, FWA filed, privacy-by-design via on-device Yar processing.
- **Clinical translation.** Decision-support framing first, not autonomous prescribing, keeping a clinician in the loop.

## 7. Schedule, milestones, and quantitative exams (U07, U08)

- **Mid-term go/no-go:** biotype-assignment concordance on held-out multimodal data (target Jaccard at or above 0.85); treatment-response prediction beating standard-of-care selection by a pre-set margin.
- **Final exam:** prospective trajectory prediction that anticipates relapse; digital-twin in-silico treatment ranking validated against retrospective response.
- **Sequence:** anchor in schizophrenia (22q11.2 / TBX1), then extend to bipolar disorder and major depression.
