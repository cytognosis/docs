**If you only read one thing:** psychiatry has no objective, causal diagnostics; we propose a universal, genomics-grounded dimensional map of the human psyche plus a causal "digital psychiatric twin" that predicts which treatment will work for which person, then adapts over time.

## Objective (U01)

Give clinicians objective, mechanism-grounded coordinates for a patient's mental state, and a model that prioritizes the treatment most likely to work for that individual, replacing symptom-checklist diagnosis and trial-and-error prescribing.

## The gap (U02, U04)

- **No objective diagnostics.** Psychiatric diagnosis relies on subjective symptom checklists; the same patient receives different diagnoses across clinicians, a limitation ARPA-H EVIDENT itself names.
- **Masked heterogeneity.** Patients inside one diagnostic label are biologically diverse; the specific symptom set behind a diagnosis is often not even recorded.
- **Dimensional and transdiagnostic structure is ignored.** Phenotypes are quantitative and shared across disorders (evident in genomic factor structure and comorbidity), yet care forces them into discrete categories.
- **Treatment failure.** Treating symptoms rather than causes, discovered by slow and painful trial and error, drives side effects, relapse, and treatment resistance.
- **Too late, no trajectory.** Care intervenes after crisis and rarely tracks the individual path over time.

## Approach and novelty (U03, U09)

- **A universal dimensional map of the human psyche.** MDD, bipolar, and schizophrenia first (extensible to PTSD and anxiety/autism), placed on 3 to 5 leading genomic factors, aligned to DSM cross-cutting symptoms, HiTOP, and RDoC via our harmonized clinical-instrument crosswalk.
- **Multi-scale biotypes.** Micro (molecular and cellular), meso (connectomic nodes, edges, and subgraphs), and macro (phenotypic and cross-trial) evidence layers combined into person-level coordinates.
- **A causal digital psychiatric twin.** A dynamic, AI world-model of an individual's cognitive, emotional, and behavioral state that simulates the causal effect of candidate treatments in silico (causal reinforcement learning over future outcomes) and adapts to the person's trajectory.
- **Treatment grounded in mechanism.** Micro effects (NbN pharmacology, neuroplastogens, and EVIDENT-accepted mechanisms), meso effects (for example dlPFC targeting, SAINT-style stimulation, default-mode modulation), and macro effects (cross-trial instrument outcomes), mapped to biotypes.

## Solution stack (U17)

- **Cytognosis platform** (general): the multimodal, causal, knowledge-graph-grounded engine.
- **Yar:** on-device causal AI and a neurodivergent-friendly companion that captures real-world trajectory.
- **Neuroverse:** the neuro foundation-model layer.
- **Brainscope (with Herve Marie-Nelly):** spectroscopy-based sensing for objective, continuous measurement.

## Why ARPA-H, why now (U18)

Fits the Mission Office mandate for objective psychiatric measurement and personalized intervention, complements EVIDENT, and aligns with the shift toward new approach methodologies. The impact is measured in **lives and life-years restored** and in more therapies that work the first time, not in dollars.

## Exams and go/no-go (U08, brief)

Biotype-assignment concordance against held-out multimodal data; treatment-response prediction beating standard-of-care selection; prospective trajectory tracking that anticipates relapse. Quantitative thresholds to be set in the Solution Summary.
