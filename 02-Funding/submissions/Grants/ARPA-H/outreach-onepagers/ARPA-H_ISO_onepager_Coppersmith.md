## The problem EVIDENT is trying to solve, in one paragraph

Psychiatry is the only branch of medicine that selects treatment from a categorical label produced by a 30-minute symptom interview, with no objective biological assay to confirm the label and no continuous physiological readout to confirm whether the treatment is working. The consequences are quantified: an ~8-year mean delay from first depressive symptoms to evidence-based care, first-line antidepressant response of 42-53%, cumulative STAR*D relapse climbing from 40% to 71% across four steps, and suicide as a leading cause of death in US adolescents with no validated clinical biomarker. Rapid-acting treatments (ketamine, esketamine, psilocybin, SAINT, zuranolone) are arriving faster than the infrastructure to match patients to them. EVIDENT's Phases 1-4 diagnose the same three gaps we address: **no objective diagnostics; within-diagnosis heterogeneity is masked; treatment selection is population-average, not personal**.

## Our thesis: a universal dimensional map, with rapid-treatment sensitivity as a first-class endpoint

We are building a **universal dimensional map of the human psyche**: an interpretable, transdiagnostic coordinate system that connects genotype to molecular pathway to cell type to circuit to symptom to treatment response. The map is dimensional (continuous axes, not categorical labels), transdiagnostic (cuts across DSM diagnoses, aligned with RDoC, HiTOP, and the Grotzinger 2025 five-factor genomic scaffold), and mechanistic (each axis is anchored in identifiable biology from micro to meso to macro).

The map's core deliverable for EVIDENT is a set of **objective, longitudinally trackable coordinates** that (a) detect who is likely to respond to which rapid-acting treatment before administration, (b) confirm mechanism engagement after administration, and (c) provide a continuous physiological readout between visits. This is the missing operational layer that translates dimensional biology (Drysdale, B-SNIP, Williams, Grotzinger, Beam-Etkin, Quah) into daily clinical practice.

## Six molecular-cellular biotypes, with rapid-acting-treatment sensitivity

Our map commits to six cross-cutting molecular biotypes as its base coordinates, each defined by mechanism, cross-disorder mapping, and treatment stratification. These are the axes EVIDENT can use to match individuals to rapid-acting interventions.

| Biotype | Mechanism | Rapid-treatment sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| **Low-BDNF / plasticity-deficit** | BDNF-TrkB signaling loss, Val66Met, reduced iPlasticity | Higher-dose or full-hallucinogenic-dose psilocybin engages maximum TrkB affinity (LSD, psilocin bind TrkB ~1,000x tighter than fluoxetine/ketamine, Moliner 2023) |
| **PV-interneuron / gamma-deficit / E-I imbalance** | Parvalbumin function loss, flat EEG aperiodic slope, low 40 Hz ASSR | Zuranolone (GABA-A PAM), PV-targeted TrkB PAMs, closed-loop gamma neurofeedback |
| **Oxidative-stress / mitochondrial** | Low brain GSH, reduced complex I, elevated 8-OHdG, abnormal fNIRS-CCO | N-acetylcysteine (~24-week course), lithium for BD with mitochondrial signature, ketogenic metabolic interventions |
| **Hyperdopaminergic** | Elevated presynaptic striatal DA (Howes Type A) | D2 antagonists/partial agonists; contraindicated where biotype is glutamate-driven (Howes Type B) |
| **Serotonergic (5-HT2A/TrkB dissociable)** | 5-HT2A partial-agonist response separable from TrkB plasticity effect | Classical psilocybin sessions vs. non-hallucinogenic TrkB-PAMs; ondansetron in HTR3A/HTR3B+SLC6A4 alcohol-use subgroup |
| **Inflammatory / cytokine** | IL-6, TNF-alpha elevation; kynurenine pathway shift | Anti-inflammatory augmentation; inflammation-stratified antidepressant trials |

The scientific evidence is not contested. What is missing is the operational layer that (i) measures these coordinates continuously, (ii) updates them from every new experiment and every patient interaction, and (iii) matches each person to the treatment their biotype predicts they will respond to.

## Treatments live across micro, meso, and macro, and the map coordinates them

- **Micro (molecular).** BDNF-TrkB engagers (psilocybin, ketamine, SSRIs, ECT, exercise, VNS), GABA-A PAMs (zuranolone), NMDA modulators, non-hallucinogenic TrkB-PAMs (Olson-lab candidates), inflammation-targeted augmentation.
- **Meso (circuit).** SAINT-style personalized accelerated TMS (~79% remission in TRD at 1-month), closed-loop DBS for OCD, closed-loop gamma neurofeedback, personalized-target fMRI-guided neuromodulation.
- **Macro (systemic).** Sleep, exercise, stress load, social rhythm, and light exposure interventions personalized N-of-1, as in the iMAP work (Mishra 2026), which doubled the standard depression remission rate from ~30% to 55%.

Rapid-acting treatments cut across all three scales. A patient with low-BDNF plasticity deficit *and* PV/gamma deficit *and* elevated inflammation needs a combination the current diagnostic system cannot see, let alone assemble.

## Why this fits EVIDENT / Proactive Health, phases 1-4

- **Phase 1 (objective diagnostics).** Molecular biotypes measured through wearable-feasible readouts: capillary serum BDNF, broadband fNIRS-cytochrome-c-oxidase for mitochondrial state, low-channel EEG aperiodic slope for E-I balance, salivary cortisol for HPA tone, digital-phenotyping vocal and motor markers. Each biotype axis becomes a continuous, longitudinally trackable endpoint.
- **Phase 2 (heterogeneity within diagnosis).** Every DSM category maps onto a mixture of the six biotype axes. Two patients with the same "MDD" label but different biotype signatures need different treatments; the map makes this explicit and testable.
- **Phase 3 (rapid-acting treatment matching).** Baseline biotype signature predicts responder class for ketamine, psilocybin, esketamine, zuranolone, SAINT, and augmentation strategies. Post-treatment biotype trajectory confirms mechanism engagement.
- **Phase 4 (population deployment and real-world evidence).** Continuous longitudinal biotype coordinates provide the real-world evidence engine the April 2026 Executive Order on Accelerating Medical Treatments for Serious Mental Illness explicitly requests. The platform is on-device, AI-native, person-first; psychological data does not leave the device.

## Anchor and evidence base

We ground the map in **penetrant genetic forms** where genotype-to-cellular-phenotype signal is cleanest, in the same move that made neurodegeneration tractable in a dish (APP/PSEN1 for Alzheimer, LRRK2/SNCA for Parkinson). For psychiatry, the anchor is the **22q11.2 deletion (~25x psychosis risk)** and large-effect SCHEMA genes (SETD1A, GRIN2A, CUL1, GRIA3). The generalization then extends to idiopathic schizophrenia and, cautiously, to bipolar disorder through shared cellular and circuit axes.

The consortium (ARPA-H IGoR / PsychIGoR, IPAI/Purdue prime, PI Ananth Grama; Cytognosis Foundation TA1/TA2 sub-lead; Matt Tegtmeyer lab at Purdue; Anne Carpenter at IPAI/Purdue for computational morphology; Brad Ruzicka at McLean/HMS for clinical translation; SIFT for TA3 protocol interoperability; Cellanome for live-cell perturbation; Illumina for perturbation-scale sequencing) is fully assembled. The IGoR Solution Summary was submitted 2026-06-25; the $42.9M full proposal is due 2026-08-13.
