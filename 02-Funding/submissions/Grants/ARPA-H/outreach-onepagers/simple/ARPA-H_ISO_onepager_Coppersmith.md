## The three gaps EVIDENT and we agree on

- **No objective diagnostics.** Psychiatry is the only field of medicine that selects treatment from a 30-minute symptom interview. No blood test, no scan in clinical use.
- **Heterogeneity is masked.** Two patients with the same DSM label often have biologically different illness. First-line antidepressant response is 42-53%. STAR\*D cumulative relapse hits 71% by step 4.
- **Rapid-acting treatments outrun the matching infrastructure.** Ketamine, psilocybin, esketamine, zuranolone, SAINT all arrived without a way to say who responds.

## What we build

A **continuous, mechanistic, transdiagnostic coordinate system** across **micro (molecular), meso (circuit), macro (behavior)**. Six molecular-cellular biotypes are the base axes:

| Biotype | Rapid-treatment sensitivity |
|---|---|
| **Low-BDNF / plasticity-deficit** | Full-dose psilocybin, LSD, ketamine engage TrkB (LSD, psilocin bind TrkB ~1000x tighter than fluoxetine) |
| **PV / gamma / E-I imbalance** | Zuranolone, PV-targeted TrkB PAMs, closed-loop gamma neurofeedback |
| **Oxidative-stress / mitochondrial** | NAC, lithium (mitochondrial BD), ketogenic diet, wearable fNIRS-CCO tracks state |
| **Hyperdopaminergic** | D2 antagonists / partial agonists; contraindicated in glutamate-driven TRD |
| **Serotonergic (5-HT2A/TrkB dissociable)** | Classical psychedelics vs. non-hallucinogenic TrkB-PAMs; ondansetron subgroup |
| **Inflammatory / cytokine** | Inflammation-stratified augmentation |

Each axis is **objective, longitudinally measurable, and treatment-relevant**. Wearable readouts include capillary BDNF, broadband **fNIRS-CCO** (mitochondrial), **EEG aperiodic slope** (E-I), salivary cortisol, digital phenotyping.

## Treatments live across scales, and the map coordinates them

- **Micro.** Psilocybin, ketamine, zuranolone, SSRIs, NAC, non-hallucinogenic TrkB-PAMs.
- **Meso.** SAINT TMS (~79% remission in TRD), closed-loop DBS, personalized neuromodulation.
- **Macro.** iMAP-style N-of-1 lifestyle personalization (Mishra 2026 doubled remission from ~30% to 55%).
