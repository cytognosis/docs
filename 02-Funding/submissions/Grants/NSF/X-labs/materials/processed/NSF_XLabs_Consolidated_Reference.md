## 2. Program Evolution: From Tech Labs RFI to X-Labs

The program began as "NSF Tech Labs" with a Request for Information in December 2025 seeking public input. It was rebranded as **NSF X-Labs** in the final announcement. Key evolution:

| Aspect | Tech Labs RFI (Dec 2025) | X-Labs OTASO (FY26) |
|--------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| **Name** | Tech Labs | X-Labs |
| **Status** | Concept/RFI | Official solicitation |
| **Focus** | "Accelerate practical research with real-world applications" | "Build and accelerate novel platform technologies capable of unlocking entirely new sectors" |
| **Team description** | "Full-time research, development and innovation (RDI) teams" | "Full-time research and development (R&D) teams focused on use-inspired scientific breakthroughs" |
| **Phase 0 duration** | 9 months | 9–12 months |
| **Phase 0 funding** | Not specified | Up to $1,500,000 |
| **Phase 1 funding** | $10M–$50M per team per year | Up to $50M per team per year |
| **Phase 1 duration** | 24 months | 24–36 months |
| **Team size** | "10-50 members" in Phase 1 | Not specified (flexibility) |
| **Strategic objectives** | 5 objectives (Build Platforms, Beyond Papers, De-risk Tech, Market Needs, Partnerships) | 2 objectives (Build Sector-Defining Platforms, Develop New Organizational Structures) |
| **Topics** | "quantum, AI, critical materials, semiconductors, biotechnology" | Released via separate Topic Announcements |
| **IP** | Discussed in questions | Full internal control required (Autonomy Factor Test) |
| **Independence test** | General expectation | Formal 6-point Autonomy Factor Test (Section 6.1.1) |
| **Selection** | "Lightweight abstract proposals (5–8 pages)" | "Written Proposals (8 pages)" + Oral Proposals |
| **Added in final** | — | Research security requirements (CHIPS Act), COI requirements, detailed milestone structure, Governance Structure Plan |

### Key Shifts in Emphasis

1. **From translation to platform technology**: The RFI emphasized "commercialization" and "technology translation." The final X-Labs OTASO pivots to "novel platform technologies" and "use-inspired scientific breakthroughs."
2. **Stronger autonomy requirements**: The final version adds the Autonomy Factor Test with 6 explicit conditions (decision-making, funding control, research direction, IP ownership, hiring, independent boards).
3. **Milestone structure formalized**: Phase 1 milestones split into Reporting Milestones (quarterly ops) and Progress Milestones (significant technical advances), with Progress payments exceeding Reporting payments.
4. **Research security**: Extensive CHIPS Act compliance requirements added (foreign entity of concern, malign foreign talent programs, proscribed parties lists).

## 3. Program Overview

### 3.1 Mission and Vision

NSF X-Labs represent a **bold, flexible, and outcomes-driven initiative** designed to build and accelerate novel platform technologies capable of unlocking entirely new sectors, including new fields of scientific inquiry. The program supports full-time R&D teams focused on **use-inspired scientific breakthroughs** and **foundational platform technologies** that traditional university and industry labs cannot easily address.

### 3.2 Strategic Objectives

1. **Build Sector-Defining Platforms** — Develop novel platform technologies that serve as the foundation for future innovations, novel engineering solutions, and/or scientific breakthroughs. These technologies should foster transformation beyond traditional outputs (publications and datasets) and accelerate innovations within relevant application domains.

2. **Develop New Organizational Research Structures** — Foster organizations with the structure, resources, and flexibility necessary to tackle large-scale scientific advancements. These organizations should lead and leverage novel partnerships across industry, philanthropic, and academic sectors.

### 3.3 What is IN Scope

Examples of high-impact outcomes:
- Development of foundational platform technologies that enable significant downstream breakthroughs in one or more sectors
- Solving a specific technological challenge or bottleneck that creates or reshapes entire fields of scientific inquiry
- Early-stage prototypes not otherwise funded by industry that accelerate novel technology capabilities

Examples of existing platform technologies cited: VLSI, the Internet, PCR, brain-computing interfaces, next-generation sequencing, AI models for protein structure prediction, LEDs.

### 3.4 What is NOT in Scope

- Advancement of multiple research arms without a clear Mission for high-impact outcomes
- Incremental advancement of the state of the art
- Advancement of technologies with substantial existing industry/VC investment
- Development of testbeds or data centers as primary focus
- Advancement where sole challenges are non-technical

## 4. Topic 2: Scientific Instrumentation for Sensing and Imaging

### 4.1 Topic Vision

> "Every revolution in science has been preceded by a revolution in what we can measure."

The frontier is **starved for radically new modalities** for sensing and imaging. Current limitations:
- Cannot watch a non-crystalline enzyme work at atomic resolution
- Cannot probe the full dynamics of a working synapse
- Cannot identify the most reactive surface defect structures on advanced catalytic materials

### 4.2 Technical Areas

Teams might draw on:
- **Quantum sensing**
- **AI-driven computational imaging**
- **Adaptive AI-based sensing algorithms**
- **Entirely new modalities** to redefine what we consider knowable

### 4.3 Example R&D Challenges

| Challenge | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Molecular-scale single-reaction events | Detection across timescales of femtoseconds to seconds |
| MRI-free deep-tissue imaging | Alternative modalities for deep tissue visualization |
| Non-destructive biomolecule microscopy | Exquisite resolution without sample destruction |
| High-sensitivity quantum sensors | Suitable for operation in a variety of environments |
| AI-native instruments | Intentionally engineered for next-generation AI training pipelines |
| Whole-brain activity | Cellular resolution across long timescales |

### 4.4 What is NOT in Scope for Topic 2

- Computational/software solutions without practical integration into instrumentation
- Technologies with narrow, non-deployable impact
- Fundamental research without platform technology potential
- Incremental advancement of state of the art
- Technologies already at full-scale commercialization

### 5.2 Phase 1 Milestone Types

| Type | Purpose | Payment Model |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **Reporting Milestones** | Quarterly reports and NSF meetings | Regular funding for ops |
| **Progress Milestones** | Significant technical progress with quantitative exit criteria | Higher payments; must substantially exceed Reporting |

Teams are expected to propose high-risk, high-reward progress. NSF anticipates even effective teams may not achieve all Progress Milestones. Teams that demonstrate substantial progress are competitive for Phase 2 (not all Progress Milestones required).

## 6. Written Proposal Requirements

### 6.1 Format

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Page limit | 8 single-sided pages (excluding COI statement) |
| Font | 12-point, single-spaced |
| Paper size | 8.5" × 11" with 1" margins |
| Tables/graphs | May use smaller font |
| Format | PDF, no cover letter |
| COI statement | Separate PDF, no page limit |
| Template | Use Attachment A (Written Proposal Template) |

### 6.2 Required Content Sections

1. **Mission** — Describe one or more novel platform technologies the X-Lab is uniquely suited to advance. Clear vision of how the work reshapes a field or sector. Explain how the Mission is unmet by existing structures.

2. **Technology Landscape** — Current state-of-the-art analysis. Justify how the platform technology would accelerate new technologies and research. Quantitative plot/figure recommended.

3. **Outcomes** — High-impact, measurable outcomes over a 5–7 year timescale. Scientific and technical performance benchmarks.

4. **Senior/Key Personnel Qualifications** — Technical expertise, past efforts, qualifications. Include all Senior/Key Personnel including those not at lead org.

5. **Team Capabilities Statement** — How leadership team brings complementary expertise in strategic leadership, technical expertise, and/or entrepreneurship. Intended governance structure for Phase 0 and Phase 1. Networks, partnerships, capital resources.

6. **Conflicts of Interest** — Potential financial interests, mitigation plans. Applies to lead org, employees, subcontractors.

## 7. Selection Criteria

### 7.1 Written Proposal Criteria (Weighted Roughly Equally)

**Team Qualifications and Structure:**
- Complementary expertise in scientific, technical, interdisciplinary, and strategic leadership
- Record of success
- Governance structure appropriate for proposed work
- Prepared to leverage novel partnerships
- Provides autonomy and flexibility

**Mission Clarity and Outcomes:**
- Appropriately ambitious, technically sound, well-defined
- Technology landscape justifies dedicated full-time team
- Aligned with NSF X-Labs strategic objectives

### 7.2 Oral Proposal Criteria (Weighted Roughly Equally)

**Team Qualifications and Structure:**
- Same as written, plus:
- Proposed Phase 0 Budget reflects reasonable resource allocation
- Full understanding of X-Labs initiative goals

**Mission Clarity and Outcomes:**
- Same as written, plus:
- Phase 0 and Phase 1 activities well-defined and ambitious
- Clear rationale for how activities achieve Mission outcomes
- Phase 0 Milestone Plan appropriate and addresses all required milestones

## 8. Eligibility and Autonomy

### 8.1 Who Can Apply

Any domestic responsible entity. Must be SAM-registered (www.sam.gov).

### 8.2 Autonomy Factor Test (6 Conditions)

An NSF X-Labs team must demonstrate ALL of the following:

1. **Decision-making independence** — Leadership structure allowing decisions in days, not weeks, independent of another organization
2. **Funding control** — Full internal control of funding allocation; no parent institution approval needed
3. **Research direction control** — Full control of research directions and partnerships; ability to shift technical approach without parent approval
4. **IP ownership** — Full ownership and control of IP produced; parent institution must NOT maintain IP ownership
5. **Hiring autonomy** — Full control of hiring, salary negotiation, contracting without parent approval
6. **Board independence** — Leadership boards must be independent; not shared with parent institution

### 8.3 Senior/Key Personnel Requirements

- Must contribute substantively to development/execution
- Core leadership must be **full-time** by Phase 1 start
- Listed on max 1 proposal per Topic Announcement
- Addition/substitution requires NSF review/approval

### 8.4 Research Security (CHIPS Act Compliance)

Ineligible entities and individuals:
- U.S. proscribed parties (BIS Entity List, OFAC, DoS sanctions, FCC covered equipment)
- Foreign entities of concern (DPRK, PRC, Russia, Iran jurisdictions)
- Citizens of DPRK, PRC, Russia, Iran as Senior/Key Personnel
- Participants in Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Programs

## 11. Key Differences from Standard NSF Grants

| Feature | Standard NSF Grant | NSF X-Labs OT |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Instrument | Cooperative Agreement / Grant | Other Transaction contract |
| Regulations | FAR-based | Non-FAR |
| Payments | Cost-reimbursement or fixed-price | Milestone-based |
| IP | University retains (Bayh-Dole) | Team must have full ownership and control |
| Duration | Typically 3–5 years | Multi-phase, potentially 7+ years |
| Team structure | PI + students/postdocs | Dedicated full-time team (autonomy required) |
| Governance | Within university structure | Independent entity required by Phase 1 |
| Scale | $100K–$5M typical | Up to $50M/year |
| Review | Merit review panels | Two-stage: Written + Oral evaluation |
| Flexibility | Limited budget reallocation | Full internal budget control |
