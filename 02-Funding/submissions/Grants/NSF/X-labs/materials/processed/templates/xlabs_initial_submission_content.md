# NSF X-Labs Initial Submission: Content Guide and Instructions

> Source: NSF-OTASO-FY26-XLabsInitiative (Main Solicitation) and Attachment A (Written Proposal Template)

---

## Document Overview

The NSF X-Labs Written Proposal is a **Phase 0 entry point** into a multi-phase funding program that awards Other Transaction (OT) contracts for developing novel platform technologies. This is a two-step competitive evaluation: Written Proposals are reviewed first, and a subset of teams are invited to submit Oral Proposal Packages.

### Key Facts

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | Other Transaction (OT) Agreement |
| **Phase 0 Duration** | 9-12 months |
| **Phase 0 Funding** | Up to $1,500,000 per team |
| **Phase 1 Duration** | 24-36 months |
| **Full Program Horizon** | 5-7 years (Phase 0 through Phase 2+) |
| **Payment Structure** | Milestone-based payments (quarterly schedule) |
| **Award Type** | Non-FAR OT contracts |

## Section-by-Section Guidance

### Section 1: Mission

**Purpose:** Establish the team's core vision and the platform technology gap being addressed.

**What NSF wants to see:**

- One or more novel platform technologies the proposed X-Lab is uniquely suited to advance
- A clear vision statement showing how the work will reshape an entire field of scientific inquiry or technology sector
- Explanation of why this Mission is currently unmet by existing organizational structures and funding mechanisms

**Examples of platform technologies NSF considers high-impact (Section 7.0):**

- Very Large-Scale Integration (VLSI)
- The Internet
- Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)
- Brain-computing interfaces
- Next-generation sequencing
- AI models for protein structure prediction
- Light-Emitting Diodes (LEDs)

**Do's:**

- Be specific about the platform technology and its transformative potential
- Articulate a clear end-state that reshapes a field
- Explain the structural gap: why existing institutions, funding, or industry cannot accomplish this Mission
- Frame outcomes as use-inspired research (Pasteur's Quadrant)

**Don'ts:**

- Propose incremental advancement of existing state of the art
- Propose technologies with substantial existing industry/VC investment
- Propose multiple unfocused research arms without a unified Mission
- Propose testbeds or data centers as the primary deliverable
- Propose platforms where the sole challenges are non-technical

### Section 2: Technology Landscape

**Purpose:** Demonstrate deep understanding of the current technology state and justify the need for a dedicated X-Lab team.

**What NSF wants to see:**

- Analysis of the current technology landscape
- Comparison of current state-of-the-art technology
- Clear description of how the team will progress the technology and unlock fundamentally new capabilities
- Quantitative plot or figure depicting the landscape and desired goals (recommended)

**Do's:**

- Include a quantitative figure showing the current technology frontier and the team's target
- Compare specific performance metrics across existing approaches
- Identify the critical bottleneck that prevents progress
- Show how the proposed platform technology enables downstream breakthroughs

**Don'ts:**

- Provide a generic literature review without quantitative comparisons
- Omit the technology gap analysis
- Fail to distinguish your approach from incremental improvements
- Ignore competing approaches or overlapping efforts

### Section 3: Outcomes

**Purpose:** Define ambitious, measurable outcomes over a 5-7 year timescale.

**What NSF wants to see:**

- High-impact, measurable outcomes that advance a nascent platform technology
- Outcomes that overcome critical bottlenecks
- Scientific and technical performance benchmarks
- Clear alignment with NSF X-Labs strategic objectives (solicitation Section 3.0)

**Outcome categories that NSF considers high-impact (Section 7.0):**

1. Development of foundational platform technologies enabling significant downstream breakthroughs in one or more sectors
2. Solving a specific technological challenge or bottleneck that creates or reshapes entire fields of scientific inquiry
3. Early-stage prototypes not otherwise funded by industry that accelerate novel technology capabilities

**Do's:**

- Define outcomes that are quantitative and verifiable
- Include specific performance benchmarks with measurable targets
- Tie outcomes to the Mission and platform technology
- Show how outcomes reshape the field, not just advance it incrementally
- Include a 5-7 year timeline with intermediate milestones

**Don'ts:**

- List only publication or dataset outputs (NSF expects outcomes beyond papers)
- Propose vague or unmeasurable outcomes
- Propose outcomes that overlap with substantial industry investment
- Ignore the 5-7 year timescale requirement

### Section 4: Senior/Key Personnel Qualifications

**Purpose:** Demonstrate the team has the expertise to execute the Mission.

**What NSF wants to see:**

- Technical expertise relevant to the platform technology
- Experience commercializing technology
- Past efforts and track record of success
- Qualifications for ALL Senior/Key Personnel, including those outside the lead organization

**Personnel rules:**

- Senior/Key Personnel must be dedicated full-time by Phase 1 start (unless approved by NSF)
- Personnel may be listed on a maximum of one proposal per Topic Announcement
- Addition or substitution of Senior/Key Personnel requires NSF review/approval
- Personnel must comply with CHIPS and Science Act Section 10636 and 10632 (research security requirements)

**Do's:**

- Highlight complementary expertise across the team
- Include entrepreneurship and commercialization experience
- Cover all team members, even those at partner organizations
- Demonstrate a record of success in relevant domains
- Show interdisciplinary strength

**Don'ts:**

- List only academic credentials without demonstrating execution capability
- Omit team members at partner institutions
- Include personnel who appear on restricted party lists (see solicitation Section 6.3)
- Include individuals who are party to a Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Program

### Section 5: Team Capabilities Statement

**Purpose:** Show how the team collectively has the structure, partnerships, and governance to execute.

**What NSF wants to see:**

- How Senior/Key Personnel bring complementary expertise in strategic leadership, technical expertise, and entrepreneurship
- How the team demonstrates experience, adaptability, and collaborative approach
- Intended governance structure and autonomy during Phase 0 and Phase 1
- Overview of existing networks, partnerships, and capital resources

**Autonomy Factor Test (Section 6.1.1) — The team must demonstrate by Phase 1:**

| Autonomy Factor | Requirement |
|----------------|-------------|
| Decision-making | Independent, in days not weeks |
| Funding allocation | Full internal control, no parent institution approval |
| Research direction | Full internal control to shift technical approach without parent approval |
| Intellectual Property | Full ownership and control, parent institution must not own or control |
| Hiring and contracting | Full internal control without parent institution approval |
| Leadership boards | Independent, not shared with parent institution |

**Do's:**

- Address the Autonomy Factor Test explicitly in the governance description
- Describe the legal entity type planned for Phase 1
- Include partnerships across industry, government, academia, and philanthropy
- Show how capital resources will be deployed in support of the Mission
- Describe advisory board composition if applicable

**Don'ts:**

- Propose a governance structure that depends on a parent institution for key decisions
- Omit governance details for Phase 0 vs. Phase 1 transition
- Ignore the autonomy requirements
- Fail to address how partnerships will be leveraged

## Conflicts of Interest Statement (Separate PDF)

**Purpose:** Disclose and mitigate potential financial conflicts.

**Requirements (Section 10.2):**

- Separate PDF document, no page limit
- Describe potential financial interests of lead organization and Senior/Key Personnel
- Describe how conflicts will be avoided, neutralized, or mitigated
- Applies to lead organization, employees, and any subcontractors/consultants
- Post-award OCIs must be promptly disclosed to NSF with a mitigation plan
- Use FAR Subpart 9.5 as a framework for identifying OCIs

## Written Proposal Selection Criteria (Section 11.0)

Both criteria are weighted **roughly equally** in the evaluation.

### Criterion 1: Team Qualifications and Structure (Section 11.1)

- The extent to which the team's Senior/Key Personnel bring together necessary and complementary expertise in scientific, technical, interdisciplinary, and strategic leadership to execute their Mission; the extent to which the team's Senior/Key Personnel have demonstrated a record of success
- The extent to which the team's intended governance structure in Phase 1 is appropriate for proposed work, is prepared to leverage novel partnerships across industry, government, academia, and philanthropy, and provides the structure, resources, autonomy, and flexibility necessary for the NSF X-Lab to tackle large-scale scientific advancements

### Criterion 2: Mission Clarity and Outcomes (Section 11.2)

- The extent to which the proposed Mission and outcomes are appropriately ambitious, technically sound, and well-defined
- The extent to which the technology landscape justifies a dedicated, full-time team to develop the proposed platform technology and achieve high-impact breakthroughs in the Mission area
- The extent to which the Mission and outcomes are aligned with the strategic objectives of the NSF X-Labs initiative (see solicitation Section 3.0)

## Post-Written Proposal: What Comes Next

Teams selected from the Written Proposal stage will be invited to submit an **Oral Proposal Package** (Section 12.0), which includes:

| Document | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Oral Presentation Slides | Mission, outcomes, Phase 0/1 activities, team overview, budget summary |
| Phase 0 Milestone Plan | Milestone and deliverables table with task descriptions |
| Senior/Key Personnel Disclosures | SciENcv biographical sketches and current & pending support (due ~48 hours after invitation) |
| Phase 0 Budget | Budget and justification by category (up to $1,500,000) |
| Letters of Collaboration | Signed one-page letters from proposed collaborators/subcontractors |

### Required Phase 0 Milestone Deliverables (Section 9.1)

| Deliverable | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Team Kickoff Meeting | Meet with NSF in first month of Phase 0 |
| Data Management and Privacy Plan | 2-page limit; data-sharing plans across team, with NSF, and publicly |
| IP Management Plan | How IP will be identified, protected, managed; must support broad adoption and U.S. competitiveness |
| Research Security Management Plan | Safeguarding data, IP, personnel, and systems |
| Governance Structure Plan | Legal entity type, Autonomy Factor Test justification, leadership structure |
| Proposed Phase 1 Milestone Plan | Milestone and deliverables table for Phase 1 |
| Proposed Phase 1 Budget | Three tiers (essential, mid-range, comprehensive) with justifications |
| Updated Senior/Key Personnel | Updated qualifications from Phase 0 proposal |

## Strategic Objectives Alignment (Solicitation Section 3.0)

When demonstrating alignment, reference NSF's strategic objectives for the X-Labs initiative:

- Driving use-inspired research and developing novel platform technologies
- Serving as the foundation for future innovation and breakthroughs
- Leveraging new organizational research structures to accelerate emergence of new technologies
- Advancing fields of use-inspired research at a pace and scale not achievable through traditional mechanisms

## Eligibility Notes (Section 6.0)

- Lead organization must be registered in SAM.gov (registration may take up to 10 days)
- NSF contracts with one lead organization only; subcontracts with partners are permitted
- All entity types are eligible (academia, industry, nonprofits, etc.)
- Must comply with research security requirements under CHIPS and Science Act of 2022
- Foreign entities of concern and individuals from DPRK, PRC, Russia, or Iran are ineligible as Senior/Key Personnel
