### Post-Residency Vision

Two goals.

**(1) Helix Framework.** A community commentary I have been developing that takes the ~5-year mark since FROs were introduced as the moment to look back at what the field has learned across FROs, ARPA-like agencies, and hybrid for-profit / nonprofit models, and proposes (a) shared boilerplate (federated DUAs, evaluation harnesses, reproducibility tooling, licensing patterns) that removes the IP and operational friction blocking new cross-institution collaborations (building on Wellcome Leap MARFA / CORFA), and (b) organizational structures intertwining nonprofit mission-alignment with for-profit scalability from Day 1, not Year 5. Co-developed with Astera (Prachee Avasthi), Convergent Research (Adam Marblestone), Speculative Tech (Ben Reinhardt).

**(2) Five-year platform funding plus first proprietary clinical study.** Highest-priority targets: NSF Tech Labs RFP (independent or with Convergent Research as an FRO; based on the RFI, this is the opportunity that best aligns with our goals, values, and stage, though final assessment depends on the call), and an ARPA-H PHO Mission Office Solution Summary. Residency deliverables provide the primary data those proposals need.

Cytognosis Foundation remains the open-source steward; a future PBC subsidiary may commercialize specific clinical-decision-support tools, but only on top of the open core, never by closing it.

## Q3. Risk Assessment

Risks framed patient-first; each leads with the patient consequence, then states the methodological mitigation. The Patient Advocacy Council is a release-gate reviewer for patient-facing deliverables.

| | Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **1** | **Patients from underrepresented ancestries get the worst molecular predictions** (modeling failure mode + equity failure mode). PGC and HiTOP benchmarks lean European-ancestry; AlphaGenome-derived dysregulation predictions inherit imbalance from PsychENCODE and other source cohorts. | Stratify NBB validation by ancestry sub-cohort; ship ancestry-disaggregated demographic-performance cards as a hard release gate with every checkpoint. Coordinate cross-ancestry validation with NeuroGAP-Psychosis (Ethiopia, Kenya, South Africa, Uganda) and H3Africa where DUAs permit. Patient Advocacy Council reviews fairness audits before any external delivery; ASAM and APA Council on Digital Health partners specifically vet ancestry-stratified outputs for the populations they represent. |
| **2** | **Patients are categorized by dimensional axes that don't replicate.** Learned axes work within a training cohort but don't recover the PGC 14-disorder five-factor on independent samples. The entire claim of dimensional psychiatry rests on this. | NBB-internal leave-one-cohort-out across the six NBB sub-biobanks runs as a hard Go / No-Go cross-validation gate, with pre-registered thresholds (Frobenius distance, off-diagonal correlation, eigen-spectrum alignment vs Grotzinger 2026). Apply for [Fondation FondaMental open-science research data](https://www.fondation-fondamental.org/en/our-actions/open-science-access-to-research-data) as an independent external validation cohort. If gates miss, publish negative result plus diagnostic decomposition (which factors recovered, which did not, which cohort effects detected) as a bioRxiv preprint and Zenodo bundle; reframe as multi-target validation against PGC and HiTOP independently rather than single pass / fail. |
| **3** | **Patients lose access to the model because controlled-access cohorts can't be openly released.** NBB, PsychENCODE WGS, and restricted ENIGMA working groups must remain controlled-access; weights and derivatives may inadvertently re-encode individual-level information. Some institutions also expect journal-tracked outputs as productivity evidence. | Apply the Cytognosis controlled-access release pattern, Astera-OSP-aligned: code MIT, weights from open data CC0, weights from controlled-access cohorts under OpenRAIL-M (Astera FAQ data-embargo exception, in consultation with Prachee Avasthi); raw data stays gated behind existing DUAs; CC0 synthetic-data substitutes ship alongside every release for end-to-end DUA-free reproduction. Membership-inference and differential-privacy probes gate every release. PAC reviews patient-facing language and re-identification risk before publication. Consortium institutions may re-publish through journal venues using non-Astera time and funds. |
| **4** | **Patients face delays from compute capacity ceilings.** AlphaGenome embedding generation across NBB 10K+ donors plus Phase-3 five-objective joint optimization plus full ablation can saturate H100 reservation windows during Months 12-18. | Stage compute hot-spots: schedule embedding generation in early Phase 2 (Months 6-9) so it does not collide with the Phase-3 optimization peak.
