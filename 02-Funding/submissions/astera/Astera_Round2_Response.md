### Post-Residency Vision

My residency goals are twofold.

**(1) Organizational structure for impact-first science.** I will use the residency to advance the Helix Framework, a community commentary I have been developing that takes the ~5-year mark since the introduction of Focused Research Organizations as the moment to look back at what the field has learned across FROs, ARPA-like agencies, and hybrid for-profit / nonprofit models, and to propose a path forward. Two specific arguments: (a) shared boilerplate that removes the IP and operational friction currently blocking new cross-institution collaborations (building on Wellcome Leap's MARFA / CORFA innovation rather than re-implementing it each time), and (b) organizational structures that intertwine the mission-alignment of nonprofits with the scalability of for-profits from Day 1, rather than retrofitting scale at Year 5. Co-developed with Astera Open Science (Prachee Avasthi), Convergent Research (Adam Marblestone), and Speculative Tech (Ben Reinhardt).

**(2) Five-year funding for platform development and the first proprietary trial.** I will use the residency to apply for mission-aligned, moonshot-supporting funding covering the next 5 years of multi-scale platform development, technology de-risking, and the first proprietary clinical study generating the multimodal data we need to jointly fine-tune these models. Two highest-priority targets: NSF Tech Labs when the RFP posts (either independently or with Convergent Research as an FRO; based on the RFI, this is the opportunity that best aligns with our goals, values, and stage, though final assessment depends on the call), and a Solution Summary to the ARPA-H Proactive Health Office (PHO) Mission Office ISO. The residency-year deliverables provide the primary data those proposals need, not promises.

Cytognosis Foundation remains the open-source steward; a future public benefit corporation subsidiary may commercialize specific clinical-decision-support tools, but only on top of the open core, never by closing it.

## Q3. Risk Assessment

| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **1** | **Cross-cohort generalization fails.** Learned axes replicate within PsychENCODE 388 paired donors but do not recover the PGC 14-disorder five-factor structure when recomputed on NBB-aggregated samples. The entire claim of dimensional psychiatry rests on this. | Run NBB-internal replication as a hard Go / No-Go gate, not a downstream sanity check, with pre-registered thresholds (Frobenius distance, off-diagonal correlation, eigen-spectrum alignment vs Grotzinger 2026). The commentary is co-authored with Astera Open Science (Prachee Avasthi), Convergent Research (Adam Marblestone), and Speculative Tech (Ben Reinhardt) and publishes during the residency year as a public good benefiting Astera's broader open-science mission directly.
