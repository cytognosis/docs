### Post-Residency Vision

| Goal | Description |
| :---- | :---- |
| **(1) Lasting collaboration with Astera and peer impact-centric organizations** | The residency seeds a lasting collaboration between Cytognosis Foundation, Astera, and peer impact-centric organizations (Convergent Research, Speculative Tech, others) working in **Pasteur's quadrant** (use-inspired basic research aimed at health challenges with global impact), in the spirit of the [Wellcome Leap LEAP innovation model](https://wellcomeleap.org/leap-innovation-model/). The residency-year commentary (drafted at M6, published openly at M12) reviews what FROs, ARPA-H, and Wellcome Leap-style funding models have actually delivered over the past several years, and proposes a path forward for the next generation of moonshot-funding organizations operating on health-related challenges with global impact. The collaboration is meant to dynamically revise this vision over time, working together. The **M12 published outcomes establish the initial organizational direction and structure for Cytognosis Foundation**, anchoring the Foundation's post-residency posture in the same shared framework. |
| **(2) Financial stability and scaling independence** | Sustainable scaling depends on financial independence: the Foundation will pursue follow-on funding for the post-residency phase. The most aligned target on our radar is **NSF Tech Labs** (we will apply when the call is open). Beyond grant funding, the Foundation envisions a **periodic open-model-update cadence** (every 6 to 12 months), with all updates openly released; the goal is for clinical-grade biotyping applications to adopt these continuously updated models. A future public benefit corporation subsidiary may commercialize specific clinical-decision-support tools but only on top of the open core, never by closing it. |

## Q3. Risk Assessment

| | Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **1** | **Patients from underrepresented ancestries get the worst molecular predictions** (modeling failure mode + equity failure mode). PGC benchmarks lean European-ancestry; AlphaGenome-derived predictions inherit imbalance from PsychENCODE. | Stratify NeuroBioBank validation by ancestry sub-cohort; ancestry-disaggregated demographic-performance cards as a hard release gate. Cross-ancestry validation with NeuroGAP-Psychosis (Ethiopia, Kenya, South Africa, Uganda) and H3Africa where DUAs permit. Pan-UKBB summary statistics as a complementary M4 cross-ancestry reference for genotypic axes. External fairness audit (Consultant Pool) reviews ancestry-stratified outputs before any external delivery. |
| **2** | **Patients are categorized by dimensional axes that don't replicate** (within-cohort axes don't recover the field-standard genetic factor structure on independent samples; genotypic axes don't generalize to broader phenotypes). | Hard Go / No-Go gate: leave-one-cohort-out validation across the six NeuroBioBank sub-biobanks; genotypic embeddings must organize diseases consistently with the field-standard genetic factor structure (modularity Q > 0.3, permutation p < 0.05) on at least 4 of 6 folds; the cross-cohort robustness gate at M4 requires left-out-cohort prediction accuracy or metric-loss recovery within 20% of the pooled-training baseline. Cross-ancestry validation against Pan-UKBB summary statistics. Apply for [Fondation FondaMental open-science research data](https://www.fondation-fondamental.org/en/our-actions/open-science-access-to-research-data) as an independent external validation cohort. If gates miss: bioRxiv negative-result + diagnostic decomposition (which factors recovered, which did not, which cohort effects detected); multi-target validation across PGC + Pan-UKBB independently. |
| **3** | **The model can't be openly released because controlled-access cohorts can't be openly redistributed** (NeuroBioBank, PsychENCODE WGS must remain controlled-access; weights may inadvertently re-encode individual-level information). | Cytognosis controlled-access release pattern, Astera-OSP-aligned: code MIT; weights from open data CC0; weights from controlled-access cohorts under OpenRAIL-M (Astera FAQ data-embargo exception, in consultation with Prachee Avasthi). Raw data stays under DUAs. CC0 synthetic-data substitutes ship alongside every release for end-to-end DUA-free reproduction. Membership-inference and differential-privacy probes gate every release. Privacy / re-identification audit (Consultant Pool) reviews patient-facing language and re-identification risk pre-publication. Consortium institutions may re-publish through journals using non-Astera time and funds. |
| **4** | **Patients face delays from compute capacity ceilings** (AlphaGenome fine-tune + disease-aware genomic model training + ablations can exceed initial compute allocation during M12-18). | Stage compute hot-spots: schedule embedding generation early Phase 2 (M6-9) so it does not collide with the Phase-3 optimization peak. Portable training harnesses from Day 1 let Astera reallocate across substrates without code rewrites. Purdue IPAI fallback through Senior Advisor (Grama) advisory effort, funded via the Google.org Round-2 subaward to avoid duplication. |
