## Q3. Risk Assessment

Risks framed patient-first; each leads with the patient consequence, then states the methodological mitigation. The Patient Advocacy Council is a release-gate reviewer for patient-facing deliverables.

| | Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **1** | **Patients from underrepresented ancestries get the worst molecular predictions** (modeling failure mode + equity failure mode). PGC and HiTOP benchmarks lean European-ancestry; AlphaGenome-derived variant-effect predictions inherit imbalance from PsychENCODE and other source cohorts. | Stratify NBB validation by ancestry sub-cohort; ship ancestry-disaggregated demographic-performance cards as a hard release gate with every checkpoint. Coordinate cross-ancestry validation with NeuroGAP-Psychosis (Ethiopia, Kenya, South Africa, Uganda) and H3Africa where DUAs permit. Use Pan-UKBB sum stats as a complementary cross-ancestry validation reference for genotypic axes (M4). Patient Advocacy Council reviews fairness audits before any external delivery; ASAM and APA Council on Digital Health partners specifically vet ancestry-stratified outputs for the populations they represent. |
| **2** | **Patients are categorized by dimensional axes that don't replicate.** Learned axes work within a training cohort but don't recover the PGC 14-disorder five-factor on independent samples, or genotypic axes don't generalize to broader phenotypes. | NBB-internal leave-one-cohort-out across the six NBB sub-biobanks runs as a hard Go / No-Go cross-validation gate, with pre-registered thresholds (Frobenius distance, off-diagonal correlation, eigen-spectrum alignment vs Grotzinger 2026 PGC five-factor). Genotypic axes additionally validated against Pan-UKBB sum stats for broader-phenotype coverage. **Patient Advocacy Council, why and how.** Models that don't reach patients don't reduce suffering. Too many open-science deliverables ship and shelve: published, indexed, ignored. The Patient Advocacy Council is our structural defense against that outcome. It convenes representatives from One Mind (transdiagnostic), the Center for Digital Mental Health (Harvard / MGH, 125-plus scientist-clinician network), the APA Council on Digital Health, Innovation, and Technology, and ASAM (SUD-specific privacy and adoption), so engagement is structurally aligned with the communities the work serves rather than relying on individual patient advocates. Three engagement stages are funded explicitly in this budget. Stage 1 (M1 / 3m) gathers patient-priority requirements, fairness concerns, adoption barriers, and clinician-facing communication standards that gate every release. Stage 2 (M3 / 12m) delivers a partner-only prototype preview with structured feedback sessions surfacing translational and adoption blockers. Stage 3 (M4 / 18m) supports translational-research pilots through Council partner organizations, feeding adoption telemetry and clinician feedback back into a v1.1 refinement cycle. Each stage produces a CC BY 4.0 brief that becomes a formal release-gate document for everything downstream. The same Council is engaged through the Google.org Round-2 track, ensuring one coherent patient-advocacy interface across the full Neuroverse program.

**Helix Framework rationale.** Openness as a stated principle is now standard in our field; openness as functioning practice that delivers patient impact is rarer. The Helix Framework community commentary, a residency-year output, takes the ~5-year mark since Focused Research Organizations were introduced as the moment to look back at what FROs, ARPA-like agencies, and hybrid for-profit / nonprofit models have actually delivered, and to propose a path forward that gives impact-first organizations targeting moonshots their best chance of thriving, scaling, and reaching patients. Two arguments. (1) The field needs shared boilerplate, not re-derived legal and operational scaffolding for every cross-institution collaboration: federated DUAs, evaluation harnesses, reproducibility tooling, and licensing patterns that keep raw data gated while making derivatives maximally reusable (MIT for code, CC0 for weights and data from open sources, OpenRAIL-M for weights from controlled-access cohorts where unrestricted release would re-encode individual-level signal, CC0 for synthetic-data substitutes, CC BY 4.0 for articles), building on Wellcome Leap MARFA / CORFA rather than re-implementing each time. (2) Organizational structures should intertwine nonprofit mission-alignment with for-profit scalability from Day 1, not retrofit scale at Year 5. Co-authored with Astera Open Science (Prachee Avasthi), Convergent Research (Adam Marblestone), and Speculative Tech (Ben Reinhardt). Cytognosis Foundation deliberately implements the framework's recommendations as a first-wave testbed, generating evidence that the path forward is operational rather than theoretical, so the next cohort of moonshot organizations does not have to derive the scaffolding from scratch.
