It convenes representatives from One Mind (transdiagnostic), the Center for Digital Mental Health (Harvard / MGH, 125-plus scientist-clinician network), the APA Council on Digital Health, Innovation, and Technology, and ASAM (SUD-specific privacy and adoption), so engagement is structurally aligned with the communities the work serves rather than relying on individual patient advocates. **Helix Framework rationale.** Openness as a stated principle is now standard in our field; openness as functioning practice that delivers patient impact is rarer. The Helix Framework community commentary, **released at M6 as a structural / open-science deliverable** (CC BY 4.0; bioRxiv + Zenodo, DOI-minted), takes the ~5-year mark since Focused Research Organizations were introduced as the moment to look back at what FROs, ARPA-like agencies, and hybrid for-profit / nonprofit models have actually delivered, and to propose a path forward that gives impact-first organizations targeting moonshots their best chance of thriving, scaling, and reaching patients. Two arguments. (1) The field needs shared boilerplate, not re-derived legal and operational scaffolding for every cross-institution collaboration: federated DUAs, evaluation harnesses, reproducibility tooling, and licensing patterns that keep raw data gated while making derivatives maximally reusable (MIT for code, CC0 for weights and data from open sources, OpenRAIL-M for weights from controlled-access cohorts where unrestricted release would re-encode individual-level signal, CC0 for synthetic-data substitutes, CC BY 4.0 for articles), building on Wellcome Leap MARFA / CORFA rather than re-implementing each time. (2) Organizational structures should intertwine nonprofit mission-alignment with for-profit scalability from Day 1, not retrofit scale at Year 5. Co-authored with Astera Open Science (Prachee Avasthi), Convergent Research (Adam Marblestone), and Speculative Tech (Ben Reinhardt). Cytognosis Foundation deliberately implements the framework's recommendations as a first-wave testbed, generating evidence that the path forward is operational rather than theoretical, so the next cohort of moonshot organizations does not have to derive the scaffolding from scratch. Releasing at M6 (rather than at residency end) maximizes the time the framework has to influence the next wave of FROs, ARPA-like agencies, and hybrid models forming in 2027-2028.
