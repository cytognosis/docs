Connectomic-to-Behavioral and clinical-scale validation live in the Google.org Impact Challenge proposal (36 months, $2.5M) and the planned ARPA-H Proactive Health Office submission.

### Post-Residency Vision

The residency is the second loop of a five-year buildout. By Month 18 the substrate, baseline foundation models, disease-aware fine-tunes at Micro and Meso, and the first multimodal Genome-to-Single-Cell joint model are all open public goods. Four lines continue immediately. First, an NSF Tech Labs five-year center grant, submitted either independently or as a Cytognosis-Convergent-Research joint center (Convergent has expressed interest in co-leading the cross-disorder benchmark and infrastructure tracks), carries the open infrastructure into a sustained, community-governed backbone. Second, an ARPA-H Mission Office ISO submission, targeting either the Proactive Health Office (PHO) for the prospective monitoring arm or Health Science Futures (HSF) for the cross-scale foundational science arm, carries the work into a 500 to 1,000-person clinical study generating proprietary continuous-monitoring evidence that feeds back into Neuroverse v1.0 (target $20-30M, currently in preparation). Third, a planned Convergent Research FRO submission scales the cross-scale, multi-modal extension toward Neuroverse v1.0, where the molecular, connectomic, and behavioral scales fuse into a single navigable health coordinate system. Fourth, the Helix Framework community commentary, which we are co-developing with Astera Open Science (Prachee Avasthi), Convergent Research (Adam Marblestone), and Speculative Tech (Ben Reinhardt), publishes during the residency year as the open organizational-infrastructure recipe for cross-institution mission-driven collaborations: federated DUAs, shared evaluation harnesses, common reproducibility tooling, and the licensing patterns (Apache 2.0 for code-and-weights, OpenRAIL-M for restricted-derivative weights, CC0 for data substitutes) that let multiple moonshot-scale orgs share a substrate without re-implementing the legal and operational scaffolding each time. Cytognosis Foundation remains the open-source steward; a future public benefit corporation subsidiary may commercialize specific clinical-decision-support tools, but only on top of the open core, never by closing it. The residency-year deliverables are the load-bearing evidence that lets each of these four downstream lines submit with primary data, not promises, and the Helix Framework commentary is a deliverable in its own right that benefits Astera's broader open-science mission directly.

## Q3. Risk Assessment

Five risks cover the most likely failure modes; each has been carried through internal review and has a concrete mitigation that does not require additional funding to activate.

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Cross-cohort generalization fails: learned axes (Phase 3) replicate within PsychENCODE 388 paired donors but do not recover the PGC 14-disorder five-factor structure when recomputed on NBB-aggregated samples.** This is the central scientific risk; the entire claim of dimensional psychiatry rests on it. | Run NBB-internal replication as a hard Go / No-Go gate, not a downstream sanity check. Compute the 14 by 14 cross-disorder covariance on NBB samples aggregated by any of the 14 PGC diagnoses and compare to the Grotzinger 2026 reference via Frobenius distance, off-diagonal correlation, and eigen-spectrum alignment. Pre-register the threshold and the analysis plan before running the comparison. If the threshold is missed, publish the negative result and its diagnostic decomposition (which factors recovered, which did not, what cohort effects were detected) as a preprint and a Zenodo-deposited reproducibility bundle. Reframe Phase 3 as a multi-target validation against PGC and HiTOP independently, rather than a single pass / fail. Activate the leave-one-cohort-out NBB-internal protocol across the six NBB sub-biobanks as a parallel evidence stream. |
| **Restricted-access cohorts (NBB, PsychENCODE WGS, restricted ENIGMA working groups) cannot be released openly, creating tension with Astera's open science policy.** Raw data must remain controlled-access; weights and derivatives may inadvertently re-encode individual-level information. | Use the Cytognosis controlled-access genotype release pattern: release Apache 2.0 weights and Apache 2.0 code, gate raw data behind the existing DUAs, and ship synthetic-data substitutes alongside every release so external researchers can reproduce the pipeline end-to-end without DUA.
