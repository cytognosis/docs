# Yar Features, in Plain Language

## The gist

Yar has **62 planned features** across six areas of support. **22 are already built and tested.** A few more are partly there. The rest are planned but not started. **8 features are on hold** until two safety pieces (a crisis-detection check and a privacy-boundary rulebook) are written and reviewed, neither of which exists yet.

Status tags below: **[shipped]** = working today. **[next]** = active build target. **[later]** = planned, further out.

---

## 1. Getting started, focus, and time

Help with starting tasks, keeping track of time, and capturing thoughts before they vanish.

- **Voice brain dump**, talk out everything on your mind and have it captured. [shipped]
- **Brain dump to action plan**, turn what you said into real next steps. [shipped]
- **Tasks from your words**, pull tasks out of what you said or wrote. [shipped]
- **Right-sized task breakdown**, choose how small tasks get chopped up. [shipped]
- **Focus companion and body doubling**, a quiet presence while you work. [shipped]
- **Flexible plan with a backup track**, a lighter version built in for harder days. [shipped]
- **Stray thought capture**, drop a random thought somewhere safe mid-task. [shipped]
- **Capture from anywhere**, save to Yar from phone, computer, or browser. [next]
- **Single-task focus mode**, one thing fills the screen at a time. [later]
- **Graceful activity pause**, Yar holds your place when you step away. [later]
- **Gentle break prompts**, a soft nudge to rest before you burn out. [later]
- **AI morning plan**, a suggested day built around how you're doing. [later]
- **Floating task reminder**, a small on-screen reminder of what you're on. [later]
- **Open data connections (MCP)**, connect Yar to tools you already use. [later, on hold for safety review]
- **All-in-one ND support app**, tasks, notes, mood, and AI in one place. [later]

## 2. Mood, energy, and emotional support

Tracking how you feel, getting support on hard days, and building habits honest about what you actually have to give. This is Yar's biggest area with almost no existing competitor.

- **Your energy and mood map**, a simple picture of how you've been. [shipped]
- **Mood tag on tasks**, note your mood on any task. [shipped]
- **Companion style and voice**, choose the tone Yar uses with you. [shipped]
- **Private space before planning**, write out how you feel first, seen by no one. [shipped]
- **Safety and consent layer**, you control what Yar can see or do. [shipped]
- **Rest day support**, a pause held without shame. [shipped, on hold for safety review]
- **Streaks that honor rest days**, consistency tracking that doesn't punish rest. [shipped]
- **Mood-anchored morning check-in**, a brief, low-pressure daily start. [shipped]
- **Voice mood awareness**, opt-in, off by default, never a diagnosis. [next]
- **Adaptive companion that builds trust**, Yar gets to know you gradually. [next]
- **On-device private AI**, processing that stays on your device. [later]
- **Companion that learns your style**, Yar adjusts to what actually helps. [later]
- **Energy check before saying yes**, a pause before committing to something new. [later]
- **No-penalty plan change**, change plans anytime, no guilt. [later]
- **Voice wellbeing signals**, research-stage, fully opt-in. [later, on hold for safety review]
- **Mood-matched companion**, Yar's tone shifts to match how you're feeling. [later]
- **Gentle reset after a hard day**, a short, kind debrief when things fall apart. [later, on hold for safety review]
- **Your week as a story**, a human narrative of your week, not just a checklist. [later]

## 5. Thinking, notes, and ideas

Organizing your thoughts and keeping your knowledge connected the way your brain actually works. This is where the branching thought-map, Yar's flagship feature, lives.

- **Thought placement assistant**, get a suggestion for where a new idea fits. [shipped]
- **Spatial thought map view**, ideas laid out visually with connections drawn. [shipped]
- **Your data in your own tools**, export to Obsidian, Logseq, or elsewhere, on your terms. [shipped]
- **Thought map reviewer**, a gentle review that catches loose threads. [shipped]
- **Private local knowledge store**, your notes live on your device, in your control. [shipped]
- **Names and terms you use**, Yar learns your people, places, and shorthand. [shipped]
- **Conversational thought map**, build your map by talking it through with Yar. [shipped]
- **Voice-grown thought map**, talk out loud and watch a map build itself. [next]
- **Structured note types**, flexible note types that match how your memory works. [next]
- **Saved smart searches**, searches that stay live and surface what's changed. [later]
- **Your personal vocabulary**, Yar learns your specific names and phrases. [later]
- **Map to document transform**, turn a messy map into a clean document. [later]
- **Thought to document templates**, apply a template and get a drafted document. [later]
- **Untangling parallel thoughts**, sort several running ideas into workable threads. [later]
- **Highlight and link on any page**, highlight any web page and link it to your notes. [later]
- **Open schema translation layer**, a technical layer that moves your data between apps cleanly. [later]

## 6. Noticing and understanding yourself

Tracking your own patterns over time and connecting your body and environment to how you feel and function.

- **Personalized gentle nudges**, nudges tuned to your own patterns. [shipped]
- **Open sensor connection layer**, a flexible way to connect data sources you choose. [next]
- **Your custom tracking axes**, define your own things to track. [next]
- **Wearable sensor connection**, bring in data from an Oura ring, Apple Watch, or similar. [later]
- **Layered wellbeing dashboard**, wellbeing data you can open up as much or as little as you want. [later]
- **Opt-in self-assessment tools**, validated, optional self-assessments, never diagnostic. [later, on hold for safety review]
- **Brain sensor connection layer**, a future research idea for brain-sensing devices. [later]

## Your calls

1. **Write the two safety pieces (crisis-detection check, privacy rulebook) after the YC deadline (7/27), not before.** None of the 8 held-back features are needed for the YC demo. Confirm this order, or tell me to move one up.
2. **Your decision to prioritize the thought-map and capture-everywhere features makes this build bigger than the minimum.** Three of those pieces are still in progress. Recommend showing the 22 shipped features as the YC demo and framing the thought-map as "in active build," rather than waiting for it to be finished. Confirm you're comfortable with that framing.
3. **The claim that "28 features have no competitor" doesn't match the underlying data**; the real number is closer to 41 features with no direct competitor, 20 of which are grouped into the 8 named signature capabilities. Recommend using "20 signature capabilities, plus 21 more with no direct competitor" going forward, and retiring the "28" figure in investor and grant materials.
4. **Two systems for naming Yar's tone of voice exist** (a shipped 4-tone version and an older 5-mode version from the legacy codebase). Recommend keeping the shipped version through YC and revisiting naming afterward, since a rename would touch tested code for no functional gain.
5. **Most feature-to-psychology-construct labels in the full document are best-guess, not sourced.** Only 5 of 62 are confirmed against the official naming rules. Recommend treating them as provisional until reviewed; no action needed before YC.
