Orientation

Two halves (hemispheres), each with the same regions. Four cortical lobes (the outside) plus deep structures (the inside).

Direction terms used throughout: dorsal = top, ventral = bottom, anterior (or rostral) = front, posterior (or caudal) = back, medial = toward the midline, lateral = away from midline. So "dorsolateral PFC" = top-and-side of the front lobe.

Lateral view (looking at the left side of the brain)

Dashed markers indicate regions that are deep to the cortical surface (insula is hidden in the lateral fissure; FFA is on the ventral temporal surface).

Medial view (brain cut in half, looking at the inside)

This is one hemisphere cut down the middle, looking at the inside surface. The deep structures (in purple) are normally buried under the cortex.

Cortical regions (the outside surface)

Frontal lobe

frontalDorsolateral PFC (DLPFC, Brodmann 9/46)

Top-and-side of the front lobe. Above your temple, slightly forward.

Working memory, executive function, cognitive control. The "thinking" PFC.

Canonical TMS target. SAINT 79% remission TRD. Cognitive-control biotype across all 6 disorders.

frontalDorsomedial PFC + dorsal ACC (dmPFC / dACC)

Top of the front lobe at the midline; dACC sits above the corpus callosum on the medial wall.

Monitoring conflict, errors, salience. The "what's important right now" detector.

Salience network hub. Enhanced ERN here = OCD endophenotype. Frontal midline theta tracks trait anxiety.

frontalVentromedial PFC (vmPFC)

Bottom-and-middle of the front lobe, behind and above your eyes.

Value computation, emotion regulation, fear extinction. The "good or bad" calculator.

Inhibits the amygdala (when it fails: threat reactivity goes up). Central in MDD, PTSD, anxiety.

frontalOrbitofrontal cortex (OFC), medial and lateral

Bottom of the front lobe, sitting on the orbits (eye sockets).

Value-based decisions. Medial OFC handles reward; lateral OFC handles punishment and behavioral inhibition.

Addiction reward-deficit biotype. OCD (medial OFC hyperactivity = CSTC marker).

frontal (deep, midline)Subgenual ACC (sgACC, Brodmann 25)

Below the genu (front curve) of the corpus callosum. Deep, midline. Hard to reach with surface optics.

Sadness, depressive rumination, emotional pain, autonomic regulation.

Mayberg DBS target. Anticorrelation with DLPFC predicts TMS response (the SAINT trick).

frontal (medial)Pregenual ACC (pgACC)

Just above sgACC, in front of the genu of the corpus callosum.

Emotion regulation, fear extinction, top-down control of the amygdala.

Threat-dysregulation biotype in anxiety; baseline pgACC reactivity predicts SSRI + CBT response.

frontalInferior frontal gyrus (IFG)

Bottom-front of the front lobe, on the lateral surface. Left IFG = Broca's area; right IFG = response inhibition.

Language (left), response inhibition (right), mirror neuron system.

Autism social-brain biotype (mirror neurons). Right IFG in addiction inhibitory control.

Cingulate cortex

cingulatePosterior cingulate cortex (PCC) + precuneus

Back of the cingulate gyrus, medial wall, behind the corpus callosum. Precuneus sits just above PCC.

Self-referential thinking, autobiographical memory, mind-wandering. "Where am I in my life right now."

Central node of the default mode network. Depression-rumination and PTSD.

Temporal lobe

temporal (deep, lateral fissure)Anterior insula

Tucked inside the lateral fissure, between frontal and temporal lobes. Hidden from surface view.

Subjective feeling of emotion, craving, salience switching. The "felt sense" hub.

Salience network with dACC. Anxiety panic biotype. Addiction craving (Naqvi insula lesion stops smoking).

temporalSuperior temporal gyrus + Heschl's gyrus + planum temporale

Top of the temporal lobe. Heschl's is the primary auditory cortex.

Hearing, auditory feature extraction, speech perception.

B-SNIP Biotype 2 sensory-hyperresponsive psychosis (reduced 40Hz ASSR here). Psychosis-specific anchor.

temporalSuperior temporal sulcus (STS)

The long groove running along the top of the temporal lobe.

Social perception, biological motion, gaze processing. "Watching other people" hub.

Autism social-brain biotype (STS hypoactivation is one of the most-replicated autism fMRI findings).

temporal (ventral)Fusiform face area (FFA)

On the bottom (ventral) surface of the temporal lobe, in the fusiform gyrus. Right hemisphere dominates.

Face recognition. Specialized for faces in most people.

Autism social-brain biotype (reduced FFA activation for faces).

parietal/temporalTemporoparietal junction (TPJ)

Where the temporal and parietal lobes meet, on the lateral surface. Right TPJ is the usual subject.

Attention reorienting, theory of mind, self-other distinction.

VAN-deficit biotype in PTSD (Etkin). Predicts psychotherapy non-response.

Deep structures

deep, medial temporalHippocampus

Curled inside the medial temporal lobe, like a seahorse. Subdivided into CA1, CA2, CA3, CA4, dentate.

Episodic memory formation, spatial maps, contextual fear conditioning.

Depression + PTSD: volume reductions. Schizophrenia: CA1 hyperactivity (Grace lab) drives downstream striatal dopamine.

deep, medial temporalAmygdala

In front of the hippocampus, deep in the medial temporal lobe. Almond-shaped.

Emotional learning, threat detection, salience for fear.

Threat-reactive biotype across anxiety, PTSD, anxious depression. Overmodulated in dissociative PTSD.

extended amygdalaBed nucleus of the stria terminalis (BNST)

Small structure above the amygdala, near the midline, by the anterior commissure.

Sustained threat response (vs amygdala's phasic response). When threat is uncertain or future.

BNST-driven sustained-threat biotype in GAD. Addiction stress-driven negative-affect.

deep centralStriatum (caudate, putamen, nucleus accumbens)

Deep, surrounds the thalamus. NAc is the ventral bottom-front; caudate is C-shaped along the lateral ventricle; putamen is lateral to caudate.

Reward (NAc), habit (putamen), goal-directed action (caudate). Big dopamine target.

Addiction (all biotypes). MDD-anhedonic (NAc hypofunction). OCD (caudate head hyperactivity). Schizophrenia Type A (hyperdopaminergic associative striatum).

center of brainThalamus (and mediodorsal nucleus)

Center of the brain, between the hemispheres.

Sensory relay (nearly all sensory information passes through), prefrontal coordination via MD nucleus.

B-SNIP psychosis (MD thalamus dysconnectivity, Anticevic). CSTC loops route through MD. Sleep-spindle deficits come from thalamic reticular nucleus.

midbrainVentral tegmental area (VTA)

Top of the brainstem (midbrain).

Produces dopamine. Projects to NAc (mesolimbic reward) and PFC (mesocortical cognition).

Anhedonic and reward-deficit biotypes. Grace's hippocampus-VTA loop in Howes Type A schizophrenia.

cerebellumCerebellum (especially right Crus I/II, lobules VI/VII)

Behind the brainstem. Crus I/II are in the lateral cerebellum, posterior lobe.

Motor coordination, but also cognitive and emotional functions (especially lateral cerebellum).

Autism (right Crus I/II added to headset target list). Psychosis cognitive dysmetria (Andreasen). Alcohol-related cerebellar atrophy.

Functional networks

Groups of regions that activate together. A region can belong to several networks.

Default Mode Network (DMN)

PCC, precuneus, medial PFC, vmPFC, angular gyrus

Activates at rest, mind-wandering, self-thinking. Deactivates on task. Depression rumination, PTSD, psychedelic desynchronization.

Salience Network (SN)

Anterior insula (bilateral) + dACC

Detects salient stimuli, switches between DMN and CEN. Gatekeeper between resting and task brain states. Anxiety panic, addiction craving.

Central Executive Network (CEN)

DLPFC + posterior parietal cortex

Working memory, cognitive control, goal-directed attention. Cognitive-control biotype across all 6 disorders. Canonical TMS target.

Threat / fear circuit

Amygdala (hub), vmPFC, pgACC, anterior insula, BNST, hippocampus

vmPFC + pgACC normally inhibit the amygdala. When they fail, threat reactivity stays high. Anxiety, PTSD, anxious-depression biotypes.

Reward circuit (mesolimbic + mesocortical)

VTA → NAc → vmPFC + OFC

VTA dopamine drives reward prediction. Addiction (Volkow), MDD-anhedonic (Pizzagalli), PTSD-dysphoric, SCZ negative symptoms.

CSTC loop (cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical)

OFC + dACC → caudate → MD thalamus → back to cortex

Action selection, habit, repetitive behavior. OCD biotype hyperactivity. FDA-approved OCD deep TMS targets this loop.

Ventral Attention Network (VAN)

Right TPJ + right ventral frontal cortex

Reorients attention to unexpected events. "Circuit breaker" for the dorsal attention network. VAN-deficit predicts PTSD psychotherapy non-response.

Social brain network

STS, FFA, mPFC, TPJ, IFG, amygdala

Social perception, theory of mind, faces, biological motion. Autism social-brain biotype hypoactivation across all members.
