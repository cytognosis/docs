# Biotypes: Anorexia Nervosa (AN)

Genomic factor loading (Nature 2025): **Compulsive / eating factor** (shared with OCD and Tourette syndrome). In Grotzinger et al., "Mapping the genetic landscape across 14 psychiatric disorders," Nature 2025 (doi:10.1038/s41586-025-09820-3, 1,056,201 cases), anorexia nervosa co-loads with obsessive-compulsive disorder and Tourette on the compulsive/eating factor, whose factor-specific pathways are more narrowly defined than the broad transcriptional-regulation signal shared across all 14 disorders. What sets anorexia apart from its compulsive-factor neighbors is a second axis of risk that is metabolic rather than psychiatric. The Watson et al. 2019 GWAS reframed anorexia as a partly metabolic disorder, showing that its genetic architecture correlates negatively with insulin resistance, fasting insulin, and fasting glucose to a degree that exceeds the BMI signal (doi:10.1038/s41588-019-0439-2). This document treats that metabo-psychiatric reframing as the organizing idea of the MICRO scale.

This document follows the shared template (`_TEMPLATE_AND_CONVENTIONS.md`). Confidence ratings: HIGH (multi-cohort/multi-site replication), MEDIUM (single-consortium or moderate effect), LOW (single-lab or failed external replication). Replication problems are flagged explicitly. Framing is person-first and weight-stigma-aware throughout; anorexia nervosa is a serious psychiatric illness with one of the highest mortality rates in psychiatry, not a lifestyle or a choice.

## MICRO scale (molecular / genetic / cellular / immune): GO-harmonized

The defining MICRO feature of anorexia nervosa is its **dual genetic architecture**: a psychiatric axis (shared with OCD, anxiety, MDD, and schizophrenia) layered on a **metabolic axis** that is partly independent of body-mass-index genetics. Watson 2019 showed negative genetic correlations between AN and insulin resistance (HOMA-IR), fasting insulin, fasting glucose, leptin, and high-BMI/obesity phenotypes, with several glycemic correlations LARGER in magnitude than the BMI correlation. This is the strongest single reason to suspect that metabolic dysregulation is a primary contributor and not merely a starvation consequence. The 2026 integrative GWAS extended this with a novel SOX5 locus and MTAG-resolved metabolic-neurological hub genes (LPL, BDNF) alongside synaptic genes (VAMP2), and single-cell analysis localized the genetic risk to limbic and striatal GABAergic neurons plus motor-related cortex. The classic neurotransmitter story (serotonin, then dopamine) remains real but is now read as one layer within this metabo-psychiatric frame.

Notes on the MICRO scale:
- The **metabo-psychiatric reframing is the headline**. Before Duncan 2017 and Watson 2019, AN was modeled as a primarily psychiatric/serotonergic disorder with metabolic abnormalities assumed to be downstream of starvation. The GWAS genetic-correlation pattern reverses part of that assumption: the negative genetic correlations with insulin/glucose traits are partly INDEPENDENT of BMI genetics and persist as heritable risk, which is hard to explain as pure starvation sequelae. The clinical implication is that some patients' difficulty maintaining weight may have a constitutional metabolic component, which reframes relapse as partly metabolic rather than purely motivational. This matters for weight-stigma-aware framing.
- The serotonergic and dopaminergic findings are trait-like (they persist after recovery and track harm avoidance), which makes them candidate vulnerability markers rather than state markers. This is also why SSRIs, which need available synaptic serotonin, generally fail in the acutely starved, tryptophan-depleted state.
- BDNF appears at TWO levels with DIFFERENT confidence: the 2026 GWAS hub signal (MEDIUM) is defensible, whereas the older Val66Met candidate-gene claim FAILED replication and should not be presented as established (LOW, flagged).
- The immune signal is real but mostly STATE-dependent: cytokine elevations track the starved state and IL-6 normalizes after refeeding, so inflammation in AN is better read as a starvation-coupled process than a stable diagnostic biotype.

## MESO scale (connectomic): Allen Atlas nodes + edges

Findings are mapped to the Allen Human Reference Atlas 3D (2020, 141 regions). Functional labels (DLPFC, OFC, sgACC, vmPFC) are given in parentheses against the containing Allen anatomical structure. The dominant AN circuit story has two intertwined themes: (1) **interoceptive and reward dysregulation** centered on the insula and ventral striatum, in which food cues recruit anxiety/cognitive-control circuitry rather than reward circuitry ("reward contamination"); and (2) **excessive fronto-striatal cognitive over-control**, in which dorsal prefrontal and dorsal-striatal systems impose rigid top-down restraint over eating. A third theme, **body-image / parietal disturbance**, localizes to precuneus and inferior parietal cortex.

EEG/MEG notes: AN electrophysiology is sparse and mostly LOW confidence. The more reproducible findings are reward/punishment event-related potentials (blunted reward-positivity / feedback-related negativity to food and monetary reward, consistent with the fMRI reward picture) and starvation-related slowing that normalizes with refeeding. There is no established diagnostic EEG/MEG signature for AN; small samples, profound metabolic confounds (the starved brain itself slows EEG), and medication effects keep confidence low.

Atlas note: insula, orbital gyri (OFC), anterior cingulate, middle frontal gyrus (DLPFC), ventral and dorsal striatum, amygdala, hypothalamus, precuneus, and inferior parietal cortex all exist within the Allen 141-region set, so no non-Allen additions were required. The DLPFC, OFC, and vmPFC functional labels map onto the Allen middle frontal gyrus and orbital gyri respectively.

## Interventional studies (incl. neuromodulation, DBS, neuroplastogens)

The honest headline for AN treatment is that **psychotherapy outperforms pharmacology**, and that the field has NO medication with a robust, replicated effect on the core disorder. Family-based treatment is first-line for adolescents; CBT-enhanced is the leading adult psychotherapy. Olanzapine produces modest weight gain but does not reliably move core psychopathology. The interesting frontier is neuromodulation (sgACC DBS for severe-enduring AN) and neuroplastogens (psilocybin), both early-stage. The metabolic component of AN implies that purely psychiatric treatment may be insufficient for the constitutionally metabolic subgroup, which is a research-stage hypothesis rather than a current treatment.

Intervention notes:
- The **pharmacology failure is genuine and should be stated plainly**: no drug reliably treats the core disorder of anorexia nervosa. Olanzapine's effect is on weight and tempo of gain, modest, and concentrated in adults. SSRIs fail in the acute state. This is one of the starkest pharmacology gaps in psychiatry and is part of why neuromodulation and plastogens attract interest.
- Weight restoration is a prerequisite, not an optional add-on. Several molecular and circuit abnormalities (gray-matter pseudo-atrophy, IL-6 elevation, leptin suppression, EEG slowing) are starvation-coupled and reverse with refeeding, so the biotype signal must always be read against nutritional state.
- The plastogen and DBS work is early and uncontrolled; the defensible claim is safety/feasibility, not efficacy. For the project's TrkB/plasticity strategy, AN is a cautious target: low body weight, electrolyte fragility, and cardiac risk demand medical-stabilization gating before any plastogen exposure.

## Most defensible biotypes (cross-scale synthesis)

1. **Metabo-psychiatric / glycemic biotype** (the anchor and the reframing). MICRO: heritable negative genetic correlations with insulin resistance, fasting insulin, fasting glucose, and leptin that partly exceed and are independent of the BMI signal (Watson 2019), extended by LPL/BDNF metabolic-neurological hubs and lipid-metabolism gene modules (Integrative GWAS 2026). MESO: links to hypothalamic homeostatic signaling and insula-hypothalamus interoceptive coupling. MACRO: difficulty maintaining restored weight, compulsive hyperactivity tied to hypoleptinemia. This is the biotype that distinguishes AN from its compulsive-factor neighbors and reframes relapse as partly constitutional/metabolic. Confidence MEDIUM-HIGH.

2. **Interoceptive / reward-contamination biotype**. MESO: anterior insula and ventral-striatum/OFC alterations in which food cues recruit anxiety and aberrant valuation rather than reward (reward-contamination meta-analysis 2025). MICRO: trait serotonergic (high 5-HT1A, low 5-HT2A) and dopaminergic (elevated striatal D2/D3) signatures that persist after recovery and track harm avoidance. MACRO: anxious response to food, distorted satiety/hunger. Anchor Allen regions: insula, ventral striatum/NAc, orbital gyri (OFC). Confidence MEDIUM.

3. **Fronto-striatal over-control biotype**. MESO: excessive DLPFC (middle frontal gyrus) to dorsal-striatum (caudate) coupling enforcing rigid dietary restraint; heightened dorsal-ACC conflict engagement. MICRO: limbic/striatal GABAergic-neuron genetic enrichment (Integrative GWAS 2026). MACRO: restricting-type AN, perfectionism, cognitive rigidity, over-control trait. Anchor Allen regions: middle frontal gyrus (DLPFC), caudate (dorsal striatum), anterior cingulate. Confidence MEDIUM.

4. **Body-image / parietal biotype**. MESO: precuneus and right inferior parietal cortex alterations in body representation and self-referential processing. MACRO: body-image disturbance, undue influence of weight/shape on self-evaluation. Anchor Allen regions: precuneus, inferior parietal cortex. Caveat: the rIPL rTMS pilot was NULL, so the parietal target is mechanistically plausible but not yet therapeutically validated. Confidence LOW-MEDIUM.

5. **Gut-microbiome / microbiota-gut-brain biotype**. MICRO: reproducible dysbiosis (depleted anaerobes), murine transplant evidence that AN-donor microbiota impairs weight gain, and microbiome-leptin coupling over the clinical course. MACRO: poor weight restoration and altered appetite signaling. This is the strongest emerging non-neural axis and a candidate for the metabolic story's mechanism. Confidence MEDIUM (consistent human dysbiosis, causal-direction murine support).

State-dependence is the cross-cutting caveat unique-in-degree to AN: gray-matter volume, IL-6, leptin, EEG, and serum BDNF are all profoundly perturbed by the starved state and largely normalize with refeeding. Any AN biotype must be separated into trait-like vulnerability signatures (serotonergic/dopaminergic PET patterns, the metabo-psychiatric genetic architecture, perfectionism) versus state-like starvation sequelae (pseudo-atrophy, cytokines, hypoleptinemia). Conflating the two has historically misled the field.

**Genomic factor statement:** Anorexia nervosa loads on the **compulsive/eating factor** (shared with OCD and Tourette) in Grotzinger et al. Nature 2025, but is distinguished from its factor-mates by a second, partly BMI-independent metabolic axis of genetic risk (negative correlations with insulin/glucose traits), the metabo-psychiatric architecture that now organizes the molecular understanding of the disorder.
