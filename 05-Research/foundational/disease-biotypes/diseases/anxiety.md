Genomic factor loading (Nature 2025): **Internalizing factor** (major depression + PTSD + anxiety; shared signal enriched in oligodendrocyte biology). Grotzinger et al. 2025 (doi:10.1038/s41586-025-09820-3).

Scope: generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder (PD), and social anxiety disorder (SAD). Obsessive-compulsive disorder loads on the separate compulsive/eating factor and is treated in its own document; the cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit is therefore excluded here. PTSD-adjacent contextual-fear circuitry is mentioned only where it differentiates anxiety from PTSD, and has its own document.

Standing caveat: anxiety biotypes are research targets, not validated clinical diagnostics. Most candidates carry moderate effect sizes and incomplete out-of-sample replication. The defensible claim is that a small number of molecular axes (serotonergic, GABAergic, glutamatergic, noradrenergic, HPA, acid/CO2 sensing) and circuit-level dimensions (amygdala-prefrontal regulation, salience-interoceptive hyperconnectivity, BNST sustained threat) recur across independent samples and modalities.

## MICRO scale (molecular / genetic / cellular / immune) vs GO-harmonized

Anxiety molecular biology distributes across six neurotransmitter and neuroendocrine axes plus a smaller immune signal. The unifying GWAS observation is that anxiety loads on the internalizing factor, whose shared genetic signal is enriched in oligodendrocyte biology (Grotzinger 2025), with substantial genetic correlation to neuroticism, MDD, and PTSD. Anxiety-specific loci from the largest dedicated GWAS (Levey 2020, MVP, ~200,000 participants, GAD-2 phenotype) include SATB1 (chr3) and ESR1 (estrogen receptor alpha, chr6); symptom-level modeling (Thorp 2021) confirms shared anxiety-depression architecture.

## MESO scale (connectomic) vs Allen Atlas nodes + edges

Anchored to the Allen Human Reference Atlas 3D (2020, 141 regions). Functional labels are given in parentheses against the containing Allen anatomical structure. The basolateral and central amygdala and the BNST are sub-millimeter to millimeter-scale and are listed under the Allen "amygdala" and "bed nucleus of stria terminalis / sublenticular extended amygdala" structures with the functional subregion noted. EEG/MEG findings (enhanced ERN, frontal midline theta, elevated resting beta) localize to dorsal ACC / midcingulate and frontocentral cortex.

## Most defensible biotypes (cross-scale synthesis)

**Biotype A vs Threat-dysregulation / amygdala-prefrontal disconnection (GAD-dominant).** MICRO: serotonergic and BDNF/TrkB extinction machinery. MESO anchors: amygdala (BLA), vmPFC (Allen frontal orbital/medial), pregenual ACC. Key edges: amygdala-vmPFC and amygdala-pgACC weakened anticorrelation (impaired top-down inhibition). The most replicated circuit finding; baseline ACC reactivity predicts SSRI/CBT response. Confidence HIGH.

**Biotype B vs Salience-hyperconnectivity / interoceptive vigilance (panic-dominant, somatic GAD).** MICRO: noradrenergic hyperarousal plus CO2/lactate/ASIC1a acid sensing (respiratory panic subtype, ~93% CO2-induction). MESO anchors: anterior insula, dorsal ACC. Key edge: anterior insula-dACC salience hyperconnectivity (positive). Adds wearable HRV and capnography traction. Confidence HIGH.

**Biotype C vs BNST sustained-threat / worry-prone (GAD-pure).** MICRO: GABAergic deficit and glutamatergic excess. MESO anchors: BNST (extended amygdala), dorsal ACC, anterior insula, right DLPFC. Key edge: BNST-CeA positive coupling during sustained/unpredictable threat. Distinguished from Biotype A by tonic versus phasic profile; rTMS and (rarely) BNST DBS candidates. Confidence MEDIUM (rodent-strong, small human cohorts).

**Biotype D vs Social-evaluative hyper-HPA (SAD).** MICRO: hyper-reactive HPA/cortisol to social stress. MESO anchors: dorsal ACC and medial PFC during social anticipation; insula/fronto-limbic structural shifts. Distinguished from Biotype A by trigger specificity (social-evaluative vs broad). Wearable cortisol as adjunct. Confidence MEDIUM.

**Biotype E vs Hypervigilant error-monitoring (transdiagnostic, GAD-onset predictive).** MICRO: glutamatergic/GABAergic cortical balance. MESO anchor: dorsal ACC / midcingulate. EEG signature: enhanced ERN (also seen in OCD) and elevated frontal midline theta; enhanced ERN prospectively predicts first-onset GAD in adolescent females. Confidence MEDIUM.

Not betting on: a clinical inflammatory biotype (signal real but no treatment-relevant cutpoint), a cellular GABA-interneuron biotype (postmortem/animal only), or a single-locus genetic biotype (effects too small). Genomic factor: anxiety loads on the **Internalizing factor** (with MDD and PTSD; oligodendrocyte-enriched shared signal), per Grotzinger 2025.
