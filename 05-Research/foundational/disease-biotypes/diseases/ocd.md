# Biotypes: Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Genomic factor loading (Nature 2025): **Compulsive / eating factor** (OCD + anorexia nervosa + Tourette syndrome). Grotzinger et al. 2025 (doi:10.1038/s41586-025-09820-3). This factor is distinct from the internalizing factor on which anxiety disorders load, which is why the cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit is treated here rather than in the anxiety document.

Scope: DSM-5/ICD-11 obsessive-compulsive disorder, organized by the empirically replicated symptom dimensions (contamination/washing, harm/checking, symmetry/ordering, taboo/forbidden thoughts, and hoarding, which DSM-5 split into a separate hoarding disorder). The hereditary continuity with Tourette syndrome and trichotillomania/excoriation (body-focused repetitive behaviors) is noted where it shapes the molecular and circuit signal.

Standing caveat: OCD is the cleanest psychiatric case of a single named circuit dominating biotype thinking, but the strength of the CSTC model rests mainly on symptom-provocation imaging, lesion and surgical outcomes, and pharmacology, not on resting-state connectivity. The largest resting-state mega-analysis (ENIGMA-OCD, Bruin 2023) reported predominantly global hypo-connectivity and did not reproduce the textbook orbitofrontal-caudate hyperconnectivity, so the canonical "OFC-caudate hyperactive loop" should be read as a task-activation and metabolic finding, not a resting-connectivity one. This tension is flagged explicitly in the edges table. Symptom-dimension heterogeneity further weakens any single-biotype claim; the contamination/washing versus checking split is the most reproducible.

## MICRO scale (molecular / genetic / cellular / immune) vs GO-harmonized

OCD molecular biology centers on the glutamate system, with serotonergic and dopaminergic axes as the second tier and a contested but important autoimmune/inflammatory subtype (PANDAS/PANS). The genomic anchor changed in 2025: Strom et al. delivered the first robustly genome-wide-significant OCD GWAS, a meta-analysis of 53,660 cases and 2,044,417 controls that identified 30 independent loci, prioritized 25 likely causal genes (including WDR6, DALRD3, CTNND1, and several major histocompatibility complex genes), and showed enrichment in excitatory neurons of hippocampus and cortex and in D1- and D2-receptor medium spiny neurons of the striatum (doi:10.1038/s41588-025-02189-z). Roughly 11,500 variants captured 90% of OCD SNP-heritability, and risk was shared with all psychiatric disorders tested, most strongly anorexia and Tourette, consistent with the compulsive/eating factor (Grotzinger 2025).

## MESO scale (connectomic) vs Allen Atlas nodes + edges

Anchored to the Allen Human Reference Atlas 3D (2020, 141 regions). Functional labels appear in parentheses against the containing Allen anatomical structure. The defining OCD substrate is the orbitofrontal CSTC loop: orbital frontal cortex projects to the head of the caudate and ventral striatum, through globus pallidus interna to the mediodorsal thalamus, and back to OFC. Medial versus lateral OFC and the dorsal ACC carry partly separable symptom-dimension signal. EEG localizes the enhanced error-related negativity (ERN), the strongest OCD endophenotype, to dorsal ACC / midcingulate cortex.

Critical replication note: the hyperactive CSTC loop is best supported by symptom-provocation task fMRI and by FDG-PET/cerebral-blood-flow studies (elevated OFC and caudate metabolism that normalizes with SRI or CBT response). The ENIGMA-OCD resting-state mega-analysis (Bruin 2023, n>1000 patients) instead found mostly global hypo-connectivity with only a few hyper-connections, mainly thalamus-sensorimotor, and did not reproduce resting OFC-caudate hyperconnectivity. Nodes/edges below mark which modality supports each claim.

## Interventional studies (incl. neuromodulation, DBS, neuroplastogens)

Regulatory accuracy notes: BrainsWay Deep TMS (H7 coil, dmPFC/ACC target) received FDA clearance for OCD in August 2018 (de novo). DBS for OCD holds a 2009 FDA Humanitarian Device Exemption for the anterior limb of the internal capsule (Reclaim, Medtronic). Contrary to a common claim, MR-guided focused ultrasound (MRgFUS) capsulotomy for OCD is investigational (phase 1/2 trials) and is NOT FDA-cleared for OCD as of May 2026; it is corrected here.

## Most defensible biotypes (cross-scale synthesis)

**Biotype A vs Glutamatergic cortico-striatal hyperdrive (OCD-core).** MICRO: elevated CSF and caudate/striatal glutamate (Glx) on MRS that normalizes with treatment; SLC1A1/EAAT3 candidate gene; Strom 2025 enrichment in excitatory neurons and medium spiny neurons. MESO anchors: medial and lateral OFC, head of caudate, mediodorsal thalamus, ventral striatum. Key edge: OFC-caudate positive (hyperactive/hypermetabolic) on provocation and FDG-PET, with the disinhibition loop closing through pallidum and thalamus back to OFC. The single most central and treatment-relevant OCD axis; glutamate modulators (ketamine, riluzole, NAC) target it. Confidence HIGH for the molecular/metabolic claim, with the explicit caveat that resting connectivity (ENIGMA-OCD) shows hypo- not hyper-connectivity.

**Biotype B vs Enhanced error-monitoring / dorsal-ACC hyperactivity (trait endophenotype).** MICRO: glutamatergic/GABAergic cortical E/I balance. MESO anchor: dorsal ACC / midcingulate. EEG signature: enhanced ERN (g approx 0.5), present in unaffected first-degree relatives, the most robust OCD endophenotype and also seen in GAD. This is the deep-TMS (H7 coil) target. Confidence HIGH for the endophenotype; the most measurable biotype for a wearable/EEG headset.

**Biotype C vs Symptom-dimension circuit subtypes (washing vs checking vs symmetry).** MICRO: shared glutamatergic substrate. MESO: contamination/washing maps to ventromedial OFC-ventral striatum; harm/checking to lateral OFC/dorsal ACC-caudate; symmetry/ordering to sensorimotor CSTC (putamen, thalamus-sensorimotor). Key edges: dissociable frontal-striatal couplings differentiate checkers from washers. The washing-vs-checking split is the most reproducible heterogeneity axis. Confidence MEDIUM.

**Biotype D vs Dopaminergic tic-related subtype (Tourette overlap).** MICRO: striatal dopamine, D1/D2 medium-spiny-neuron enrichment; antipsychotic-augmentation responsiveness. MESO anchors: putamen, ventral striatum, sensorimotor CSTC. Distinguished by comorbid tics, male predominance, earlier onset, and SRI-refractoriness that responds to antipsychotic augmentation. Confidence MEDIUM, strongest within the compulsive-factor continuum with Tourette.

**Biotype E vs Autoimmune/inflammatory pediatric-onset subtype (PANDAS/PANS).** MICRO: anti-neuronal/anti-basal-ganglia antibodies, complement and microglial activation in a contested subgroup. MESO anchor: basal ganglia (caudate/putamen) neuroinflammation. CONTROVERSIAL and flagged as such: the inflammatory signal and abrupt-onset clinical picture are real in some children, but the causal autoimmune model is disputed, antibody assays do not reliably predict immunotherapy response, and case definitions remain unsettled. Confidence LOW; included because it is the only mechanistically distinct, potentially immunomodulator-treatable OCD subtype.

Not betting on: a clinical oxidative-stress biotype (peripheral markers, nonspecific) or a single-locus serotonergic genetic biotype (SRI pharmacology is specific, but the genetics are not). Genomic factor: OCD loads on the **Compulsive / eating factor** (with anorexia nervosa and Tourette syndrome), per Grotzinger 2025.
